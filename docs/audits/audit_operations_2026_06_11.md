# Operations Audit — agentplain.com — 2026-06-11
**Date:** 2026-06-11 · **Lens:** VP Operations / SRE — "would a local-business owner trust this to keep running, and do they know what happens when it breaks?" · **Method:** read-only, live fetch + `git -C C:/agentplain show origin/main:<path>`

## 1. Executive summary
The operational *machinery* is far stronger than the operational *story*. On `origin/main` today there is real SRE substance a competitor at this stage rarely has: a cheap dependency-checking `/api/health` (live, returning `status:"ok"` on both apex and app — DB 4–40ms, Inngest mounted, `version d77ffcef3dad`), Sentry runtime-error capture behind a swappable `lib/observability` adapter, structured logging, Inngest cron monitors, a written incident log with real post-mortems, and — correcting Wave 2 — the **Pillar-6 fleet-health heartbeat (PR #220) and the L1 support escalation classifier (PR #221) ARE merged to main** (`fleetHealthCheckFn` is registered in the Inngest serve array at `app/api/inngest/route.ts:165`; daily `0 6 * * *` ET cron; escalate-first deterministic classifier that fires even when the LLM is dead). The degraded-chat experience — the live test case — is genuinely excellent: calm, in-voice, lead-capturing copy with a no-jargon snapshot test. **But the customer sees almost none of this.** There is no status page, the Terms carry no SLA, the in-app activity feed shows successes only (a skill that silently never fires leaves no customer-visible trace), the heartbeat pages *Conner*, never the affected customer, and escalation collapses to one email address (`hello@agentplain.com`) routed to a single human's inbox with no published response time and no named backup. The owner with a $10K/mo problem can't tell, from any surface they can reach, whether the thing is running or who answers at 2am when it isn't. **Lens score: 3/5** — strong internal reliability engineering, weak external reliability *trust*.

## 3. Per-surface findings

**`/api/health` (apex + app) — STRONG, score 4.5.** Live, both return `{"status":"ok",...db ok, inngest mounted}`. Well-designed: 503+reason on degrade so a monitor and a human triage in one read, `no-store`, no secret leakage, dynamic (can't be CDN-masked). **Gap:** the route exists but the doc admits no external monitor is wired yet ("pick one when you sign up" — Better Stack/UptimeRobot). A health endpoint nobody probes is a smoke detector with no battery.

**`/security` — incident response is REAL, score 4.** Commits to: contain within 24h of confirmed detection, notify affected workspace owners by email within 72h, publish a post-mortem with root cause/timeline/remediation; daily Postgres backups with PITR, 30-day rolloff, encrypted; 1-business-day response to *vulnerability* reports. This is more than most seed-stage SaaS publishes. **Gap:** it's all security-incident framing — nothing about *availability* incidents (the fleet not running ≠ a breach), and no status page to host the promised post-mortems.

**In-app activity feed — score 2.** Success-only. The single biggest operations trust gap because it makes silent failure indistinguishable from a quiet day.

**Degraded chat (the live test case) — score 4.5.** I could not reproduce the Wave-2 "degraded" today (live `/api/health` is green on both hosts; `POST /api/chat` 405s to GET as expected). But the *handling* is exemplary: `lib/plaino/degraded-copy.ts` ships three calm, in-character, lowercase, no-exclamation replies — paused ("Plaino's resting just now — but a person will follow up the same day"), transient ("catching his breath — give it a moment"), network ("didn't quite catch that") — every one pivots to email lead-capture, and `tests/plaino-degraded-copy.test.ts` *forbids* the strings "line/endpoint/API/stack/500/request/fetch/server." A model outage becomes a calm hand-off, not an HTTP error. This is what good degraded UX looks like.

**Fleet-health heartbeat (PR #220) — ON MAIN, score 4 internally / 2 for customer.** `lib/ops/fleet-health.ts` (pure model) + `lib/inngest/functions/fleet-health-check.ts` (`TZ=America/New_York 0 6 * * *`, registered at `app/api/inngest/route.ts:165`, snapshots to AuditLog, operator panel at `/operator/fleet-health`). Watches 7 metrics incl. `unsupported_vertical_signups`, `integration_breakage`, `support_backlog_age`, `llm_spend_vs_cap`. Excellent internal SRE. Invisible to customers.

**L1 support escalation (PR #221) — ON MAIN, score 3.5.** `lib/skills/customer-support-triage/escalation.ts` is a deterministic escalate-first classifier (distress > billing dispute > explicit human ask) that fires *before* any auto-answer and *even when the LLM is dead*. Conservative-by-design (tunes toward false positives). Genuinely good. **Gap:** it escalates *to* the same single operator inbox; the owner-facing promise ("a human will answer") has no published SLA behind it.

**Terms / About — score 2.** Covered in issues #3/#5. /about leans entirely on "every tier comes with a service team" as the reliability story — humans-as-uptime — which is fine positioning but undercut by no published response time anywhere a buyer looks.

## 4. Strategic gaps (vs what a competitor would do)
- **A status page is table-stakes** for "trust us to run your back office." A $20/mo Better Stack / Instatus page at `status.agentplain.com` that (a) pings the *already-built* `/api/health` and (b) hosts the post-mortems /security already promises would close three issues at once. Competitors selling to regulated SMBs (anyone with a SOC 2 story) all have one.
- **A "your fleet this week" digest** — proactive, customer-facing, "12 drafts queued, 1 skill skipped because QuickBooks is disconnected (reconnect →)." The internal heartbeat already computes per-workspace breakage; the customer never sees it. The competitor move is to point the *same* signal at the customer, turning silent failure into a reconnect CTA (and a retention touch).
- **A published support SLA on the pricing/contact surface** ("we respond within 1 business day; critical fleet outages within 4 hours"). Right now the only SLA-shaped number (1 business day) is buried in /security and scoped to security bugs.
- **On-call beyond one inbox.** `pageHuman`'s own doc flags it: recipient is Conner's inbox, "ideally NOT Conner's personal inbox," no backup. A bus-factor of one is an operations risk the customer is implicitly inheriting.

## 5. Quick wins (≤1h)
1. **Wire an external monitor to `/api/health`.** The endpoint is built and live; signing up for UptimeRobot/Better Stack and pointing it at `https://agentplain.com/api/health` is ~15 min and lights up the smoke detector. (No code change.)
2. **Stand up a hosted status page** (Instatus/Better Stack) at `status.agentplain.com`, fed by the health monitor; add a footer "Status" link. Closes the "does a status page exist" gap (today: ECONNREFUSED) without building anything.
3. **Add a `failed`/`skipped` row state to the in-app activity feed** so a skill that errors or no-ops is *visible* to the owner, not silent. The data already exists (`SkillRun` outcomes + the first-fire poller's 4-bucket collapse logic) — it just isn't rendered in steady state.
4. **Publish a support response time** on /pricing or a /contact surface ("1 business day; critical issues 4 hours"). One copy line; the security page already proves the commitment exists internally.
5. **Set `OPERATOR_EMAIL_ALLOWLIST` to a shared/aliased inbox + add a designated backup** so pages don't single-thread on one personal inbox (the page-human doc explicitly asks for this).

## 6. Deep work (>1d, high impact)
1. **Proactive customer-facing failure notification.** When a skill fails / never fires / an integration breaks, tell *the affected customer* (in-app banner + optional email), not just the operator. This is the single highest-trust operations investment: it converts the product's biggest silent-failure exposure (Wave 2's "skills that never fire") into an observable, recoverable event with a reconnect CTA. The heartbeat already computes the signal per-workspace; this is the customer-facing arm of it.
2. **Close the integration-break loop push-side.** Promote the manual "Test Connection" route into a scheduled per-credential check that flips a customer-visible "needs attention — reconnect" state and (optionally) emails the owner. Stops dependent workflows from silently dying.
3. **Drill the 24h guarantee + the un-drilled key-rotation/restore** (the production-growth plan already flags audit-log immutability + un-drilled key-rotation/restore as hardening gaps). A guarantee that's never been exercised end-to-end is a claim, not a capability.

## 7. What I'd cut
- **Nothing built should be cut** — the observability/heartbeat/escalation stack is the asset, not the liability. The thing to cut is the *gap between internal and external*: stop treating the heartbeat/incident-response machinery as operator-only plumbing and surface it (status page, customer notifications, published SLA). The cheapest high-trust move in this whole audit is wiring the monitor + status page to an endpoint that already returns `status:"ok"` today.
