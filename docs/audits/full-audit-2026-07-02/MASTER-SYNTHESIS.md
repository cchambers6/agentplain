## 1. Executive read — the five cross-audit patterns

### Pattern 1 · The truth seam is the #1 defect class: copy promises what the runtime doesn't deliver
This showed up in **12 of 19 audits** and produces most of the P0s. flatsbo sells a broker partnership, MLS filing, 48-hour cash offers, "real humans" chat, virtual staging, showing schedulers, and ~15 other features that have zero code behind them — asserted on pages, in the global footer, in FAQPage JSON-LD served to Google, and **generatively, in the chat AI's system prompt** (`flatsbo/01,02,09,10`). agentplain has the same disease in a milder strain: BYO storage marketed present-tense with no write path, discipline-heads routing that routes nothing, a "live" savings counter that never refreshes, "delete everything we hold" that retains the User row, a decorative Test-connection button, portal uploads that say "received" while discarding the bytes (`agentplain/04,05,06,09,10`). The fix discipline is the same on both: **the runtime is the spec; copy earns its tense.** Every "included/available/live/we do X" line either points at working code or gets cut.

### Pattern 2 · The trust boundary is open: PII and money endpoints have no auth, and neither product rate-limits anything
flatsbo's mutation layer is guarded but its **read layer and three write paths are wide open**: two unauthenticated JSON endpoints dump every seller's and buyer's name/email/phone plus offer terms keyed on public listing IDs; the buyer track page is world-readable; anyone can upload/delete photos on any listing, forge offers as any email (overwriting that user's name/phone via upsert), and mint Stripe checkout sessions against any listing (`flatsbo/03,05,06,07,08`). agentplain has no PII leak — but has **zero inbound rate limiting** on any auth endpoint, and its end-client portal accepts unlimited LLM-triggering messages and 25MB uploads from third parties, plus a cross-tenant `caseId` injection on upload (`agentplain/02,06`). flatsbo also has zero rate limiting anywhere, including two public endpoints that each trigger a paid LLM call (`flatsbo/07,09,10`). Nine audits hit some form of this.

### Pattern 3 · The front door is locked: each product's flagship funnel is dead at step one
The most expensive finding class isn't a bug — it's that the money path can't be walked. flatsbo: a blank **optional** "Year built" field hard-blocks every listing at wizard Step 1 (the likely reason prod has 0 organic listings); the `/homes` search input and every filter chip have no handler at all; the buyer counter-response loop 403s on two of its three actions; Stripe cancel lands on a 404 and success lands the anonymous seller on a login wall (`flatsbo/02,04,06,08,10`). agentplain: the customer portal **cannot be activated from the product** (setup/invite are raw API calls with zero UI callers — reachable-customer count: 0), TaxDome and Karbon are advertised `available` but unconnectable through any UI (the CPA vertical's killer-workflow connectors), and the primary-nav "How it works" link has 308-redirected to a home anchor since the page shipped (`agentplain/01,05,06`). Six-plus audits, both products, same shape: **build depth behind doors nobody can open.**

### Pattern 4 · The safety nets exist but aren't wired: gates and tests have systematic blind spots
Both repos invested in enforcement (voice-gate, brand-gate, RLS invariant tests, smoke suites, sole-writer invariants) and then let the surface area outgrow the nets. agentplain: the portal test suite fails on main and **no CI workflow runs it**; the RLS/data-category invariant tests that would have caught the portal-tables P0 the day it shipped are not in any workflow; voice/brand gates skip `app/portal`, `lib/reports` (the flagship Friday email — which has already drifted off the Heritage palette exactly as the gap predicted), `lib/integrations`, and `app/(operator)`; the wave-2 smoke test pins DocuSign as UNGATED when production is gated — the fleet's own coverage test asserts the opposite of truth (`agentplain/04,05,06,07,10`). flatsbo: the `paidViaTestMode`-in-prod alert its own CLAUDE.md mandates does not exist, and the **wrong disclosure statute is codified in STATE_CONFIG and pinned by a unit test** — the test suite actively defends a false legal claim (`flatsbo/08,10`). The enforcement layer must be manifest-driven, not hand-listed.

### Pattern 5 · Built but not landed, landed but not wired: the last mile is where work goes to die
flatsbo main **has not moved since 2026-06-16**. The cancel_url fix, the buyer-loop repair (PR #45), the webhook idempotency guard, and the `paidViaTestMode` refusal all exist — on unmerged branches — while the audit brief asserted them "landed" (the ghost-merge pattern; `flatsbo/01,06,08`). agentplain merges, but leaves seams **wired to exactly one call site**: `recordSavedTime` has one caller so 4 of 7 calibrated guarantee actions save zero minutes and the product offers refunds it doesn't owe; approval push-notify fires on one of ~8 creation paths; the pass-through-read breadcrumb counts email only; `MemoryAuditLog` is never written; the tiering sweep has zero callers; the ratified two-bucket positioning single-sources **never merged to main** (`agentplain/04,05,09,10`). And this sweep itself: 4 of 20 audits weren't on a remote when synthesis began. The fleet's constraint is not finding or building — it is **landing and wiring**. Definition of done must be: merged to main + a caller at every seam + a read-back verifying it.

## 4. What the audits agree we should STOP doing

1. **Stop writing feature copy before the feature exists.** This one habit generated the plurality of all 50+ P0s across both products. New rule at PR review: any present-tense capability claim cites the file that implements it.
2. **Stop shipping surfaces with no activation path.** The portal (API-only setup, 0 reachable customers), BYO storage (no form), saved searches (dead model), instant-offer (write-only table), the 50-detector insight library ambition — either the door ships with the room, or the room doesn't ship. Finish or delete; don't accumulate.
3. **Stop hand-maintaining gate scan lists.** The six-file email list, the per-directory voice-gate roots, the wave-comment smoke assertions — every one drifted. Manifests derived from the tree, or gates that scan everything and allowlist exceptions.
4. **Stop recording fixes as landed without a read-back against `origin/main`.** flatsbo's three "landed" heals were all absent (`flatsbo/08` M1); 4 of this sweep's own audits weren't pushed. Done = merged + verified on main, nothing else.
5. **Stop running two overlapping weekly emails** (`agentplain/07`). One weekly proof-of-value email, one opt-out.
6. **Stop carrying parallel dead stacks in-tree.** flatsbo's orphaned old-brand `SearchPage`/`MapView`/`ListingCard` are a regression trap (an agent already nearly edited the orphan believing it live). Port, then delete.
7. **Stop the flatsbo brand-v3 cascade until v2/v3 is ratified.** Live code is v3 "Concierge"; CLAUDE.md and memory mandate v2 and mark v3 "do not cascade" (`flatsbo/10`). Every styling fix this wave inherits the ambiguity — ratify first, one line from Conner.
8. **Stop auditing faster than we land.** June-16 findings were re-confirmed verbatim in July because main never moved. The next fleet-week is fixes-only; no new audit loops until the top-20 table above is burned down.

## 5. What each product specifically needs next

**agentplain.** The architecture is genuinely strong — authz re-derivation everywhere, the approval-gate factory seam, honest deterministic reporting — and the failures are concentrated at the truth seam and the last wiring mile. Move one: the **portal package** (rows 9–10) — RLS + teardown + CI, then the five trust-chain P0s — or, if no customer is near it, flag the portal off entirely; either is honest, the current state is neither. Move two: the **guarantee counting fix** (row 11) — it is the only finding that recurringly refunds money the fleet earned. Move three: the **connector activation + inert-controls pass** (rows 12–13) — TaxDome/Karbon connectable, disclosure un-bypassed, BYO/discipline-heads/billing copy telling the truth — because the CPA vertical is unsellable until it lands. Everything else (notifications, gates manifest, session revocation) rides in the hardening week behind those three.

**flatsbo.** The product is pre-revenue with a frozen main, an open PII surface, an advertising layer that is materially false, and a funnel dead at step one — in that order of legal exposure but the reverse order of effort. Move one: **lock the API surface and merge the stranded branches** (rows 1, 4, 6) — under two days of work, closes the breach class and the ghost merges. Move two: the **truth + legal package** (rows 2–3, counsel predicate) — strip broker/MLS/entity/cash-offer/statute claims at their single sources and ship privacy/terms; until this lands, every marketing dollar and every organic visitor increases exposure. Move three: **make the funnel walkable end-to-end** (rows 5, 7, 8, 16) — wizard unblocked, money path atomic, search functional, lifecycle emails wired — then, and only then, does the $499 mechanic have a product behind it. If the broker partnership won't sign this quarter, take the transactional path to waitlist mode; that single decision dissolves half the P0 list.

## 6. What the audits DIDN'T say — Truth Wave check on the sweep itself

1. **Zero customers, zero social proof — and every audit priced against a $500/mo bar nobody pays.** The audits rigorously scored "would an SMB pay for this" and never said the louder thing: no SMB has ever exercised these flows. Findings like "queue truncates at 50 pending items" are hypothetical at zero customers, while the actual P0 — **no prospect motion exists** (no outreach in flight, no demo environment, Conner-time single-threaded per the sales retro) — sat outside every audit's scope. The sweep audits supply; nothing audits demand.
2. **Degraded mode is the default operating condition, not an edge case.** `agentplain/06` calls the paused ANTHROPIC key "the historically common state"; flatsbo's audit env ran on a zero-credit key. Both products' AI value proposition spends much of its life switched off, and the audits test the off-state politely instead of saying: **the product routinely demos its own outage.** Fund and monitor the key like the production dependency it is, or stop leading with AI in the copy.
3. **The audits recommend "wire or soften" and duck the scope question.** Portal, BYO storage, discipline heads, insight library, instant-offer, services page: the honest aggregate read is that **the built-and-marketed surface area exceeds what a one-operator company can keep truthful.** The uncomfortable fix is subtraction — pick the two or three surfaces per product that earn the subscription and delete the rest — and no audit was chartered to say it.
4. **Counsel doesn't exist yet.** Six audits route findings to "counsel review" (agentplain legal pages, flatsbo statute claims, RESPA line, guarantee terms). Per the legal/compliance retro, there is **no engaged counsel and no confirmed entity** — so "route to counsel" is currently a null pointer. Engaging a Georgia real-estate attorney and forming the entity are the actual blockers on rows 2–3, and they're Conner-tasks, not fleet-tasks.
5. **The audit machine outruns the delivery machine — including on this sweep.** June's flatsbo findings re-confirmed verbatim; 4 of 20 July audits never reached a remote; agentplain 08-voice is still in flight. Nobody wrote: the fleet's binding constraint is merge-and-verify throughput, and adding audit waves without adding landing capacity just deepens the backlog this document now ranks for the third time.
6. **flatsbo's continued existence as a live site is itself the top risk.** Every audit inventoried the false claims; none said plainly that with an open PII surface, false broker/entity advertising, no privacy policy, and a dead funnel, the cheapest correct posture may be **waitlist-dark until entity + broker + counsel close** — a one-day change that eliminates more P0 exposure than the entire fix table. It deserves an explicit Conner decision rather than a silent default of staying up.
