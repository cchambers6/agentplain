# Case-study capture framework — how to use this folder

**Written 2026-07-08. Zero paying customers, zero on-record design partners exist today. Nothing in this folder is a case study.** This is the capture system for the first real one, built now so that the day design partner #1 signs, the fleet already knows exactly what story to collect, on what days, with what permissions — no scramble, no month-three reconstruction.

Why this exists before the customer does: case study #1 is the highest-value marketing asset the company will produce in year one. Every subsequent sales conversation leans on it — the trust gap is the binding constraint, and one named, permissioned, quantified broker story is the single strongest instrument against it. A story reconstructed at day 90 from memory is worth a fraction of one captured forward from Day 0. This folder is the difference.

## When capture triggers

Capture begins the moment the design-partner letter is signed — not at activation, not "once there's something to show." Day 0 is when the "before" story is freshest and the baseline numbers are honest. The full rhythm is in `03-capture-cadence.md`; the short version:

- **Day 0 (signed):** consent on record, baseline metrics, before-workflow in the partner's words.
- **Day 1 (activation call):** first-moment reactions, activation friction as it happens.
- **Week 2 / Weeks 4–8:** instrumented outcomes plus emerging quotes, captured with methodology attached.
- **Week 12:** publish-ready draft goes to the partner for word-by-word approval.

## Division of labor: what Conner captures, what the fleet ghostwrites

**Conner captures (live, human, unrepeatable):**
- Consent, delivered in his voice on the Day-0/Day-1 calls (script in `01-capture-framework.md` §1).
- Quotes — said by the partner on recorded calls, flagged live ("that — can I have that for the case study?"), timestamp logged. Quotes are never drafted for the partner; they say it, we transcribe it, they approve the transcription.
- The before-workflow walkthrough (discovery recording) and after-workflow walkthrough (week 4–8 call) — the partner narrating their own Tuesday night.
- The partner's gut-check on the saved-time numbers ("does this match your week?") — that answer *is* the "partner reviewed these figures" clause in the methodology footnote.

**The fleet ghostwrites (afterward, from captured artifacts only):**
- The case-study draft itself, assembled from transcriptions, the instrumented numbers, and the dated capture files — never from memory or invention.
- The methodology footnotes, pulled from the saved-time ledger and calibration table at draft time.
- Derivative assets (one-pager proof block, landing-page pull-quote, LinkedIn founder-post draft) — each only after the underlying quote/number has its approval logged.

The fleet writes nothing the capture files can't source. If a sentence in the draft has no artifact behind it, it comes out — that rule and its siblings live in `05-anti-fabrication-rules.md`.
