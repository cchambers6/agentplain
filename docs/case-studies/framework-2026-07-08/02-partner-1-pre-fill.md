## Field 5: Expected quantified outcome + methodology (honest, not inflated)

**Headline shape we expect to earn: N hours reclaimed per week, ledger-measured, in the 3–6 hrs/week band by Week 8.** Deliberately below the 8–12 hrs/week research anchor: the ledger counts only completed fleet actions in the live workflows, not the whole coordination burden, and an honest under-claim outsells an inflated one to this buyer. If a contracts-closed number emerges (a lead answered at night that became a signed deal), it publishes only as a traceable fact ("an evening lead that got a drafted reply within the hour went under contract on [date]"), never as a conversion-rate claim.

**The exact methodology (written into the capture file next to the numbers, from Day 1):**

1. **What counts:** per-action time credits from the saved-time ledger, using the conservative calibration table (`lib/guarantee/` as of the capture date), counted **only for actions the fleet actually completed** in the partner's workspace between two named dates. No estimates, no recollections, no projection beyond the measured window.
2. **What's reported alongside it, same source:** drafts landed in the queue (N/week) and approval rate (approved ÷ (approved + rejected)) — the approval rate stays in even when it's unflattering, because it's the number that proves a human is in the loop.
3. **Partner review:** the numbers are walked through with the partner on a recorded call ("does this match your week?") and their answer is logged. The published footnote's "reviewed and approved these figures" clause refers to that logged moment.
4. **Published verbatim (template format):** *"Hours reclaimed are computed from per-action time credits calibrated conservatively by agentplain (calibration table: `lib/guarantee/` on the date of publication), counted only for actions the fleet actually completed in [partner]'s workspace between [date] and [date]. [Partner] reviewed and approved these figures. Individual results vary."*
5. **Known blocker, tracked:** 4 of 7 calibrated actions have no saved-time writer today (audit 9, P0-1). The lead-triage path is verified during pilot week 1 (runbook Day 2/3); any calibrated action that ran without writing carries an asterisk in the capture file until Engineering closes it. **We do not hand-compute a substitute number for an unwired action.**
