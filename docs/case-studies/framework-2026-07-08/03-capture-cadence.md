# 03 — Capture cadence (day-by-day through the pilot)

The case study is written forward from Day 0, never reconstructed. Each capture moment below rides an interaction that already exists in the pilot plan — capture is minutes inside scheduled calls, not extra ceremonies the partner has to sit through. Week 1 moments align with the week-1 runbook (`docs/pilot/week-1-runbook/`); Day 5's five-minute block is specified in detail there (`04-DAY-5-case-study-material-capture.md`) and is not re-specified here.

Every artifact lands in the partner's capture file **dated, with its source noted** (recording timestamp, ledger query, written approval), so week 12 produces numbers the same way week 2 did.

## Day 0 — signed

*The "before" is never fresher than today.*

- **Consent on record:** the letter covers case-study rights; Conner says the recording consent out loud (script in `01` §1) on the first recorded call.
- **Baseline metrics, dated:** the partner's stated hours/week on coordination work (their number, from the discovery conversation — the research range never substitutes for it), team size, tool stack, and current lead-response reality ("what happens to a lead that arrives at 9pm today?").
- **Before-workflow capture:** the concrete Tuesday-night scene, in their words, from the discovery recording — what the owner personally did, how long it took, what slipped. Notes are fine; a short screen-share video of "here's my evening inbox" is better if the partner offers. This becomes field 3 and is a primary source from this day on: never rewritten, never punched up.

## Day 1 — activation call (90 minutes)

- **First-moment quotes:** the reaction when the first drafted reply appears in their queue — flagged live, timestamped. The activation bar (first approval live on the call) is itself a capture moment: note the time from call start to first approval.
- **Activation friction, honestly:** what took longer than it should have, what confused them, what needed a workaround. This goes in the file even though it may never publish — it's the credibility ballast for everything that does, and it feeds the next activation regardless.

## Day 5 — first check-in (minutes 22–27 of the Friday call)

Per the week-1 runbook: the week's instrumented numbers (drafts landed, approval rate, ledger hours with the methodology footnote written next to them), the quote mine (questions 1 and 5), and the same-day follow-through (transcribe, send for approval, log). If nothing quotable happened, the file says "no quotable moment yet — revisit week 2" and that is a fine outcome.

## Week 2 check-in

- **First-outcome data:** second ledger pull, same queries, dated. Two points make the beginning of a trend; one point stays a point and gets described as one.
- **Emerging quotes:** week 2 is when the rhythm becomes describable ("I clear the queue with coffee") — listen for the habit language; it's usually better material than week 1's novelty reactions.
- **Shot-list update:** one line in the file header — what the study still needs (e.g., "need: approval-gate quote; a dollar-real outcome; week-4 habit evidence"). This list is what Conner aims each subsequent call at.

## Weeks 4–8 — mid-pilot

- **Mid-pilot metrics:** ledger pulls on the standing cadence (with each biweekly check-in), same methodology, cumulative window noted. If a calibrated action ran without a saved-time writer, the asterisk stays on the number until Engineering closes the gap.
- **After-workflow capture:** the same Tuesday-night scene, now — "walk me through what happened with [a real lead from this week], from when it came in to when the reply went out." Same shape as the Day-0 capture, real names scrubbed. Video/notes as on Day 0.
- **The delta sentence, theirs:** "what's the difference between that and week zero?" Captured raw. Their phrasing beats anything we'd polish.
- **Second-workflow evidence** only if a second workflow actually went live — the study describes what ran, not what was configured.

## Week 12 — publish-ready draft

- The fleet assembles the draft from the capture file only (division of labor in `00-README.md`): every sentence traceable to an artifact, methodology footnotes attached, permissions checklist from the template embedded with real dates.
- Draft runs the voice gate; pass logged.
- Draft goes to the partner with the standing framing: *"Edit anything; nothing moves without your yes."* Their edits are final. Approval of the exact text, in writing, dated — then, and only then, the publish path in `04` opens.

## Ongoing — quarterly refresh

- Once per quarter for the life of the engagement: one fresh on-record quote and a re-pull of the headline numbers over the trailing quarter, same methodology.
- A case study whose numbers are more than two quarters stale gets re-verified or gets a dateline (template rule). The refresh takes ten minutes of a call that's already happening; the alternative is a decaying asset we'd eventually have to pull.
- Refresh material also feeds the derivative surfaces (`04`) — a new quote can rotate onto the landing page without touching the case study itself.
