# 04 — The permissioned asset shelf

Captured artifacts are worth more than one case study — each one, once its specific approval is logged, feeds a set of downstream surfaces. This file maps artifact → surfaces, so a single well-captured pilot stocks the whole go-to-market instead of producing one PDF.

**Standing rule:** an asset moves to a surface only when the approval covering *that use* is logged and dated. Quote approval for the case study does not automatically cover an ad; when in doubt, the consent conversation happens again. The shelf is only as good as its permission log.

## Where assets live

Captured raw material (recordings, transcriptions, ledger pulls, approvals) lives in the partner's capture file, private, one folder per partner. Publish-approved derivatives are the only things that leave it. Nothing in the capture file is ever linked or screenshotted externally — end-client scrubbing happens at capture time, but the file as a whole is still partner-confidential.

## Artifact → surface map

### On-record quote (approved verbatim, named person, named brokerage)
- **Website:** the case study itself at `/customers/[slug]`, and one pull-quote on the real-estate landing page.
- **One-pager:** the proof block the sales deep-dive template reserves — one quote, name, brokerage.
- **Outbound emails:** one sentence of borrowed trust in the founder-send sequences ("[Name] at [brokerage] said it this way: …"). The approval for outbound use is asked for separately and explicitly — email feels different to a partner than a webpage does.
- The approval-gate quote specifically is the objection-handling asset: it goes wherever objection #2 ("AI will say something that gets me sued") gets answered.

### Quantified outcome + methodology footnote
- **Pricing-conversation anchor:** the measured hours-reclaimed figure, with its footnote, is what replaces the research-derived value math in live sales conversations — "here's what the ledger recorded for a brokerage like yours" beats any modeled range. The methodology footnote travels with the number everywhere; a number that shows up without its footnote is a bug.
- **Discovery deck:** one slide, per the template's publish path.
- **Guarantee credibility:** the same ledger that feeds the case study feeds the saved-time guarantee — the case study is public proof the meter is real.

### Before/after workflow (the narrative spine)
- **Killer-workflow demo state:** the demo's synthetic before/after gets re-anchored to the real captured arc — same scene shape, honestly labeled as illustrative where it remains synthetic. The demo stops being a guess about a broker's evening and becomes a portrait of one.
- **Google Ads landing page:** if/when the paid test's gate is met, the before/with contrast *is* the ad copy, per the per-vertical narrative ("the before/with contrast is the ad"). Real captured phrasing replaces the placeholder narrative spine.
- **/how-it-works and vertical pages:** the workflow walkthrough, retold in the partner's shapes (with approval where their specifics are recognizable).

### Founder testimonial video (if the partner agrees to camera — never required)
- **LinkedIn hero:** the founder-voice post with partner tagged (pre-approved), per the template's publish path. A 60–90 second partner clip is the strongest single trust asset the company could hold; it is also the biggest ask, so it's raised once, at week 12, when there's a story the partner is proud of — not at signing.
- **Fallback if no camera:** the golden-hour photograph (briefs at `docs/brand/photography-briefs-2026-06-22.md` — zero produced photos today) plus the approved quote does most of the same work.

## What the shelf feeds, in priority order

1. **The next five sales conversations** (one-pager proof block + pricing anchor) — the shortest path from asset to revenue.
2. **The website case study + landing pull-quote** — compounding, passive.
3. **Founder LinkedIn post** — the distribution moment; one post, partner tagged, timed to the study going live.
4. **Outbound sequences** — refreshed with the borrowed-trust line for cohort #2 recruiting.
5. **Ads** — last, and only behind the existing paid-spend gate.

The order matters: every asset serves recruiting design partners #2–5 before it serves anything else, because the second case study is the first one's compounding payoff.
