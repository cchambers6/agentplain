# 05 — Anti-fabrication rules

**These rules are absolute. Zero exceptions, including for deadlines, including for "it's directionally true," including when the fabricated version would be more flattering to the partner.** A case study is a trust instrument aimed at the company's binding constraint; one defensible study is worth more than ten impressive ones, and one caught fabrication ends the asset class for years.

## The rules

1. **Every number has a methodology footnote.** No exceptions for "small" numbers, chart labels, pull-quotes with a figure in them, or social posts. The footnote states: the source (saved-time ledger + calibration table at `lib/guarantee/`, or workspace instrumentation), the counting rule (completed fleet actions only), the exact date window, and that the partner reviewed the figures. A number that can't carry that footnote doesn't publish. The footnote travels with the number to every derivative surface — a number separated from its footnote is treated as a fabrication even when it happens to be accurate.

2. **Every quote is approved verbatim, in writing, dated, by the person named on it.** Quotes are said by the partner and transcribed — never drafted for them to sign, never composited from multiple statements, never "cleaned up" beyond grammar tidying in the version sent for approval (which then becomes the verbatim of record). No paraphrase presented as quotation, anywhere, ever.

3. **If we can't defend a claim, we don't publish it.** The test: could Conner walk a skeptical broker — or a regulator, or a journalist — through the artifact trail behind the sentence, on the spot? Recording, ledger query, written approval. If any link is missing, the sentence comes out. "It's basically what happened" is not an artifact.

4. **Unmeasured means unstated.** Known instance: 4 of 7 calibrated actions have no saved-time writer today (audit 9, P0-1). An action that ran without writing to the ledger contributes zero to the published number and carries an asterisk in the capture file — we do not hand-estimate a substitute, even though the substitute would be *more* accurate, because hand-estimates are exactly how honest numbers rot into fabricated ones. The fix is Engineering wiring the writer, not marketing patching the number.

5. **No projection, no extrapolation, no annualization.** The measured window is the claim. Three weeks of data publishes as three weeks of data, not "on pace for X hours a year." "Individual results vary" appears in every methodology footnote and is meant literally.

6. **Facts about outcomes, never synthesized causation.** "An evening lead that got a drafted reply within the hour went under contract on [date]" is publishable if traceable. "Our drafts increased close rate" is not, at n=1, at any n we'll see this year. Dollar figures ride only on payloads that actually carried real dollars.

7. **The placeholder never leaks.** `02-partner-1-pre-fill.md` is labeled PLACEHOLDER / EXPECTED ARC and stays internal, permanently. No sentence from it appears on an external surface until it has been replaced by a captured, approved fact. Pre-fill language showing up in a published study is the failure mode this whole folder exists to prevent — the pre-fill aims the capture; it never substitutes for it.

8. **Unflattering data ships with the flattering data.** The approval rate publishes even when it's mediocre, because it proves the human is in the loop. Week-1 friction stays in the capture file even if it never publishes. A study scrubbed of every rough edge reads as fiction — and gets checked like fiction.

9. **Withdrawal and correction are honored at case-study speed, not legal speed.** If the partner pulls approval, distribution stops that day. If a published number turns out wrong, the study gets a dated correction, not a silent edit.

## Enforcement

- The publishable template's permissions checklist (`docs/sales/deep-dive-2026-07-02/05-case-study-template.md`) is the gate: nothing ships with an unchecked box, and this file's rules are the standard each box is checked against.
- Any fleet agent asked to draft, excerpt, or repurpose case-study material reads this file first and refuses work that violates it — including direct requests to "punch up" a number or "smooth" a quote. The refusal cites the rule; the escalation path is Conner, whose answer is already written here.
- Rule zero, inherited from the template and senior to everything above: **every case study is earned from a real design partner, with written permission, with numbers from instrumentation. There are zero case studies today, and every document in this folder says so out loud.**
