## The lever

**Send the first five design-partner emails to named Georgia real-estate prospects this week — Conner sends, the fleet clears the send path in the same five days.**

Concretely:

## Why this one, against the alternatives

**Every strategic document this company has produced converges on this action, and it has never happened.** The June business plan named the trust gap as the binding constraint and design partners as the cure. The sales deep-dive says, verbatim, "nothing else in this plan matters if this rhythm doesn't start." The marketing plan calls proof "the input every other channel is starved of." The audit synthesis's own meta-finding: "the sweep audits supply; nothing audits demand." Three planning cycles have now re-derived the same conclusion. A fourth would be malpractice.

**Against "one more product wave":** the product is not the constraint. The approval spine audited clean; the demo runtime exists; the remaining P0s are days of work, not the reason revenue is zero. A week of fixes without outreach produces zero information about willingness to pay. A week of outreach produces reply-rate data even if every answer is no — and prospect replies will re-prioritize the fix queue better than a 21st audit.

**Against paid ads:** gated by our own ratified plan (no proof asset, no measurement, front door broken), and correctly so — cash CAC into a site with zero tracking and zero social proof is burning money to avoid a scarier, cheaper act.

**Against "build the killer demo first":** it's built (PR #303, synthetic data, LLM-free). It needs an audience, not a rebuild.

**Against "fix everything first":** the top-20 fix table is two fleet-weeks deep and will still be there next week. The four send-path items ride along *inside* this lever; the rest can land while replies come back. Outreach links point to `/` and `/pricing` until the redirect dies — the plan already handles the imperfect site.

## Against the no-social-proof constraint (the strongest objection)

"We have zero proof — won't outreach just burn our best prospects?" Three answers:

1. **The design-partner pitch does not require proof; it sells participation.** "Be one of five founding partners, three months free, weekly founder access, shape the product" is an offer whose *premise* is that we're early. It is the only sales motion that gets stronger, not weaker, from having no logos.
2. **Outreach is the only action that manufactures proof.** Every alternative consumes the constraint; this one produces it. The proof shelf (3 permissioned assets) has exactly one input: partners using the product on record.
3. **The prod-key unlock is literally gated on this.** Policy: market-ready AND actively prospecting. The fleet can grind toward market-ready forever; only Conner sending mail satisfies the second condition. This lever is the key that turns the product's AI back on.

The honest risk isn't burning prospects — five emails into a metro-area RE community is not a reputation event. The risk is the same one that's held for three weeks: that the block doesn't get calendared. Which is why the lever is sized at five emails and ninety minutes, not fifty and a campaign.
