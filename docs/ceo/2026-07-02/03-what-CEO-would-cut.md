# The kill list — what is not on the path to profitable

The audits said it politely: "the built-and-marketed surface area exceeds what a one-operator company can keep truthful." The CEO version: this company's failure mode is not building too little, it is building — and analyzing — too much. Cuts below, hardest first. "Kill" = stop now; "Hold" = frozen until a named trigger.

## 1. KILL: new audit / retro / deep-dive / planning loops until the top-20 fix table is burned down

June's flatsbo findings were re-confirmed **verbatim** in July because main never moved. The 2026-07-02 cycle produced ~40 documents ranking the same backlog for the third time. The audit master synthesis itself ordered it: "the next fleet-week is fixes-only." Enforce it: no new analysis waves — including additional passes of the continuous journey loop that generate findings rather than land fixes — until rows 9–14 of the top-20 table (the agentplain fleet-week) are merged and read back on main. This CEO series exempts itself only by staying docs-thin and decision-focused; if Pass 2 finds Pass 1's decisions unexecuted, the series pauses too.

## 2. KILL: all GTM fronts except Georgia real estate

CPA and law stay closed until 2 RE pilots are live (already the sales plan's rule — ratify it as a kill, not a preference). Stop equal-weight asset polishing across five verticals; stop CPA outreach prep while TaxDome/Karbon are unconnectable. The one CPA exception: the S-effort truth fix (stop advertising those connectors) and the QBO books-recon worker, because they're cheap and protect the eventual second front. Law, PM, home-services, general: no GTM attention at all this quarter.

## 3. KILL (recommend; Conner ratifies): flatsbo fleet attention — waitlist-dark

Open PII endpoints, materially false broker/MLS/entity advertising, no privacy policy, a funnel dead at step one, no entity, no license, no counsel. The synthesis said the quiet part: continued existence as a live site is itself the top risk, and waitlist-dark "eliminates more P0 exposure than the entire fix table." agentplain is THE priority (locked memory); flatsbo waits on license + counsel by standing rule. Take it dark in one day, do the two-day API-lock so the dark site isn't leaking, and reassign every flatsbo-pointed cycle to the agentplain fix table. Anything more is investment in the wrong company this quarter.

## 4. KILL: the client portal as a funded workstream — flag it off

Product's own framing is a binary with no middle: 0% activatable, five trust-chain P0s, 9 PII tables outside RLS — all the liability, none of the adoption. Until a signed design partner asks for it, the correct spend is the S-effort safety net (gate the routes, RLS + teardown so the DPA is signable) and nothing else. The L-effort "make it real" wave is cut. Revisit trigger: first partner who needs client document exchange.

## 5. KILL: shipping LLM-dependent features against a paused key

Any feature whose customer value only materializes with a live key is queue-blocked until the un-pause trigger fires. The fleet builds deterministic wiring, activation paths, measurement, and truth fixes — all of which work in degraded mode. This also kills the quiet absurdity the audits flagged: a product that "routinely demos its own outage" getting more AI surface area.

## 6. HOLD: paid media and the photography commission — until the first partner is signed

Paid was already gated (front door + measurement + one proof asset); keep the gate and stop producing new ad creative against it — 25 concepts are enough inventory for a test that can't run yet. Photography (kaizen investment #3) is real but is proof-adjacent polish; pre-proof, the brand's strongest asset is honesty, which is free. Trigger to release both: first signed design partner + measurement wired.

## 7. KILL: new surface area, period

No new verticals (locked rule), no new connectors beyond the RE activation path and the CPA truth fix, no voice/Twilio expansion, no mobile-app store push, no BYO-storage completion, no insight-library buildout, no seasonal-pause billing (profitability lens already ruled it do-not-build). The test for any new build this quarter: *does it appear in the top-20 fix table or the five profitability gates (01 §5)?* If not, it waits.

## What survives — for contrast

The fixes-only fleet-week (top-20 rows 9–14), the spend pipeline + CI floor + guarantee writers (kaizen first five), the send-path items inside this week's lever, the weekly kaizen loop as fix-tracking (not new analysis), and the sales rhythm itself. That's the whole company until revenue exists.
