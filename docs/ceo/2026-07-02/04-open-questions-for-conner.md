# Five decisions only Conner can make

Ranked by decision-value: how much blocked work each answer releases, weighted by how close that work is to revenue. Each has a default the fleet will assume if unanswered by the next CEO pass — silence is itself a decision, so the defaults are stated.

## 1. Commit the sales motion — calendar it, fund it, put your name on it

**The decision:** (a) a recurring 60–90 min Monday block on your calendar for review-and-send, starting this week; (b) ratify the cohort target at 3–5 design partners, RE/Georgia first (the "10 RE partners" figure becomes funnel width, not signed target); (c) approve the ~$100–160/mo GTM stack (Apollo, Calendly/Cal.com, HubSpot Free) — accounts only you can create; (d) ship the founder bio and go on record. With zero customers, you are the only proof surface the company has.
**Why it's #1:** every revenue path in 01 starts at the first send date. This is the only decision where a week of delay measurably moves the profitability curve.
**Default if silent:** the fleet keeps drafting into a queue nobody sends from, and Pass 2 reports the same $0 with one more week of curve-shift.

## 2. Entity + counsel — form it, engage them, this month

**The decision:** confirm or form the legal entity (with founder IP assigned) and engage counsel — one engagement can cover the agentplain packet (ToS/AUP/Privacy sign-off, DPA, clickwrap, guarantee terms, CPA sentinel verification) and the flatsbo exposure question. The counsel-ready handoff packet already exists (`docs/launch/legal-risk-prelaunch-review.md`).
**Why it's #2 not #1:** it blocks the *high-ARPU* future (CPA/law, engagement-letter-grade buyers, a signable DPA) and caps legal risk — but it does not block the first five RE sends. It becomes #1 the day a partner asks to sign something.
**Default if silent:** professional-service verticals stay unsellable; every "route to counsel" item in 20 audits remains a null pointer; personal liability exposure continues unpriced.

## 3. Prod-key un-pause — pick the trigger and the scope

**The decision:** the standing policy (paused until market-ready + active prospecting) collides with finance (un-pause now on a small workspace set to get real cost telemetry) and with sales (a partner can't pilot on a paused key; 2–4 week lag from first send to first pilot). Pick one: **(A)** telemetry un-pause now — governor-capped, 1–2 workspaces, spend measured; or **(B)** un-pause on first booked discovery call, pre-verified so an eager partner never waits.
**Recommendation:** B, with the cost-governor verification done *this week* so the switch is instant. A is defensible only if the spend pipeline (kaizen #1) is wired first — otherwise we un-pause into the same blindness that motivated the pause.
**Default if silent:** policy holds (paused); risk is a signed partner stalled at activation — burning the most expensive reference we will ever recruit.

## 4. flatsbo — waitlist-dark or keep it live

**The decision:** the audit synthesis is unambiguous: open PII surface + false broker/entity advertising + no privacy policy + dead funnel = the live site is net liability. Take it waitlist-dark (one day) + the two-day API lock, and reassign all flatsbo fleet cycles to agentplain until entity + broker + counsel close — or explicitly accept the exposure and fund the flatsbo fix-week now.
**Recommendation:** dark. agentplain is THE priority by your own locked rule; flatsbo's P0s are cheaper to eliminate by subtraction than by repair this quarter.
**Default if silent:** the site stays up, the exposure compounds daily, and it remains version-controlled evidence.

## 5. The subtraction ruling — bless the kill list and settle the standing copy contradictions

**The decision:** (a) ratify 03-what-CEO-would-cut (portal flagged off, non-RE GTM closed, paid/photography held, no new surface area); (b) resolve the two copy conflicts that have each sat 16+ days: the `/security` absolutes (soften now vs. fund the hardening — holding both is the worst state) and the "built on Claude" canon conflict (the vendor-invisible rule vs. the 2026-06-06 SBM memory — pick one, write it into the allowlist).
**Why it matters:** the fleet executes whatever isn't explicitly cut; without a blessed kill list, surface area regrows and the truth-maintenance burden with it. The copy rulings unblock queued marketing and sales work and cost you ten minutes each.
**Default if silent:** fleet treats 03 as ratified except item 4 (flatsbo, which needs your explicit call), keeps the stricter vendor-invisible rule, and leaves `/security` as-is — the one default that is actively bad, since skeptical prospects will read that page the week outreach starts.
