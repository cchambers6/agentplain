# CS's contribution to profit — retention math, the value of a save, and why three quotes outweigh everything

**All dollar figures are modeled, not measured** — no customer has ever exercised the runtime (path-to-profitable §1, PR #348). Prices from `lib/pricing/tiers.ts`; contribution ≈ **$185/mo per RE solo seat** (~95% modeled margin). Code supersedes this doc.

---

## 1. The structural fact: at 95% margin, CS is the profit engine

When contribution per seat is ~$185 on a $199 price, acquisition adds seats but **retention decides whether they compound**. The path-to-profitable sensitivity says it plainly: above ~7–8%/mo churn, founder-led acquisition (the only channel we have) cannot even hold $10K MRR — every seat saved is a seat Conner doesn't have to re-sell at ~2 hrs/wk of the company's scarcest input. And CS at n≤5 runs on ~$0 cash (founder minutes + Fable drafting + code already on main). A function with near-zero cost defending a ~95%-margin revenue line is, arithmetically, the highest-ROI department in the company — *provided it defends rather than builds* (`05-what-CS-must-stop.md`).

## 2. What one converted design partner is worth

A design partner who converts at month 3 (RE solo, $199/mo):

| Retention after conversion | Contribution (12 mo from July start) | Lifetime shape |
|---|---|---|
| Churns month 5 (bad onboarding echo) | ~$370 | Program failure — and a *negative* reference risk |
| Stays 9 paid months (year one) | ~**$1,665** | Baseline success |
| Stays + one seat of expansion or one referral | ~$3,300+ | What the weekly-call relationship is designed to produce |

So the direct math on the pilot cohort is modest: 3 partners × ~$1.7K ≈ **~$5K of year-one contribution**. If direct revenue were the point, the program would be a poor use of 13 founder-weeks of calls. It isn't the point — see §4.

## 3. What one save-motion is worth

A save (per `02-…success-criteria.md`) costs roughly **90 founder-minutes + one fleet fix**. What it buys, for the *first* partners:

- **Direct:** the partner's remaining LTV (~$1.7–3.3K modeled).
- **Replacement cost avoided:** at the sales plan's own funnel rates (~5 sends → 1 discovery → ⅓–¼ sign), replacing a lost partner costs ~15–20 sends' worth of founder outreach cycles — weeks of calendar, not dollars.
- **The asymmetric term:** a churned *design partner* is not a churned customer; it's a **failed reference in the exact 3–5 person cohort the entire GTM plan depends on**, in a Georgia RE community small enough to talk. The kill radius of losing partner #1 badly exceeds any LTV table.

Priced honestly: the first save-motion of the pilot era is worth **more than any feature the fleet could ship in the same week**. That's why `02` gives it a 5-business-day clock and Conner's personal ownership.

**The other save that's pure margin:** guarantee discipline. Audit 9's P0 means saved-time reads 0 minutes for 4/7 calibrated actions — auto-refund in human-review mode (kaizen 06, fix 4) is CS directly plugging the only known recurring dollar leak until Engineering's writers land (`04`, Engineering item 3).

## 4. Why the first 3 partners' on-record quotes are the highest-value asset in the company

The claim sounds like marketing sentiment. It's balance-sheet reasoning:

1. **Proof is the ratified binding constraint.** Every planning cycle converged on it (CEO doc 02: "the trust gap — zero paying customers, zero social proof"). The company's revenue curve is gated not on features (product audited fine) but on a stranger believing a claim. A named Georgia broker saying "Plaino drafts my lead replies and I approve them over coffee — it gave me back four hours a week" is the only artifact that closes that gap. We cannot build it, buy it, or write it ourselves — **CS is the only department whose output can be a quote.**
2. **The quotes are the input to every other channel.** The sales plan's funnel rates assume a proof shelf; the marketing plan calls proof "the input every other channel is starved of"; paid spend is gated behind a proof asset existing at all. Three quotes don't add a channel — they *un-gate all of them simultaneously.* No other asset in the company has that fan-out.
3. **They compound and they're durable.** The program calendars a quarterly on-record quote from signing day (design-partner doc §6) — a stream of proof, not a one-shot. And a true, named, word-approved quote survives every pivot, redesign, and pricing change on the roadmap. Code depreciates; a reference from a respected local broker-owner appreciates as the community it circulates in learns the name.
4. **The counterfactual prices them.** Without quotes: outreach converts at cold-stranger rates and every sale is founder-labor-intensive, forever. With three: warm referral motion opens in a community that talks, the case study feeds SEO/AEO, and the demo call starts from "you know Smith Realty? that's us." The delta between those two funnels, over 2026, is the difference between the month-8-and-month-never $10K MRR scenarios in the path-to-profitable timeline. Measured against that delta, ~$5K of pilot-cohort revenue is a rounding error — **the cohort's real output is the quotes, and CS's plan (`01`, `02`) is engineered so the product experience makes them true.**

## 5. The CS scoreboard, in profit terms

Until n≥5, CS reports four numbers on the Friday scoreboard, each mapped to the equation it moves:

| Number | Moves |
|---|---|
| Approvals/week per partner | Leading churn indicator → retention → the compounding term |
| Saved-time minutes accruing (once writers land) | The case-study number → proof → every funnel rate |
| Save-motions opened / resolved inside 5 days | LTV defended + reference risk contained |
| On-record quotes banked (target: 3 by month 3 of the pilot) | The binding constraint, dissolving |

Everything else CS could measure at this scale is decoration.
