# What Legal must stop doing

Date: 2026-07-03. The kill list is ratified (PR #354) and the loop mandate is
design-for-profitable. Legal capacity — fleet drafting time and, far scarcer,
counsel review minutes and Conner decision minutes — goes only to what converts
design partners or caps a live exposure. Everything below is stopped, with the
restart condition stated so nothing is killed ambiguously.

**1. STOP drafting terms for non-RE verticals.**
No CPA engagement annexes (§7216/Circular 230), no law-vertical UPL language, no
PM landlord-tenant terms, no RIA/insurance/mortgage/title paper. Nine of ten
sentinel corpora are DRAFT and fire nothing; sales has closed CPA/law until two RE
pilots run (`project_sales_deepdive_2026_07_02`). Terms for verticals nobody can
buy are inventory that expires.
*Restart when:* two RE pilots live and a named CPA or law prospect is in a real
conversation — then draft that vertical's annex only.

**2. STOP writing or endorsing compliance claims that require funded hardening.**
No SOC 2 / ISO / pen-test / HIPAA language, no hard incident-response SLAs, no
"bank-grade" adjectives, on any surface or in any sales answer. KILL #7 blocks the
hardening spend that would make such claims true; the /security ruling (PR #354)
already softens the live absolutes. If a partner's counsel asks a security
question, the answer is the architecture-grounded truth on the softened /security
page — encryption, RLS isolation, OAuth scope minimization — never an aspiration.
*Restart when:* the profitable milestone fires and a hardening workstream is
funded; claims follow artifacts, not the reverse.

**3. STOP any DPA path — reviewing, signing, or promising one.**
The kaizen retro shows the DPA blocked three ways, and audit 10/10 shows the
runtime cannot honor processor deletion/RLS commitments for portal tables. Signing
a DPA today warrants something untrue; *promising* one in a sales thread is the
same commitment on a delay. If a design partner requires a DPA to sign, that fact
escalates to Conner + counsel as packet item 3.1 rather than being absorbed as a
drafting task. The pilot cohort signs the short-form as direct customers.
*Restart when:* E1/E3 (doc 05) land — portal invariants in CI and a verified
deletion path — then the DPA template merges from the data-minimization branch and
goes to counsel.

**4. STOP one-shot legal audit waves.**
Three waves (06-11, 06-16, 07-02) each found drift the previous one had already
found; the `/security` absolutes survived 16 days *between* waves. The full-audit
synthesis (PR #344) already ruled fixes-only. Legal runs the small standing checks
(claims rule in doc 03 item 4, `[COUNSEL]` burn-down in the packet) and otherwise
does not commission new audits of surfaces that haven't changed.
*Restart when:* a surface materially changes, or the weekly-check automation
(kaizen improvement #1) exists to make watching cheap.

**5. STOP expanding jurisdiction-specific machinery beyond GA real estate.**
No per-state AI-disclosure trigger matrices, no 50-state voice-consent data layer,
no state privacy-statute tracker builds. The sell motion is RE/Georgia; the
14-state voice list is hardcoded, env-gated, and not in the offer. These are
counsel-review *data* problems to solve once, later, with the sentinel-corpus
pattern — not fleet projects to start now.
*Restart when:* a paying workflow actually crosses the relevant state line.

**6. STOP letting legal work enter through side doors.**
Meetings, sales threads, and audit PRs keep generating "legal should look at…"
items with no queue. From today the counsel handoff packet (doc 01) is the single
intake: an item not in the packet does not get counsel minutes. This is a stop —
it stops untracked legal work — phrased as the system that replaces it.

## What this frees capacity FOR

The short-form signature path (doc 02), the five blocking ToS/Privacy items
(doc 03), the flatsbo minimum (doc 04), and keeping the counsel packet moving.
That is the entire legal department until the first partners sign.
