# How Legal turns the design-partner motion into signed contracts

Date: 2026-07-03. Legal is usually modeled as a cost center that slows deals. At
this company's stage the causality is reversed: with zero customers, the first
five conversations are the entire pipeline, and the ways legal contributes to
profit are concrete and near-term.

## 1. Speed: yes-to-signature in under 48 hours

The CEO lever is Monday's five sends. The conversion math on a five-send cohort is
brutal — losing one interested reply to a two-week paper cycle is losing 20% of
the pipeline. The short-form (doc 02) is one page, references only published
documents, needs no negotiation for the standard offer, and signs electronically.
The profit contribution is measured as a cycle time: **first yes → signed
agreement < 48 hours**, with zero terms improvised in email threads (each
improvised term is a future dispute we drafted ourselves).

## 2. Trust: the honesty spine is a sales asset legal maintains

The pitch to a broker-owner is "it drafts, you approve, nothing leaves without
your name on it" — that is simultaneously the product's liability posture
(no-outbound architecture), the ToS autonomy clause (doc 03 item 7), and short-form
§§1–2/7. When the legal paper, the marketing page, and the runtime all say the
same sentence, diligence takes minutes instead of meetings. The same holds for the
softened /security page: a skeptical prospect who reads defensible,
architecture-grounded claims trusts them; one who reads a 24-hour IR SLA from a
one-founder company discounts everything else on the site. Truthful copy is not
compliance overhead — it is what lets an unknown vendor pass a buyer's sniff test.

## 3. Focus: counsel minutes spent only where they convert

Conner's counsel review is ongoing and finite. The handoff packet (doc 01) orders
it by revenue adjacency: Batch 1 is what a signing partner touches; Batch 2 is
what the first self-serve dollar touches; Batch 3 is parked. The stop-doing list
(doc 06) prevents the classic failure — counsel time consumed by nine verticals'
hypothetical terms while the one real cohort waits. One engagement covering both
agentplain and the flatsbo question (CEO open question #2) keeps the total legal
spend near the floor.

## 4. Downside protection priced against the pipeline, not in the abstract

The two exposures that could actually cost more than the pipeline is worth this
quarter: (a) a discriminatory phrase in an approved RE draft — mitigated by the
live HUD sentinel plus short-form §7 putting final responsibility with the
approving partner; (b) the flatsbo live site — capped by the doc-04 minimum
(privacy notice, no-brokerage disclaimer, PII lock) at about one fleet-day of
cost. Everything else on the risk map is real but slower-moving than 14 days, and
is queued rather than firefought.

## 5. The reference asset: publicity rights done right

The design-partner program's entire return is the case study, the testimonial,
and the sales reference. Short-form §6 secures those as *rights we can actually
use* — written approval per specific use, so a partner's later blessing is clean
and irrevocable-in-fact because it was never coerced. A testimonial with murky
consent is unusable in front of exactly the buyers it was collected for. Legal's
contribution here is that the company's first proof artifacts are legally load-
bearing.
