# Marketing's profit contribution — the mechanism, stated honestly

The profit equation (CEO pass 1): cash-breakeven is 3–9 seats at ~$185 contribution per RE seat; $10K MRR is roughly the founder-inclusive line; the entire revenue curve is anchored to the first send date. Marketing controls neither the send nor the product. What it controls is the conversion probability at two moments — the reply and the booking — and whether the funnel is legible afterward. All of it at zero cash cost, which at a $0-revenue starting line is the only kind of marketing spend that makes sense.

No reply-rate or booking-rate predictions appear below. We have zero sends ever; any percentage would be invented. Monday establishes the baseline. What follows is the mechanism by which each deliverable moves the two numbers, so the Jul 17 readback can judge each one against what actually happened.

## How the work improves Monday reply rates

1. **A matched link beats a home page** (pipeline piece 1 + the Sales stack map). A first-touch that says "you run Follow Up Boss; here's our honest take on what it does well and what we'd draft around it" proves homework before asking for time. It converts the email from cold pitch to relevant note. This is the single highest-leverage thing marketing ships this window, and it is why piece 1 publishes before the send, not after.
2. **The look-up surfaces don't leak trust anymore.** Between reading the email and replying, a prospect checks the sender and the site. The send-path wave fixed the front door (`/how-it-works` reachable, `/contact` real); marketing's banner update fixes the founder surface, and the operator story gives the "does anyone use this?" question a true answer. Each is a leak plugged at the exact moment a maybe becomes a reply or a delete.
3. **The offer reads as an offer, not a discount.** The refreshed one-pager holds the ratified design-partner frame: founding cohort of five, three months free, weekly founder access, name goes public only with sign-off. That premise gets stronger from having no logos, which is the only marketing position that is true for us and unavailable to every funded competitor.
4. **The honest-concession posture is the differentiator prospects can verify in one click.** Every comparison names where the incumbent wins first. A broker who has been burned by tool marketing notices; it is the brand argument compressed into a behavior.

## How the work improves week-2 discovery-call bookings

1. **Chain touches carry fresh, specific material.** The 5/12/21 chain stops being "bumping this" the moment touch 2 references the operator story published two days earlier and touch 3 can reference the demo walk-through. Each touch gives a silent prospect a new reason to click that did not exist at the last touch.
2. **The booking moment is de-risked.** Invite copy promises 25 minutes, a conversation, nothing to prepare; the thumbnail makes the calendar entry look like a person runs it. Friction at this step is invisible in averages and fatal at n=5, where one no-show is 20% of the cohort.
3. **The pre-read does the demo's job before the call.** Piece 4 attached at booking means the call starts at "how would this run on my desk" instead of "what is this," which is the difference between a 25-minute qualification and a 25-minute explanation. It also survives being forwarded to the partner or spouse who wasn't on the email.

## The proof-shelf flywheel (the real contribution, one quarter out)

Reply → call → partner → permissioned proof asset. Marketing's structures wait on the far side of that chain: the case-study skeleton (deep-dive 02, content type 4), the citation tracker, the comparison cluster ready to carry a true quote. The first partner converts the operator story from dogfooding to customer proof, unlocks the photography trigger, and opens the paid gate's third condition. Every channel this business will ever run is currently starved of exactly one input, and this window's work is the shortest path to producing it.
