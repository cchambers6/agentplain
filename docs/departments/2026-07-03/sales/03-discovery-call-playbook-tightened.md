# 03 — Discovery call, tightened to 20 minutes

**Parent:** `docs/sales/deep-dive-2026-07-02/04-discovery-call-playbook.md` remains the full operating layer (objection library §6, qualification frame §4, living sections §7 — not restated here; read it the night before the first call). This document compresses the 30-minute agenda to 20 because the first-touch ask is literally "worth a 20-minute call this week?" — **the call must cost what the email said it would.** Running long is the prospect's choice, never the agenda's.

**Standing rules unchanged:** recorded with stated consent · listen-first (they talk >60% of the first half) · CRM updated same day · a "no" with a stated reason is a deliverable.

---

## The 20-minute agenda

| Time | Block | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| 0–2 | Open + frame | Consent to record. The service-partner framing paragraph (parent §2, adapted not recited): reads your existing tools, drafts the work, **nothing sends without your approval**, we install and run it. Agenda check: "I want to spend most of this on how your week actually runs, show you one thing, and decide together if there's a next step — fair?" |
| 2–11 | Discovery | The three must-ask questions (below) plus whatever the answers open. Follow the pain, not a list. RE add-on if it fits naturally: "when a lead comes in at 9pm, what happens before 9am?" |
| 11–15 | Show one thing | The synthetic-data demo (PR #303), one workflow only — a draft **sitting in the approval queue**, edited, approved. Say: "demonstration workspace on synthetic data — real product, not your data." The approval gate is the headline, not the AI. Resist showing a second thing. |
| 15–18 | Qualify + the ask | If design-partner fit: the terms, plainly — 3 months free, weekly call with me, joint case study you approve word by word, on-record testimonial once you've seen value. The asks are stated in the same breath as the gives — nothing sprung later. |
| 18–20 | Next step | One concrete step **with a date**: kickoff booked, or one-pager sent + revisit date named, or a clean warmly-delivered not-yet. Never "I'll follow up sometime." |

## The 3 questions that MUST get asked

Every other question is optional; if the call ends without these three answered, the call failed as a discovery call regardless of how it felt.

1. **"Walk me through last Tuesday — where did the day actually go?"**
   *Why it's mandatory:* their answer is the case-study "before" in their own words, the input for objection #11's ROI math ("take the hours you told me about…"), and the fastest test of whether a recurring block of draft-shaped work exists at all. No pain named = no pilot (disqualifier, parent §4).

2. **"What tools run the business — email, calendar, books, e-sign? And who touches them: just you, or staff?"**
   *Why it's mandatory:* it's the activation gate in disguise. Google/M365 + QuickBooks = pilotable this month. Everything-inside-a-franchise-stack or PMS-only books = an honest not-yet **on this call**, before either side spends more. Also surfaces the seat count for tier fit and pre-fires objection #16 on our terms.

3. **"Has anything ever gone out to a client that shouldn't have — and what happened?"**
   *Why it's mandatory:* it opens the approval-gate value without pitching it — the broker carries fair-housing exposure personally and *their story* makes the case better than our copy can. Their answer scripts the demo emphasis two minutes later and pre-loads the honest response to "can't it just send automatically?" (objection #19: no, by architecture — and this is why you'll be glad).

## Hard rails (unchanged from parent, restated because they're load-bearing)

- **Vendor invisibility:** if asked which AI is under the hood — "we don't discuss which models we run; we manage that layer so you don't have to." Never confirm or deny a vendor name in any direction. The privacy-page subprocessor list is the sole sanctioned disclosure (parent §6, objection 6).
- **Never position against Claude/ChatGPT as competitors.** The comparison is with tools and labor, not models.
- **No invented proof.** Zero named customers, said plainly. The dogfooding line ("the same fleet pattern runs a real brokerage's operations daily") is the claims-spine-sanctioned substitute.
- **Disqualify warmly and specifically** when a disqualifier surfaces — and always ask the exit question on any no: *"what would have had to be true for this to be a yes?"* Log it verbatim (parent §7); at n=5 prospects, that answer is worth more than the call.
- **Design-partner weekly call is a program benefit, not a tier benefit** — say so explicitly, or it converts as a churn-shaped misunderstanding.
