# 05 — What Sales must STOP doing (ratified stop-list, 14-day window and until revisited)

The kill list and the planning direction check both say the same thing: the failure mode isn't bad strategy, it's activity that substitutes for the send. Everything below is stopped **because it competes with the only deliverable** — five sends, two replies, one call. Each item names what would reopen it, so stopping is a decision with an exit, not a mood.

| # | STOP | Why | Reopens when |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **All CPA and law prep.** No lists, no scripts, no "just researching the GSCPA directory," no connector-contingent planning. | Ratified kill: CPA/law closed until 2 RE pilots live. CPA additionally blocked by the TaxDome/Karbon P0 and counsel-unverified sentinel. Every hour there is an hour not spent on the five names. | 2 RE pilots active (and for CPA: connector fix + counsel pass). |
| 2 | **Cold email at scale.** No sequences beyond the 5/week founder cap, no sending tools, no list-buying beyond enrichment of named rosters, no domain warm-up projects. | The motion *is* the demo: fleet drafts, founder approves and sends. Scale before the conversion path is proven burns the most expensive prospects we'll ever contact — and a scaled motion before product-market signal is scaled guessing. | Weekly rhythm held ~a month, reply handling never slipped, and observed rates (deep-dive doc 06) justify more volume. |
| 3 | **All paid acquisition.** No ads, no boosted posts, no sponsorships, no "small tests." | Gated by the marketing plan's own 4-condition gate — none met (no proof asset, no tracking, front door only just fixed). CAC math (CEO doc: payback box ~$370/qualified trial) is unverifiable with zero funnel data. | All four gate conditions met, after design partners produce proof. |
| 4 | **New sales assets.** No new decks, no new one-liners, no new email variants, no rewriting the packet library. The five variants + objection library + playbooks are the canon. | 31 claims-grounded files already exist; the kaizen headline was assets-without-motion. New copy is the most seductive form of not-sending. | Observed reply data (≥15 sends) shows a specific variant failing — then edit that variant, with claims-spine review. |
| 5 | **The drifted legacy scripts.** `docs/marketing/sales-scripts/*` are deprecated for use ("first month free" drift, stale agent lists). Don't send from them, don't quote them, don't reconcile them this window. | Known-wrong claims adjacent to a live outreach motion are a liability; reconciliation is real work that isn't the send. | Week 3+, as a marketing hygiene task (delete or reconcile — sales' vote: delete). |
| 6 | **Discounting and improvised terms.** No "pilot pricing" (banned term), no discounts off the ladder, no custom quotes on a first call, no promising integrations or dates. | At ~95% modeled margin the constraint is trust, not price. The design-partner offer (3 months free, terms priced for being first) *is* the concession, and it's better-shaped than any discount. | Never, in this form. Pricing changes are a Conner/CEO decision, not a sales tactic. |
| 7 | **Secondary lanes — including warm-ish ones.** No property-management outreach, no "general" prospecting; a warm *inbound* referral that doesn't fit RE gets a polite hold ("we're heads-down with our founding real-estate cohort — can I come back in a few weeks?"), logged with a date. | Five-front war is the named historical failure. PM/general open weeks 3–4 *only if* the RE cadence held. An exception "just this once" is how the beachhead dissolves. | RE cadence stable for 2–3 weeks (per deep-dive sequencing). |
| 8 | **Re-auditing and re-planning.** No new sales audits, no strategy revisions, no additional prospect-research sweeps past the named five + alternate + the fleet's week-3 refill. This document series is the last planning artifact until the Friday-review data exists. | The direction check's verdict: strategy right, activity mix wrong. A fourth convergent plan would be malpractice (CEO doc's word, and it's the right word). | Real funnel data exists to plan against (post-week-2 review). |

**The test for anything not on this list:** does it make Monday's send more likely to happen, or a reply more likely to convert to a call, within 14 days? If no — it waits, and it goes in the Friday review's parking lot, not in the week.
