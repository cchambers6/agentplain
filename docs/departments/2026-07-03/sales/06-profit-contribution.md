# 06 — Sales' contribution to profitable

**Frame:** `docs/ceo/2026-07-02/01-path-to-profitable.md` is the equation of record; this document states what the sales department contributes to each term, what it costs, and the one variable it actually controls. All funnel rates are operator estimates until observed (deep-dive doc 06 replaces them with real rates from week 3). Prices from `lib/pricing/tiers.ts` — code supersedes this doc.

## 1. The one variable sales controls: the first-send date

The CEO math is explicit: cash-breakeven is **3–9 customers** (lean/operating bands at ~$185 contribution per RE seat); $10K MRR ≈ founder-inclusive profitability and is a **month-8-to-12 event from a July start — anchored entirely to the first send date**. Every week of send-delay shifts the whole revenue curve right by a week; no product work shifts it left.

That makes the sales department's profit contribution unusually legible right now:

> **Value of this 14-day plan ≈ the value of not losing another week of the curve.** At the $10K/mo target run-rate, each week of delay is roughly $2.3K of eventual MRR arriving one week later, forever after — compounding against every future cohort that referral and proof would have seeded.

## 2. Contribution by term of the profit equation

| Equation term | What sales does to it (next 14 days → 90 days) |
|---|---|
| **Revenue (Σ seats × price)** | Builds the only pipeline that exists: 5 sends → target 2+ replies → 1 discovery → design partners at 3 months free → **first paid conversions day 90–120**. Planned revenue through day 90 is $0, printed honestly — the contribution is the *dated* pipeline that makes post-day-90 revenue a schedule instead of a hope. |
| **Margin (1 − v)** | Protects it by policy: no discounting off the ladder, no "pilot pricing," no custom terms (stop-list #6). At ~95% modeled margin, every point given away in a nervous first negotiation is pure contribution lost. The 3-months-free design-partner term is bounded, pre-ratified, and buys proof — the highest-ROI use of margin available. |
| **Z (fixed cost)** | Adds ~$100–160/mo (booking tool, Apollo enrichment, CRM at free tier) — the entire cash cost of the GTM motion, already inside the CEO's lean band. Sales adds no headcount, no agencies, no paid spend (stop-list #3). |
| **H × R (founder time)** | Caps the draw: ~2 hrs/wk pre-pilot + 30 min per active pilot. The motion is deliberately shaped so the founder is the closer, not the pipeline — an SDR-or-fleet can run everything but the calls. This is what keeps "truly profitable" reachable without pricing Conner's whole week into the equation. |
| **Churn (the future killer)** | Above ~7–8%/mo churn, founder-led acquisition can't hold $10K. Sales' pre-emptive contribution: partner selection filtered for reference quality *and* activability (deep-dive doc 02 filter), measurement defined before pilot week 1, and the no-with-reason log feeding product priorities — churn defense built into who we sign, not patched after. |

## 3. What the 5 partners are worth (why free customers are the profit move)

Design partners generate $0 for three months, then convert at standard pricing (60% haircut assumed until observed). Their profit contribution isn't their seats — it's that they are the **input to every constrained term at once**:

- **Proof → conversion rate:** 3–5 on-record partners collapse objection #1 ("you have no customers") for every subsequent prospect. The trust gap is the binding constraint of the whole business; partners are its only manufacturer.
- **Proof → the prod-key unlock:** policy gates the key on market-ready + actively prospecting. The send literally turns the product's AI back on.
- **Case studies → CAC:** the permissioned proof shelf is what eventually makes paid spend pass its gate at a sane CAC (payback box: ~$370 per qualified trial at ~$185/mo contribution). No partners, no proof, no paid channel — ever.
- **Verbatim market data → roadmap:** five discovery calls' worth of stated objections and no-reasons re-prioritizes the fix queue better than any internal audit (and did the audits' own meta-finding not say exactly this — "nothing audits demand").

## 4. The honest risk ledger

- **These numbers are modeled, not earned.** Reply/call/close rates are priors; week-3 reviews replace them with observed rates and the forecast re-anchors. We will not report weighted pipeline as if it were money (deep-dive doc 06's banned-metrics list applies).
- **Downside of the plan failing is bounded and cheap:** ~$150 of tools and ~4 founder-hours. The downside of *not running it* is unbounded curve-shift. Asymmetry is the argument.
- **The stall is the risk.** Not the market, not the copy, not the price. If week 2's review shows zero sends, the profit contribution of this department is negative (it consumed planning cycles) and the Friday conversation says so in those words.

**One-line summary for the CEO roll-up:** Sales converts ~2 founder-hours/week and ~$150/month into the first-send date moving from "never" to 2026-07-06 — the single variable the entire path-to-profitable curve is anchored to.
