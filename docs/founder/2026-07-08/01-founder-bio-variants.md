# Founder bio — three lengths, one voice

Three drafts of the same person at three depths. Written in the founder register (`docs/brand/voice-guidelines-2026-06-19.md` §6–7): direct, concrete, no hedging, no inflation. Every claim traces to the ledger at the bottom.

**What none of these claim:** customers, revenue, funding, exits, degrees, press, or team size. We have none of the first five to claim and the last is "me plus the fleet," which the 200-word version says outright.

---

## 25-word — X/Twitter bio field

> Founder, agentplain — a service partnership doing the 9pm work for Georgia local businesses. The fleet drafts; you approve. My own company runs on it.

*24 words. If the field is tight, the last sentence drops first; the first two carry the offer and the control line.*

## 75-word — LinkedIn About section

> I'm building agentplain: a service partnership that does the drafting work local business owners shouldn't be doing at 9pm — lead replies, invoice chases, the overnight recap. The fleet drafts; you approve. Nothing goes out without your name on it. I run my own real-estate company on the same fleet. I'm choosing a small cohort of Georgia broker-owners as founding design partners: three months free, weekly calls with me, first say in what gets built.

*74 words. Builds on the About-line draft already ratified in `docs/departments/2026-07-03/marketing/03-outbound-support-collateral.md` §2. "Small cohort," never a countdown (design-partner program rule).*

## 200-word — site "About Conner" section

> I'm Conner Chambers. I started agentplain because I watched local business owners — starting with the real-estate people around me in Georgia — spend their evenings on work nobody pays them for. The 9pm lead reply. The third invoice chase. The transaction email answered from the car. The work they're actually good at gets whatever time is left.
>
> agentplain is a service partnership, not a tool you configure. We install a fleet of capable AI partners inside your business, connect the tools you already use, and run a monthly review. The fleet drafts the between-the-records work; you approve and send, every time. Nothing leaves without your name on it.
>
> I built the fleet by running it on my own company first. My real-estate operation runs on the same system I'm offering you — same drafts, same approval queue, same morning recap. When something breaks, it breaks on me before it reaches a customer.
>
> Honest position: agentplain has no named customers yet. That's why I'm working with a small cohort of founding design partners — three months free, a weekly call with me, and a real say in what gets built.
>
> Intelligence rooted in reality. That's the whole pitch.

*~197 words. First person throughout; Conner signs it. The tagline closes the about page per `project_agentplain_mission_and_positioning`.*

**Held decision for Conner:** none of the three mention the day job. His career in strategy and product at a large consumer-goods company is real credibility, but naming it in a founder bio invites "so this is a side project?" on a first call. Recommendation: leave it off the bios, keep it on the LinkedIn experience list, and have the one-line answer ready (see `02-linkedin-profile-audit.md`, item 7).
