# Founder's note — the warmer paragraph for first-touch emails

**Where it goes:** any first-touch email where the prospect deserves a warmer open — it slots where `{{custom-hook}}` sits in the outreach-kit variants (`docs/outreach/2026-07-03-design-partner-kit/01-first-touch-emails.md`). Use it whole or not at all; trimmed to a sentence it loses the point. It also works nearly verbatim as the one LinkedIn post the profile audit calls for.

**What it is not:** marketing copy. It reads like a person because it only says things that are true of the person.

---

## The note *(94 words)*

> A word on why this is me emailing and not a marketing tool: agentplain has no sales team. It's me. I picked Georgia real estate first because it's the business I know — my own company runs on the same fleet I'm offering you, every day, and when something breaks it breaks on me first. I'm writing to a handful of broker-owners whose names I chose myself, because whoever answers will shape the next three months of what gets built. If it's not you, no hard feelings — and if you know the right broker-owner, I'd take the name.

---

## Why each line earns its place

- **"No sales team. It's me."** — answers the why-personally question in six words and is verifiably true.
- **"The business I know / runs on the same fleet"** — the why-RE answer, and the one sanctioned production proof (the dogfooding story). Same claim, same shape, as the shipped first-touch emails.
- **"Names I chose myself"** — true (the shortlist is hand-built in `docs/outreach/2026-07-03-design-partner-kit/00-cohort-shortlist.md`) and it tells the reader this isn't a blast.
- **"Whoever answers will shape the next three months"** — the why-now, stated as a fact about the design-partner program rather than urgency theater.
- **The referral close** — costs nothing, and a warm name from a "no" is the second-best outcome of any first touch.

**Rules it obeys:** no booking link (first touches carry a soft ask only), no customer claims, no vendor names, no exclamation points. If the email already uses variant (a)'s referral opener, cut this note's last sentence so the email doesn't ask for a referral twice.
