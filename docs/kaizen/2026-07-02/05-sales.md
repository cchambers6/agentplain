## 1. Ten things we're set up well for

1. **A complete, symmetric packet library.** Every vertical (real-estate, CPA, law, property-management, general) has the same six assets: cold + warm outreach email, 4-touch follow-up sequence, 30-min discovery-call agenda, call-booking templates, program one-pager, 10-slide why-us deck. Nothing has to be written from scratch to start outreach.
2. **A claims compliance spine.** `_shared/CLAIMS_GROUND_TRUTH.md` makes every claim traceable to code or content files, declares code the source of truth over prose, and doubles as a reviewer checklist. This is the Truth Wave discipline applied to sales — most seed-stage outreach has nothing like it.
3. **Honesty as the differentiator, not a caveat.** The packets say plainly: no existing customers, ROI is operator-modeled not customer-attested, the fleet drafts and a human approves. "The approval gate is the product, not a footnote" is a genuinely distinctive pitch in a market of autonomy overclaims — and it is exactly the right posture for a trust-gap-constrained seller.
4. **Per-vertical persona discipline.** Plaino voice is allowed for real-estate/PM/general and explicitly dropped for law and CPA ("compliance copy is never cute"; sign as Conner, sober). The banned-word list and subject-line rules are enforced in the assets themselves.
5. **Concrete compliance-penalty hooks per vertical.** $26,262 first-offense HUD Fair Housing penalty, $1,000–$5,000 per-return IRC §6694, ABA Model Rules 1.6/7.1 exposure. Specific, citable numbers that earn credibility with exactly the buyer who carries the liability.
6. **Design-partner terms fully specified and internally consistent.** 3 months free → convert or don't; weekly founder time during the pilot (the one sanctioned Conner-time exception outside Max/Custom); early access; joint case study with partner word-approval. The asks back (weekly 30-min call, on-record testimonial, sales reference) are the social-proof engine, pre-negotiated.
7. **The discovery agenda is a real playbook, not a talk track.** Timed blocks, listen-first qualifying questions, demo beats that showcase the approval gate, an explicit ask script, and next-step choreography (one-pager → workspace link → kickoff booking). A first-time seller could run it.
8. **The follow-up sequence is engineered, not repeated.** Four touches at T+3/7/14/21 with a different angle each time (concrete busy-season scene → the liability argument → step-by-step first close → graceful breakup). This is above-median cold-sequence craft.
9. **Channel strategy is already ratified.** The money-GTM pack names the #1 channel (founder-led design-partner outreach), the beachhead (real estate — live killer workflow, counsel-ready corpus, Conner's FlatSBO-adjacent network), and named communities (local/state Realtor associations, Lab Coat Agents, FUB/BoldTrail user groups). No strategy debate is blocking a send.
10. **Pricing and trial mechanics are reconciled to code.** 7-day trial (14 for CPA/law), card at signup, 14-day money-back, Conner-time isolated to Max/Custom in paid tiers — the packets cite `lib/pricing/tiers.ts` verbatim, so a prospect who converts hears the same numbers on the pricing page.

## 2. Ten friction patterns blocking activation

1. **The motion is single-threaded on Conner.** Every email is signed Conner, every discovery call is Conner, every pilot carries a weekly Conner call, and every approval is Conner's. Nothing in the packets designs an async substitute (e.g., a recorded walkthrough in place of a first live call), so zero committed founder hours means zero sends — which is what happened.
2. **Zero named prospects.** The emails are mail-merge-ready (`{{first_name}}`, `{{referrer_name}}`) but no prospect list exists anywhere in the repo or memory. Communities are named; humans are not. The warm variant — the stronger one — depends on a referrer map that also doesn't exist.
3. **`{{CALENDLY_LINK}}` is an unresolved placeholder.** Every booking asset (in-email ask, invite description, confirmation, reminder) dead-ends into a template variable. No scheduling product is provisioned. The conversion path is structurally broken at the last step even if outreach fired today.
4. **No CRM / pipeline of record.** The T+3/7/14/21 cadence is untrackable without per-prospect state and next-action dates. FlatSBO has a pipeline-stage skill (`flatsbo-b2b-sales-followup`: DISCOVERY → QUALIFIED → DEMO → PROPOSAL → CLOSED); agentplain has no equivalent and no store.
5. **No demo asset is wired to the agenda.** The discovery script says "show the draft sitting in the queue" but names no demo workspace, seeded environment, or URL. The synthetic-data killer-workflow demo runtime (PR #303, LLM-free) exists and would serve — but no sales asset references it, so each call would improvise its demo.
6. **Zero social proof and no interim proof ladder.** The binding constraint per the brief is the trust gap. The packets handle it honestly ("you'd be among our first") but nothing substitutes for proof in the meantime: founder bio was held for Conner's approval in the SEO wave and never shipped, there is no screenshots pack, no recorded product walkthrough, no "built in public" artifact a skeptical owner can check.
7. **The post-sale activation path is gated.** Even a converted design partner can't cleanly onboard today: prod `ANTHROPIC_API_KEY` remains paused pending live cost-governor verification (hard gate in the money-GTM memory); the connectors audit (PR #326) found available-but-unconnectable tiles; the customer-portal audit (PR #327) rated the portal 0%-activatable. Selling ahead of this risks burning the first — and therefore most expensive — references.
8. **No outreach rhythm, owner, or freshness check.** Nothing is scheduled, no weekly send target exists, and 0 sends have occurred in ~2.5 weeks since merge. Meanwhile the assets decay: live-workflow and integration facts drift as PRs land, and the claims doc is dated 2026-06-16 with no re-verification cadence.
9. **The older sales scripts contradict the packets.** `docs/marketing/sales-scripts/real-estate.md` opens with "First month is free" (vs. the ratified 7-day trial and the design-partner 3-months-free) and names a different live-agent list than the packets. Objection handling exists only as fragments scattered across these drifted scripts — there is no unified, claims-grounded objection library.
10. **Cohort math and sequencing were never reconciled.** The claims doc caps the cohort at 3–5 partners across all verticals; the money-GTM plan targets 10 real-estate partners; the packet library weights all five verticals equally despite the ratified real-estate beachhead. Without a recorded sequencing decision, the default is to spread Conner-time across five verticals — the worst allocation under the binding constraint.

## 3. Top 5 sales-motion fixes

1. **A weekly outreach rhythm that dogfoods our own pattern.** Monday: the fleet researches ~10 new beachhead prospects and drafts 5 first-touches + due follow-ups into an approval queue; Conner reviews, edits, sends from his own inbox (~60–90 min). Friday: 15-min pipeline review. This is exactly the draft-and-approve, no-outbound architecture we sell — the founder's approval queue becomes the first case study. Target: 5 sends/week sustained beats 50 sends once.
2. **A named-prospect pipeline before more assets.** Build a 50-name real-estate beachhead list (Georgia-first, where the FlatSBO-adjacent network gives warm paths): brokerage, owner name, size, CRM stack (FUB/BoldTrail), source community, warm-path-if-any. Separately map the referrer network the warm variant depends on. CPA/law open only after the RE cadence is stable.
3. **Discovery-call recording + a living playbook.** Record every call (with consent), transcribe, and after each call fold what was heard into the agenda, the objection library, and the ICP notes. The first five calls are product research as much as sales — treat a "no" with a stated reason as a deliverable. Version the agenda so learning compounds instead of evaporating.
4. **One objection library, claims-grounded.** A single file beside `CLAIMS_GROUND_TRUTH.md` seeded with the predictable objections — "you have no customers," "where does my data go," "is this just ChatGPT," "what if it sends something wrong," "which AI is under the hood" (answer per the vendor-invisibility rule), price vs. a VA, "my tool isn't integrated" — each with the approved, honest answer. Reconcile or deprecate the drifted `sales-scripts/*` so there is exactly one source of talk-track truth.
5. **Per-vertical case-study template built now, measurement defined before the first pilot.** Structure: the problem in the owner's words → what we installed → drafts/week and approval rate → hours reclaimed (tied to the saved-time instrumentation the guarantee audit (PR #328) showed is incomplete — fixing that serves sales and the guarantee at once) → partner quote. The pilot then produces publishable proof from week 1 instead of retro-fitting a story at week 12.

## 4. Top 3 investments

1. **Prospect data: Apollo (or ZoomInfo).** At this scale Apollo's entry tier (~$50–100/seat/mo) is likely sufficient to build the ICP-filtered list: owner-operated local businesses, 1–10 seats, US, beachhead SIC/NAICS for residential brokerages then CPA firms. ZoomInfo only if Apollo's local-business coverage proves thin. Deliverable: the 50-name list in fix #2, enriched with email + stack.
2. **Calendly (or Cal.com) + a proper outbound mailbox.** Resolves `{{CALENDLY_LINK}}` — the booking, confirmation, and reminder copy is already written for it. Pair with a dedicated sending setup (subdomain or separate domain, SPF/DKIM/DMARC, gradual warm-up) so 5–20 sends/week from the founder's name never jeopardizes the primary domain's deliverability.
3. **A CRM of record — lightweight.** HubSpot free, Attio, or even Airtable is enough: one row per prospect, pipeline stage (adopt the DISCOVERY → QUALIFIED → DEMO → PROPOSAL → CLOSED-WON/LOST states already defined for FlatSBO), next-action date, and touch history. The requirement is that the T+3/7/14/21 cadence becomes executable and auditable — not that the tool is fancy.

## 5. Conner-decision items (reiterated)

1. **Founder-time budget + cohort target.** Commit a weekly hour count for the sales motion (the rhythm above needs ~2 hrs/wk pre-pilot; each active pilot adds its weekly 30-min call). Resolve the cohort discrepancy: 3–5 partners total (claims doc) vs. 10 real-estate partners (money-GTM plan).
2. **Beachhead sequencing sign-off.** Ratify real-estate-first, Georgia-first, with CPA/law deferred until a set number of RE pilots are live — so the packet library's five-vertical symmetry doesn't silently become a five-front war.
3. **Account and spend approvals.** Apollo/ZoomInfo, Calendly/Cal.com, CRM, and the outbound sending domain all require accounts, cards, and identity decisions only Conner can make.
4. **Founder bio / on-record identity.** Held for Conner since the SEO/AEO wave; with zero customers, the founder is the only proof surface available — this is now sales-critical, not just SEO.
5. **Prod key un-pause after cost-governor verification.** The hard gate from the money-GTM pack still stands; a design partner cannot pilot on a paused `ANTHROPIC_API_KEY`. Sequencing outreach ahead of this is a decision, not a default.
6. **Previously flagged copy items.** The `/security` absolute claims (Truth Wave) and the guarantee/saved-time reconciliation (PR #328) should be resolved before outreach points skeptical, liability-conscious prospects at the site.
