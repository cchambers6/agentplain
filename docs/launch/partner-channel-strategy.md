# agentplain Partner & Channel Strategy

**Owner:** Conner · **Status:** v1, 2026-06-14 · **Audience:** internal
**Positioning anchor:** agentplain is the service layer that makes Claude usable for local businesses — "built on Claude, configured by us." We partner *with* the ecosystem, never position against Claude/Anthropic.

---

## 0. Why channel, and why now

Direct outbound is slow and we have **zero social proof today** (no logos, no named customers, no testimonials on any public surface). Partnerships solve both: they borrow trust we don't yet have, and they put us in front of pre-qualified, vertical-clustered buyers. The constraint: we can only credibly partner where the **killer workflow is live** — real estate, CPA, law, and the general on-ramp today; home services landing; the rest waitlisted. **Do not build a partner motion ahead of a live workflow** — it generates demand we route to a waitlist, which burns the partner relationship.

A second hard rule from the product architecture: we are **no-outbound** and **coopetition-friendly**. We don't replace a broker's CRM, a CPA's practice tool, or a law firm's case system — we sit on top and draft. That makes vertical-SaaS vendors potential *partners*, not just competitors.

## 2. Outreach playbook by partner type

### 2.1 Trade body / association
- **Entry:** state/local chapter first (reachable, runs vendor showcases), then national.
- **Ask:** member-benefit listing, vendor-directory placement, a webinar/CPE/CLE session, conference sponsorship.
- **Hook:** "a member benefit that gives your {{agents/CPAs/attorneys}} their evenings back" — local-business framing, not "AI."
- **Cycle:** long (3–9 months for national; 4–8 weeks for local chapters).

### 2.2 Influencer / coach / media
- **Entry:** warm, value-first — give them free access, let them experience the workflow.
- **Ask:** sponsored content, an honest review, an affiliate/referral arrangement.
- **Hook:** their audience trusts them on tooling; we give them a genuinely useful thing to recommend + an affiliate cut.
- **Cycle:** fast (weeks). Highest ROI early because we lack social proof — borrow theirs.

### 2.3 Vertical SaaS (coopetition / integration)
- **Entry:** developer/partnerships contact; lead with "we complement, we don't compete."
- **Ask:** integration listing in their marketplace, mutual referral, co-marketing.
- **Hook:** we make their platform stickier (we draft *from* their data); they extend our reach.
- **Cycle:** medium; gated on the integration actually existing (FUB, TaxDome, Karbon have surfaces today).

### 2.4 Franchise / broker network
- **Entry:** regional/team level, never start at corporate.
- **Ask:** a pilot with 1–2 offices, then network-wide rollout.
- **Hook:** consistency + time-savings across locations, one configuration.
- **Cycle:** long, but lands many seats at once.

### 2.5 Community
- **Entry:** participate authentically before pitching; sponsor where allowed.
- **Ask:** AMA, sponsored thread, founder presence.
- **Hook:** be useful in the community first; the product sells itself to a warmed room.

## 3. Term templates

> All terms are **drafts for Conner + counsel**. Nothing is signed without legal review. Keep terms simple and reversible early — we're building proof, not locking ourselves in.

### 3.1 Co-marketing agreement (lightweight)
- Mutual logo use + a joint piece of content (webinar, blog, case study).
- No money changes hands; each party promotes to their audience.
- 6–12 month term, 30-day termination either side.
- Best first step with associations and SaaS partners — low commitment, builds the relationship.

### 3.2 Referral agreement
- **20% of first-year subscription revenue** for referred customers who convert and stay past the money-back window.
- Tracked via referral code / attribution.
- Paid quarterly, clawback if the customer refunds inside the guarantee window.
- Cap nothing in year one — we want partners motivated.

### 3.3 Integration MOU (vertical SaaS)
- Defines the integration scope (read-only data access through their API/MCP), data handling, and the no-outbound boundary.
- Mutual marketplace listing + co-marketing.
- Non-exclusive — we integrate with their competitors too (portability is our north star).
- No revenue share at MOU stage; revisit once volume justifies it.

## 4. When to hire a partner manager

Not yet. The trigger is **proof + volume**, not ambition.

**Hire a partner manager when:**
- We have ≥3 signed/active partnerships generating a steady referral trickle, AND
- We have ≥10 paying customers with at least 2–3 public case studies (social proof to make the partner pitch land), AND
- Conner is spending >25% of his time on partner relationships and it's bottlenecking either product or sales.

Until then, Conner owns partnerships directly. A partner manager hired before we have proof has nothing to sell.

Start the partnership conversation and marketplace-listing groundwork now so we activate the moment estimate-followup goes runtime-live — turning a lag into a same-day launch instead of a standing start.
