A

Pure heritage wordmark — no mark

McKinsey-class restraint. The wordmark carries the whole brand; the Plaino character carries the warmth. Strength: survives any size, never competes with Plaino's avatar, ages well. Weakness: no glanceable mark for favicons or social avatars — would need a separate monogram for square containers.

B

Wordmark + horizon line

A short hairline rule above the wordmark — the prairie reduced to its purest gesture. Geometric, calm, abstract. Strength: reads as "the plains" without being literal; sits quietly next to Plaino without arguing for attention. Weakness: the rule vanishes below 24px — at favicon size the wordmark stands alone, so essentially collapses into Candidate A.

C

Wordmark + wheat sheaf accent

Three hand-drawn stalks of grain to the left of the wordmark — literal heartland iconography in the same hairline language as the rest of the surface. Strength: the most recognizable "rooted" mark; works as a standalone favicon; signals heritage at first glance. Weakness: at 16px the kernels turn to mush; risks reading as "farm-tech" rather than "service partner for brokerages."

D

Wordmark + lone-tree accent

A single tree on a hairline horizon — the lone landmark on the prairie. Conner's earlier top three included this direction. Strength: distinctive silhouette that holds up at favicon size; reads as "rooted" without being agricultural. Weakness: the canopy can read as a balloon or pin at very small sizes; needs the horizon line to anchor it.

E

Plaino-as-mark + wordmark

The named character IS the logo — same line-art figure from PlainoAvatar.tsx, anchored to the left of the wordmark. Strength: the most personable; ties the brand to the character; the Plaino avatar already exists across onboarding and email signatures. Weakness: this is the direction Conner rejected as "too cartoonish for a professional AI consulting logo." The Geico-gecko model argues Plaino should stay warmth on top of a serious wordmark, not be the wordmark itself. Shown here so the rejection is documented next to the alternatives.

F

Wordmark + plowed-rows accent

Furrows converging to a vanishing point — the prairie in single-point perspective. Geometric, structural, suggests order without an animal or plant in sight. Strength: abstract enough to feel professional, literal enough to feel rooted; the geometry holds up at small sizes better than wheat heads. Weakness: low immediate recognition — reads as a graphic device before it reads as "plowed fields"; benefits from the wordmark next to it to disambiguate.
