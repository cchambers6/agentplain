> **Channel note:** LinkedIn skews lower for trades — many owners and office managers aren't daily LinkedIn users. Run this as **owner-targeting** (the people who run the shop, who do keep a profile), and expect **Meta and Google to outperform** on volume and CPA for this vertical. LinkedIn earns its place for higher-deal multi-crew firms and roll-ups, where the buyer is more likely to live here.

**Honesty spine:** the fleet drafts; the owner approves and sends. Live integrations = email + Google Calendar + QuickBooks. Never imply auto-send. No price quoted in-creative beyond the published $199/seat.

## Targeting

- **Job titles / function:** Owner, Co-Owner, President, General Manager, Operations Manager, Office Manager, Business Manager.
- **Industry:** Construction; Building Materials; Consumer Services (use the closest trades-aligned categories LinkedIn offers).
- **Sub-trade signal (via skills/groups/interests):** roofing, HVAC, plumbing, electrical, general contracting; restoration; storm/insurance-restoration groups.
- **Company size:** 11–200 employees (multi-crew shops and small roll-ups — the firms where an owner keeps an active LinkedIn and the deal size justifies the channel CPA). Smaller solo shops are better reached on Meta/Google.
- **Geography:** layer storm-active and high-construction metros first.
- **Exclude:** job-seekers; students; consumers/homeowners.
