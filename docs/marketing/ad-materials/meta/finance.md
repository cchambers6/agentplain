# Meta Ads — finance (financial advisors / RIAs)

**Vertical:** `ria` · **Tier:** Max (quote-based) · Partner floor ($299 → $199/seat)
**Channel intent:** lower than search. Meta is interrupt, not intent — these advisors aren't searching, they're scrolling. Hook on the felt pain (a week of evenings on quarterly letters), not on the product.

> **Policy flag — read before drafting any variant.** Meta enforces financial-services advertising rules. Drafts must **never imply guaranteed returns, performance, or investment outcomes.** We have an easy out: agentplain doesn't make return claims at all — the fleet renders no dollar figure and states no investment view; every number is an `{{advisor: …}}` merge field the advisor fills. Keep that the spine of every ad and the policy risk stays near zero. Never show a rising chart, a portfolio balance, or a "+%".

> **Ground truth:** `CREATIVE_PACK_GROUND_TRUTH.md` §4 (finance) + voice scenario 23. The fleet **drafts; the advisor approves and sends.** Live integrations = email (Gmail/Outlook) + Google Calendar + QuickBooks only. No Orion/Redtail/custodian claims.

## Image-direction brief

**The feeling: a real practice, restrained and trustworthy.** This buyer is compliance-paranoid; the imagery has to read as *sober and credible*, not tech-hype.

**Shoot / source:**
- A real advisor's desk at dusk — a printed client letter, a fountain pen, a coffee gone cold. The "week of evenings" made literal.
- A heritage independent-advisory office: wood, warm light, a handshake at a small table. Local, established, human.
- A single drafted client letter on screen with a visible blank `{{ }}` merge field where a dollar figure would go — the honesty spine, shown.

**Hard NO:**
- No stock "charts going up," no green arrows, no portfolio balances, no "+14%".
- No robots, no glowing-brain AI clichés, no neon.
- No models in suits high-fiving. No dollar signs raining.
- Nothing that could read as a performance or returns promise (Meta policy + our own rules).

**Treatment:** muted, warm, documentary. Plaino, if shown, is small and calm — the 8-bit mark, never a "perky AI" character.

## Story format (vertical, 15s)

- **0–3s:** Text over the dusk-desk shot — `The quarterly letters take a week of your evenings.`
- **3–8s:** Cut to the drafted letter on screen, merge field blank — `What if they were drafted overnight?`
- **8–12s:** Pen signing — `Every number stays yours to fill. You approve and send.`
- **12–15s:** agentplain mark + Plaino — `Built on Claude. Run by us. Talk to a service partner.` CTA sticker: **Learn More**

> No motion-graphic charts. Calm cuts, no swooshes. VO optional; if used, match the YouTube VO direction (calm, fiduciary).

## Audience targeting

**Lookalikes (strongest):**
- 1–3% lookalike of scoping-call bookers / trial signups (RIA source list).
- Lookalike of any uploaded RIA-principal CRM list (hashed).

**Interest stacks (narrow the cold prophase):**
- Job titles / interests: *Registered Investment Advisor, CFP, CFA, independent financial advisor, wealth management, financial planning practice.*
- Publications/groups: *Financial Planning Association, NAPFA, XY Planning Network, ThinkAdvisor, Citywire RIA, Kitces.*
- Behaviors: small-business owners + the above advisor interests (catches principals of small RIAs).

**Exclusions (load-bearing):**
- Exclude **retail investors / consumers** — interests like "investing," "stock market," "personal finance," "Robinhood," "day trading."
- Exclude career/exam seekers ("CFP exam," "finance jobs").
- Exclude existing customers and current trial signups.

> Keep audiences narrow and let lookalikes carry scale. This is a quote-based Max buyer — Meta's job is to *surface* the pain and route warm advisors to a scoping call, not to close.
