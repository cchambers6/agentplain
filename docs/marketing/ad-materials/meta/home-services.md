## Image-direction brief

Heritage, grounded, real. Shoot or source like you'd photograph a shop that's been around 20 years — not a tech ad.

- **Yes:** a real work truck backed up to a job site at golden hour; a roof tear-off mid-job with a crew on it; the back office of a trades shop — a worn desk, a landline, a job binder, an estimate on a clipboard; an owner in a company tee at a parts counter; a phone showing an estimate, in a calloused hand.
- **No:** stock hardhats-and-thumbs-up; suited "business people shaking hands"; glowing robots, circuit-board brains, holograms, blue-gradient AI clichés; confetti; anything that looks like a SaaS landing page.
- **Tone:** muted, real-world color. Dust, daylight, plain backgrounds. Plaino (the 8-bit dog mark) may sit small in a corner as the brand signature — never the hero of the photo.
- **Text overlay:** at most one short line, same as the headline. Keep under Meta's text-heavy threshold.

## Audience targeting

**Goal:** reach the owner/office manager of a trades firm. Trades owners are reachable on Meta (they live on Facebook for local-business networking) — this is a stronger channel for this vertical than LinkedIn.

- **Interest stacks (narrow with AND where possible):**
  - Roofing contractor / HVAC contractor / plumbing business / electrical contractor / general contractor (business-owner interests)
  - Trade associations + publications (e.g., roofing/HVAC trade groups, contractor business pages)
  - Small-business-owner + QuickBooks / contractor business software interest as a stacking signal
- **Lookalikes:** 1–3% lookalike off the trial-start + talk-to-a-partner pixel events once seeded; off any owner email list if available.
- **Behaviors:** small business owners; admins of business pages in construction/home-improvement.

**Exclusions (hard — enforce the shop-not-consumer rule):**
- Exclude "homeowner," "home improvement DIY," "home repair," and home-buying/renovation-shopper audiences.
- Exclude job-seeker / "jobs in construction" intent.
- Exclude anyone who engaged as a *consumer* looking to hire a contractor.

> If consumer leakage shows in comments ("how much to fix my roof?"), tighten interest-AND stacks and add the leaking audience to exclusions. We sell to the shop, not its customers — every dollar spent reaching a homeowner is wasted.
