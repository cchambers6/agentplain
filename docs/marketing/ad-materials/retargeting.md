# Retargeting Copy — Outbound Creative Pack

**Branch:** `marketing/ad-and-dtc-materials-pack-2026-06-14` · **Canon:** `CREATIVE_PACK_GROUND_TRUTH.md` + `brand-voice-scenario-library.md`

Five distinct retargeting audiences. Every line stays inside the honesty spine: the fleet
**drafts and proposes; you approve and send.** Nothing leaves without your name on it. One
CTA per ad. No "instant/real-time AI" — drafts land in your queue on a cadence. Lowercase
`agentplain`, capitalize `Plaino`. First month free (never "pilot"). Live integrations claimed:
email (Gmail/Outlook) + Calendar + QuickBooks (+ realty: DocuSign/Drive) only.

CTA verbs map to two real events: **Start free trial** (`/app/sign-up`) and **Talk to a service
partner** (talk-to-Plaino).

---

## Audience 1 — Visited /pricing, didn't sign up

**Definition.** Saw the price, weighed it, left without starting a trial.
**Why they bounced.** The objection is value, not money: *"$99 a seat — but will it actually
save me time, or is this one more thing I have to babysit?"*
**The single job of the ad.** Answer the worth-it question with a concrete ROI number and the
draft-then-approve control — they keep the wheel, the fleet does the typing.

### Variant A (Meta / Google display)
- **Headline (≤40):** What $99 a seat actually buys back
- **Primary text:** A broker we talked through the math with was losing 8–12 coordination hours
  a week. The fleet drafts the lead replies, the invoice chases, the overnight summary — and
  lands them in your queue. You approve and send. That's the time back, with your name still on
  every send.
- **CTA:** Start free trial

### Variant B (Meta / Google display)
- **Headline (≤40):** You stay the only one who hits send
- **Primary text:** The worry with AI is that it does something you didn't sign off on. Ours
  can't. The fleet drafts the routine work and stops at your approvals queue — every item is
  pending until you decide. First month free, month-to-month, cancel anytime.
- **CTA:** Start free trial

**Email / SMS-safe nudge:** You looked at the price — here's the other half: a real-estate
shop reclaimed about 8–12 hours a week, and you still approve every send. First month free.

## Audience 2 — Started signup, didn't complete

**Definition.** Began the trial flow, stopped before finishing.
**Why they bounced.** Friction and cold feet — a card field, the "what am I connecting?" pause,
the quiet worry that an AI is about to act on their behalf.
**The single job of the ad.** Lower the temperature: first month free, you stay the only one
who hits send, and connecting takes two buttons.

### Variant A (Meta / Google display)
- **Headline (≤40):** Two buttons to connect. That's it.
- **Primary text:** You were a step away. Connecting is one button for email, one for calendar —
  the fleet starts reading what's already in your inbox and drafts the routine replies into your
  queue. Nothing goes out until you approve it. First month free.
- **CTA:** Start free trial

### Variant B (Meta / Google display)
- **Headline (≤40):** Nothing sends without your say-so
- **Primary text:** If the hesitation was "what's it going to do on its own" — the answer is
  nothing. The fleet drafts and proposes; you approve and send, from your own inbox. Pick up
  where you left off. First month free, cancel anytime.
- **CTA:** Start free trial

**Email / SMS-safe nudge:** You were one step in. First month's free, connecting is two buttons,
and you stay the only one who hits send. Finish when you've got a minute.

## Audience 3 — Trial users, no approved draft in first 48h

**Definition.** Signed up, but haven't opened the approvals queue or approved anything yet.
**Why they're quiet.** They haven't seen the value land — the drafts are sitting there waiting,
unseen.
**The single job of the ad.** Get them to open the queue. Reassure that opening it commits to
nothing — approval is a separate, deliberate step.

### Variant A (Meta / Google display)
- **Headline (≤40):** Your queue has drafts waiting
- **Primary text:** The fleet has been drafting into your approvals queue since you connected.
  None of it has gone anywhere — nothing sends until you say so. Open the queue, read one draft,
  approve it or tell it what's off. That's where the fleet learns your voice.
- **CTA:** Start free trial

### Variant B (Meta / Google display)
- **Headline (≤40):** Opening the queue sends nothing
- **Primary text:** Worried that clicking in means something fires off? It doesn't. Every item is
  pending until you approve it — reading is free. Take two minutes, look at what the fleet drafted,
  and keep the ones that are right.
- **CTA:** Start free trial

**Email / SMS-safe nudge:** There are drafts in your queue waiting on you — and nothing sends
until you approve it. Open it, read one, see if it's right.

## Audience 4 — Trial users, day 10–12 (conversion push)

**Definition.** Active trial, two to four days from the card charge. Gentle conversion window.
**Why now.** They've seen some value; they need a calm reason to keep going, not a countdown.
**The single job of the ad.** Anchor to the ROI they've started to feel, restate the terms, no
pressure spam.

### Variant A (Meta / Google display)
- **Headline (≤40):** Your trial ends Friday
- **Primary text:** No action needed to continue — your first paid month starts after, $99 a seat
  on Regular, month-to-month, cancel anytime, 14-day money-back guarantee behind it. If the fleet
  has been earning its seat in your queue, just keep going.
- **CTA:** Start free trial

### Variant B (Meta / Google display)
- **Headline (≤40):** A few hours a week, back
- **Primary text:** A week and a half in, the question is simple: is the fleet drafting the work
  that used to eat your mornings? If it is, that's the case. Month-to-month, cancel anytime. If
  something's still not pulling its weight, tell us before the trial ends and we'll fix it.
- **CTA:** Talk to a service partner

**Email / SMS-safe nudge:** Trial ends Friday — first paid month is $99 a seat, month-to-month,
cancel anytime. Nothing to do to keep going.

## Audience 5 — Churned customers, 90-day winback

**Definition.** Cancelled roughly three months ago. Low-pressure re-touch.
**Why the re-touch is earned.** We have something concrete to show them — a gap they hit is now
closed. No "we miss you."
**The single job of the ad.** Name what shipped since they left, offer first month free again,
respect their prior decision.

### Variant A (Meta / Google display)
- **Headline (≤40):** The QuickBooks chase you wanted is live
- **Primary text:** When you left, automatic invoice chasing tied to your books was the gap. It's
  working now — the fleet reads your overdue invoices and drafts a chase for each, escalating in
  tone as they age. You still approve every send. First month free to take another look.
- **CTA:** Start free trial

### Variant B (Meta / Google display)
- **Headline (≤40):** A few things changed since you left
- **Primary text:** No countdown, no win-back theater. We've shipped real work since you cancelled —
  including the books-tied invoice chasing you'd asked about. Your old setup is a click away. First
  month free again if you want to see where it's at.
- **CTA:** Talk to a service partner

**Email / SMS-safe nudge:** The invoice-chase gap you left over is closed. First month free again
if you want another look — no pressure, no countdown.
