# agentplain Brand-Voice Scenario Library

The canonical voice reference. Every customer-facing word agentplain ships — emails,
in-app copy, support replies, Plaino's own chat — is written against this file. So is
every draft the fleet produces inside a customer workspace. When a writer (human or
agent) is unsure how something should sound, this is the answer.

Each scenario carries an **On-brand** example and a **Don't write** counter-example.
The bad version is not a strawman — it is the realistic, plausible draft someone
ships when they forget the canon. Read both. The gap between them *is* the voice.

---

## How to use this

- **Writing something net-new?** Find the closest scenario, copy the on-brand shape,
  swap in the specifics. Don't reach for adjectives — reach for the concrete fact.
- **Reviewing a draft?** Run it against the banned-word list and the cadence rules
  below. If it trips one, it's a rewrite, not a tweak.
- **Building a fleet skill or prompt?** The per-vertical scenarios (19–23) are the
  reference for how a *drafted* email must sound and what it must never assert. The
  no-outbound and merge-field rules there are load-bearing — they are compliance, not
  style.
- **Disagree with an example?** Good. File it. This document is versioned and ratified;
  it changes by decision, not by drift.

## Voice in one screen

**Who's talking.** A heritage local-business partner, owner to owner. Calm, plain,
concrete, confident, warm — never chirpy. Plaino (our named AI partner) talks the
same way: grounded, never a perky chatbot. We do real work for real businesses and
we say so plainly.

**Do**
- Short declarative sentences. One idea each.
- Specifics over adjectives. "Drafts land in your queue every five minutes" beats
  "lightning-fast automation."
- Honest about limits. If a thing isn't connected yet, say so.
- Lead with what the reader gets, then how it works.
- Name the human's control: *the fleet drafts and proposes; you approve and send.*
- Lowercase **agentplain**, always. The AI partner is **Plaino**.
- Call the unit **the fleet** / **capable AI partners** — never a number of agents.

**Don't**
- No hype adjectives, no exclamation-point spam, no emoji confetti.
- Never promise the fleet *sends*, *pays*, *files*, or *commits* anything. It drafts.
- Never say we replace a team, replace a person, or "automate everything."
- Never position against Claude/ChatGPT as a competitor or "alternative." We're the
  service that runs the AI for you. ("The easy way to actually use Claude" is fine.)
- Never call the audience "SMB," "knowledge workers," or "white-collar workers."
- Never expose internal state words to customers: not "rooting," "live,"
  "schema-only." Use **Setting up / Working / Watching**.

**Banned words and phrases** (these are exactly what the Don't-write examples use)
> supercharge · unlock · transform · revolutionize · revolutionary · seamless ·
> leverage · empower · game-changer · 10x · magic · AI magic · cutting-edge ·
> next-gen · robust · synergy · delight · effortless · "automate everything" ·
> "replace your team" · "AI assistant" · "exciting news!" · "we're thrilled" ·
> "reach out" · "circle back" · "at the end of the day" · emoji-laden hype ·
> exclamation-point spam

**Cadence rules**
- Subject lines: lowercase-leaning, plain, ≤ 8 words, no exclamation point, no emoji.
  Say what the email is about, not how excited we are.
- Open on the reader or the fact, not on ourselves. Not "We're thrilled to…" —
  "Your trial ends Friday."
- One CTA per message where you can manage it. Make it a verb the reader does next.
- Sign as a person or as Plaino. Plaino never claims to be human and never over-apologizes.
- Numbers and nouns over modifiers. Cut any sentence that survives intact when you
  delete its adjectives — the adjectives were doing the lying.

**Brand facts you'll reuse**
- Tagline: *Intelligence rooted in reality.*
- Mission: *We lift up local businesses by doing the work that takes their time and
  money away from the people they serve.*
- Pricing: Regular $199 → $99/seat · Partner $299 → $199/seat · Max quoted ·
  Custom $5K–$15K. First month free, month-to-month, cancel anytime.
- Trial: card at signup; 7-day default; **14-day for CPA and Law**; 14-day money-back guarantee.
- Support: Regular/Partner → hello@agentplain.com. Founder/named-partner time is
  Max/Custom only. Never the phrase "pilot pricing."

## Scenarios

### 1. Welcome email (new signup)
**Context:** Sent the moment someone signs up and starts their free trial.
**On-brand:**
> **Subject:** your agentplain workspace is ready
>
> Welcome. Your workspace is set up and the fleet is getting to know your business.
>
> Here's what happens next. Connect your email and calendar from the Integrations page
> — that's one button each. The fleet starts reading what's already in your inbox,
> drafts the routine replies, and lands them in your approvals queue. Nothing goes out
> until you approve it. You stay the only one who hits send.
>
> Your first month is free. Take a look at what shows up in your queue over the next
> few days, and tell us where it's off — that's how it learns your voice.
>
> Questions any time: hello@agentplain.com.
>
> — The agentplain team

**Don't write:**
> **Subject:** 🎉 Welcome to agentplain — let's supercharge your business!
>
> We're thrilled to have you on board! 🚀 Get ready to transform the way you work.
> Our cutting-edge AI assistant will automate everything so you can finally ditch the
> busywork. Just connect your tools and watch the magic happen — it's that seamless!
>
> Can't wait to see you unlock your potential. Let's do this! 💪

**Why:** The good version opens on the reader, states what happens in concrete steps,
and names the no-outbound control. The bad one is wall-to-wall banned words
(supercharge, thrilled, transform, cutting-edge, AI assistant, automate everything,
magic, seamless, unlock), emoji confetti, and exclamation spam — and it over-promises
autonomy the architecture never has.

**Don't write:**
> **Subject:** ⏰ Don't miss out!! Your trial is ENDING!
>
> Time's running out to unlock the full power of agentplain! Don't let this
> game-changing opportunity slip away. Upgrade now to keep the magic going and
> supercharge your workflow before it's too late!!! Act fast! 🔥

**Why:** Good version states the fact (trial ends tomorrow), reassures on terms, and
gives one concrete next step. Bad version is fear-of-missing-out theater with banned
words and exclamation spam, and it sells urgency instead of value.

### 6. Failed payment
**Context:** A card charge failed; the account needs a payment update to avoid interruption.
**On-brand:**
> **Subject:** your payment didn't go through
>
> Your last payment didn't clear — usually an expired card or a bank hold, not anything
> on your end. Your workspace is still running for now.
>
> Update your card from Settings → Billing and the charge will retry on its own. If it
> keeps failing, reply here and we'll sort it out with you. We won't cut anything off
> without telling you first.

**Don't write:**
> **Subject:** ⚠️ ACTION REQUIRED: Payment Failed!
>
> Uh oh! 😬 Your payment failed and your access is at risk!! Don't let billing issues
> derail your momentum. Update now to keep your game-changing workflow seamless and
> uninterrupted. We'd hate to see you go!

**Why:** Good version is calm, assumes good faith ("not anything on your end"), gives
the fix, and promises no surprise cutoff. Bad version is alarmist, uses emoji panic and
banned words, and makes a routine billing event feel like a threat.

turns a cancellation into a hype pitch.

**Don't write:**
> **Subject:** We miss you! Come back and let's reconnect 🥺
>
> It's been too long! We've been busy transforming the platform with tons of exciting
> new features. Come back and rediscover the magic — we're thrilled to welcome you home
> and supercharge your business all over again! Special win-back offer inside! 🎁

**Why:** Good version earns the re-touch with a specific, true reason (the exact gap
they left over is now closed) and respects the prior decision. Bad version is generic
"we miss you" sentiment with banned words and no concrete reason to return.

> supercharge your decision-making. It's seamless, it's powerful, it's a total

(counts only, never quotes a draft), and asks for feedback. Bad version is a banned-word
pileup that never actually says what the feature does.

### 10. Outage / incident notice
**Context:** Something is degraded or down; customers need a calm, factual status update.
**On-brand:**
> **Subject:** drafting is delayed right now
>
> We're seeing delays in the fleet's drafting since about 9:15 AM ET. Your data is safe
> and nothing has been sent — the queue is just slower to fill than usual. We've found
> the cause and we're working on it now.
>
> We'll send an update by 11:00 ET, sooner if it's resolved. Nothing you need to do.
> Sorry for the lag.

**Don't write:**
> **Subject:** Minor hiccup — but we're on it! 💪
>
> Hey there! Just a tiny blip on our end — no biggie! 😅 Our robust systems are super
> resilient and we'll have everything back to seamless in no time. Thanks for your
> patience, rockstars! We've got this! 🙌

**Why:** Good version gives a timestamp, the impact, the safety status, and a next-update
commitment — the things a worried customer needs. Bad version minimizes a real problem
with chirpy filler, calls customers "rockstars," and offers no facts or timeline.

## Appendix: the five quick tests

Before any customer-facing copy ships, run it through these:

1. **The banned-word scan.** Search the draft against the list above. Any hit is a
   rewrite. (Exception: a banned *word* used in its plain, specific sense rather than as
   hype — see scenario 36's note on "unlock." When unsure, swap it.)
2. **The autonomy test.** Does any sentence imply the fleet sends, pays, files, books,
   or commits something? If so, it's wrong — the fleet drafts and proposes; the owner
   approves and sends.
3. **The adjective-delete test.** Delete every adjective. If the sentence still says
   what you meant, the adjectives were lying. Put back only the ones carrying real
   information.
4. **The vertical-discipline test** (for drafted emails): no rendered dollar figures for
   RIA, no quoted price for home services, no tax position for CPA, no legal conclusion
   for law, no premium/binding date for insurance, no rate/APR/DTI for mortgage. Every
   such claim is a merge field.
5. **The across-the-counter test.** Would you say it, in these words, to a business
   owner standing across the counter from you? If not, rewrite it until you would.
