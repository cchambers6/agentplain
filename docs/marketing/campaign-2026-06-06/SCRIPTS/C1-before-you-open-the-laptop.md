## Voiceover guide

- **Talent:** one voice, mid-40s, warm baritone or low alto. Reads like a trusted colleague,
  not an announcer. Think "the broker everyone in the office trusts," not radio-ad energy.
- **Pace:** unhurried. Leave the 9:14pm beat to breathe. The product is calm; the read is calm.
- **Tone words:** grounded, dry, quietly confident. Never chirpy, never hype. No exclamation
  in the delivery even where copy has energy.
- **Banned in read:** "magic," "revolutionary," "game-changer," "effortless," anything that
  implies the AI *sends* or *decides*. Say "drafts," "proposes," "you sign," "you send."
- **Key stressed words:** "*while you slept*," "*your* voice," "*your* name," "*four* minutes."

## Storyboard (12 frames)

1. **9:14pm, nightstand.** Phone face-up, glowing: "Counter-offer received." Bedroom dark, warm.
2. **The pull-back.** A hand reaches for the phone, hesitates, withdraws. Decision: not tonight.
3. **Phone face-down.** Screen goes dark. Composition settles into stillness.
4. **Time dissolve.** Clock/UI clock morphs 9:14pm → 6:30am. Light shifts from blue to gold.
5. **Morning kitchen, wide.** Sarah in a robe, pouring coffee. Ordinary, real, lived-in home.
6. **Laptop opens.** Over-shoulder: the agentplain workspace, already populated — not a blank box.
7. **Draft reveal.** The drafted counter-response fills the panel; subtle highlight as it loads.
8. **Comps reveal.** Three comparable closings slide in beside the draft.
9. **Thread summary.** A tidy summary of the buyer's-agent history; the chaos made legible.
10. **The one edit + Plaino marker.** Close on cursor changing a single number; footer:
    *drafted by Plaino.*
11. **Send from dotloop.** Cut to her own transaction system; she signs and sends. Confirmation
    shows *her* name.
12. **End card.** Laptop closing, coffee in hand. Wordmark on paper, ten verticals beneath,
    "Intelligence rooted in reality.", CTA "Start free trial."

## Production brief

- **Talent:** one principal — a credible 40s real-estate professional (not a model). Wardrobe:
  soft, real morning clothes; no power-suit. Hands matter (the edit/sign beats) — cast for
  natural hands or use an insert double.
- **Location feel:** a real, lived-in suburban/small-city home kitchen. Warm wood, paper,
  morning light through a real window. *Not* a glass-and-steel loft. This is "the plains" —
  grounded, Midwestern/Southern Main-Street America. Reinforces "rooted in reality."
- **Art direction / UI:** the workspace on screen must be the *real* agentplain UI (paper/ink/
  clay tokens, Instrument Serif) — shoot screen captures of the actual product, composite in
  post. Plaino footer marker per `components/ui/ap/PlainoAvatar.tsx`. No fake sci-fi UI, no
  glowing-orb "AI" cliché.
- **Music:** sparse, acoustic, warm — single piano or fingerpicked guitar, low and steady.
  Builds almost imperceptibly. No EDM, no corporate-uplift stab. Heritage, not coastal.
- **On-screen text:** Instrument Serif for the wordmark/end card; the brand's mono for the small
  labels. Keep text minimal — the scene carries it.
- **Color:** warm, filmic, slightly desaturated. Blue-hour → golden-hour shift is the visual
  spine (night-anxiety → morning-calm).
- **Aspect ratios:** shoot 16:9 hero (YouTube) + protect 9:16 (Meta/Reels) and 1:1 framing.
- **Mandatory legal/voice checks:** no claim the fleet sends; "first month free" not "pilot";
  ROI figure on-screen cites the 26x/$5,300 anchor; end card names ≥the vertical breadth via
  the ghosted vertical row (page-one-verticals rule).
