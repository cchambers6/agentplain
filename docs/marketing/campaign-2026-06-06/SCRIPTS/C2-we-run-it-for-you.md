## Voiceover guide

- **Talent:** measured, credible, late-40s/50s. The voice of a senior partner or a trusted CPA
  peer. Dry authority. Reads like someone who has *done* a tax season, not someone selling one.
- **Pace:** deliberate. This persona buys on trust and proof; rushing reads as hype. Let the
  "it drafts — it does not send" line land hard and slow.
- **Tone words:** precise, sober, reassuring. The emotional payload is *relief* and *control*,
  not excitement.
- **Banned in read:** "magic," "effortless," "10x your firm overnight," anything implying the AI
  files, sends, or advises clients directly. Say "drafts," "you review," "you sign," "your
  signature."
- **Key stressed words:** "*runs it for you*," "*it drafts — it does not send*," "*your
  signature, every time*," "*show our work*."

## Storyboard (11 frames)

1. **The blank box.** Tight on a blinking cursor: "How can I help?" A tired CPA's face reflected.
2. **The struggle.** Hands type, delete, type. A clock ticks. The free tool is *work*.
3. **The cut.** Same desk, exhale. agentplain workspace opens — populated, ordered.
4. **The partner appears.** A brief, real human moment — the service partner in a review frame.
   Establishes "run for you."
5. **March 17, 5:42pm.** Wall clock + pipeline view: 23 files, each with its specific gap.
6. **The drafting.** 23 emails generate, each citing the exact missing doc.
7. **The skip.** Four extension clients grey out — the fleet knew not to chase them.
8. **The review tray.** Partner approves/edits fast; *drafted by Plaino* footers; "19 in 35 min."
9. **The control beat.** A held frame on a single line: "Nothing sends until you sign."
10. **The payoff.** March 18 noon, "17 of 23 responded"; partner on an advisory call, relaxed.
11. **End card.** Wordmark, ten verticals, compliance line, "Intelligence rooted in reality.",
    CTA "Talk to a service partner."

## Production brief

- **Talent:** one principal CPA (credible, 45–60, not a model) + one brief appearance of the
  "service partner" (warm, competent, business-casual — represents the agentplain human team /
  Plaino-as-people). Avoid robot/mascot literalism; Plaino appears only as the small UI footer
  marker, not a costumed character.
- **Location feel:** a real small-firm office — wood desk, paper files, a window onto a normal
  street. Tax-season texture: a stack of folders, a coffee, sticky notes. Grounded, not glassy.
  Heritage Main-Street America, consistent with "the plains."
- **Art direction / UI:** real agentplain UI captured from the product (pipeline view, review
  tray, Plaino footer). The empty-chatbot box at the open should read as a *generic* tool —
  deliberately sterile and blank — to contrast with the populated, ordered agentplain workspace.
- **Music:** restrained and warm; a single sustained piano motif. The shift from
  open-frustration to populated-calm is carried by the *removal* of tension, not a beat drop.
- **On-screen text:** Instrument Serif wordmark/end card; mono for labels and the proof line.
  The proof figures ($42k, 12x) must match the citation; the compliance end line says "built
  around the rules that govern your work" — never "guarantees compliance."
- **Color:** clean, warm, slightly desaturated. The generic-tool open is cooler/flatter; the
  agentplain world is warmer.
- **Aspect ratios:** 16:9 hero (YouTube/LinkedIn) + 1:1 and 9:16 protected. A LinkedIn-native
  square cut with captions-on is mandatory (most P2 LinkedIn viewing is sound-off).
- **Mandatory checks:** name the alternative honestly (Claude for Small Business / a free
  chatbot) as *useful but different*, never disparaged; the control/no-send message must be
  unmistakable; "first month free," never "pilot."
