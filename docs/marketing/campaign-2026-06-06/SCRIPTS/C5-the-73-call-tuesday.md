## Voiceover guide

- **Talent:** plain-spoken, no-nonsense, 40s–50s. Sounds like an owner who's been on a roof —
  or like the guy other owners trust at the supply counter. Slight regional warmth (Southern /
  Midwestern) fits "the plains." *Not* a polished announcer; not coastal-tech.
- **Pace:** direct, conversational, a little gravel. Confident, never salesy. The line "there's
  one of her and seventy-three of them" should feel like a real observation, not a pitch.
- **Tone words:** straight, grounded, respectful of the work. The hero is the owner, not the AI.
- **Banned in read:** "magic," "seamless," "set it and forget it," anything implying the AI
  dispatches crews or sends to homeowners on its own. Say "drafts," "ranks," "you approve,"
  "you sign," "you dispatch."
- **Key stressed words:** "*seventy-three*," "*none of them drop*," "*you still make the
  calls*," "*you sign it*."

1. **The storm.** Night, hail on a roof, gutters overflowing. "Tuesday, 11:47pm."
2. **The morning flood.** Office manager pinned to the phone, lines blinking, sticky-note chaos.

## Production brief

- **Talent:** one principal — a believable trades owner (work shirt, real hands, weathered, not
  a model) + an office-manager character for the phone-flood scene. Cast for authenticity over
  polish; this audience smells a fake instantly.
- **Location feel:** a real trades shop / yard — trucks, ladders, a cluttered but functional
  office. A real neighborhood with real roofs for the storm-damage beats. Heartland, not
  coastal. The whole film should feel like it was shot in a working business, because the brand
  is "rooted in reality."
- **Art direction / UI:** real agentplain UI captured from the product (lead queue, dispatch/
  route view, supplement-draft view, Plaino footer). The storm and phone-flood are practical/
  filmed; the workspace is composited real screens. No glowing-AI cliché — the contrast is
  *chaos vs. order*, both grounded.
- **Music:** starts tense and percussive under the storm/phone-flood (driving but not EDM),
  resolves to steady and warm when the workspace takes over. The emotional arc is overwhelm →
  control.
- **SFX:** the ringing phones and the counter are key — let the call-volume sound design carry
  the stress. Cut the noise sharply when the workspace appears.
- **On-screen text:** Instrument Serif wordmark/end card; mono for the counter + labels + proof
  line. Proof figures ($50k+, 14x) must match the citation.
- **Color:** storm scenes cold and high-contrast; the solution world warmer. Filmic, a little
  desaturated, never glossy.
- **Aspect ratios:** 16:9 hero (YouTube) + 9:16 (Meta/Reels — this concept's primary buy) + 1:1.
  Build a 15s storm-trigger geo cut (frames 1–4 + 9–10 + end card) for rapid post-storm
  activation.
- **Mandatory checks:** the owner always makes the dispatch/send decision (no autonomous
  outbound); supplement framing says "drafts the rebuttal, you sign," never "wins your claim";
  "first month free," never "pilot."
