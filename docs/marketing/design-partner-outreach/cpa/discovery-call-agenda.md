# CPA Design-Partner — Discovery Call Agenda (30 min)

Facilitator: Conner Chambers, founder. Audience: firm owner or managing partner.

Goal: understand the firm's stack and where the hours go, show how drafts move into
approvals and how the credentialed-approval gate works, and make the design-partner
ask. Sober throughout. Never promise the fleet sends or files anything. ROI is a
model — say so. There are no existing customers; this program is how we find the first.

---

## Open — 2 min

**Talking points**
- Thanks for the time. Keep it to thirty minutes.
- Frame: "I'm not here to pitch a tool you have to drive. agentplain is a service —
  we install it, configure it, and run a monthly review. The fleet drafts; a
  credentialed person at your firm approves before anything files."
- One sentence on why this call exists: "We're choosing a small cohort of firms to
  build the CPA workflows with. You'd be among the first."

---

## Their stack + pain — 10 min

Listen more than you talk. Goal is to find the one workflow that hurts most.

**Qualifying questions**
- What tax software does the firm run? (Lacerte, UltraTax, Drake, ProConnect, CCH?)
- Are you on QuickBooks Online for your own books and your clients'?
- Walk me through the document chase in March. Who does it, and how?
- How do clients hear about a missing document today — email, portal, phone?
- Roughly how many staff hours a week go to chasing and to close status updates?
- How many returns does the firm file in a season, and how many staff prepare them?
- What's the part of busy season that makes you think "there has to be a better way"?

**Talking points**
- Reflect back the most expensive workflow you heard. Quantify it in their words.
- Note which of the live connectors they already use — email, calendar, QuickBooks.
- If they name a tool we don't connect to yet, say so plainly. Don't claim it.

---

## Demo — drafts into approvals + the approval gate — 10 min

Show the honesty spine in action. This is the differentiator; let it carry the demo.

**Talking points**
- "Here's the missing-document chase the fleet drafts." Show the draft sitting in the
  queue, marked pending.
- "Notice what it does not do: it doesn't send, and it states no tax position. It lists
  the missing items and asks for them." (Point to scenario-20 shape if helpful.)
- "A credentialed person at your firm reads this, edits if needed, and approves. Only
  then does it go out — from your own systems, with your name on it."
- Show the month-end close status update and the QuickBooks AR-aging pulse the same way.
- Be explicit on the boundary: under Circular 230 and IRC §6694, a position that
  understates liability is a $1,000–$5,000 per-return penalty. That liability stays with
  the credentialed preparer — which is exactly why a person approves before anything files.

**Qualifying questions**
- Who at the firm would be the approver? Does that map to how you already work?
- Where would this save the most time first?

---

## Design-partner ask — 5 min

**Talking points**
- "Here's the offer. As a design partner you get agentplain free for three months, with
  direct founder time — me, weekly — during the pilot. Early access to features as they
  ship, and a direct line into what we build next."
- "After the pilot you convert to standard month-to-month pricing for your tier, or you
  don't. No obligation."
- "What we ask back: a weekly thirty-minute call during the pilot, an on-record
  testimonial once you've seen value, and willingness to be a reference for future firms."
- "The cohort is small on purpose — a handful of firms across our verticals — so each
  one gets real attention."
- Be honest: "You'd be among our first CPA partners. That's the trade — early, with
  founder time, in exchange for building it with us."

**Qualifying questions**
- Does a three-month build, with weekly founder time, fit how you'd want to try this?
- Any reason the timing wouldn't work this season?

---

## Next steps — 3 min

**Talking points**
- Recap the one workflow you'd start with.
- Name the connections we'd set up first: email, calendar, QuickBooks.
- Concrete next action: "I'll send a one-page summary and a link to start the workspace.
  If it looks right, we book the kickoff and connect your tools together — about twenty
  minutes."
- Confirm the approver and a target start week.
- Thank them. Leave the door open if it's a "not now."
