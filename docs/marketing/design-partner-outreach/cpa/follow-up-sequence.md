# CPA Design-Partner Follow-Up Sequence

Four emails after the first-contact send. Each varies the angle — no repeated hook.
Sober, factual, signed by Conner Chambers. The fleet drafts; a credentialed person
at the firm approves before anything files.

ROI figures are operator-modeled, not customer-attested. There are no existing
customers — that is why this program exists.

## Email 2 — T+7 days · the §6694 downside

**Subject:** why a person approves every draft

Hi {{first_name}},

One thing I want to be plain about, because it's the whole design.

Under IRC §6694, a preparer position that understates a client's liability carries a
penalty of $1,000 to $5,000 per return, and Circular 230 governs the conduct behind
it. That exposure does not belong to software. So agentplain never files and never
states a tax position. It drafts; a credentialed person at your firm approves before
anything leaves.

The AI does the repetitive drafting. The professional judgment — and the sign-off —
stays with the credentialed preparer. That line is fixed.

Worth a short call to see how the approval gate works?

— Conner Chambers, founder, agentplain
