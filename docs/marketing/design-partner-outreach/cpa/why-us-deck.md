## Slide 2 — The last week of March

- A preparer has six returns open. Four are stalled on a document a client hasn't sent.
- Half the day goes to writing the same chase, tracking who replied, re-checking the list.
- The hours don't go to the return. They go to the work around the return.

*Speaker notes:* Make it a scene they recognize. Don't list features yet. The goal is for
them to nod — "that's exactly my March." Ask which workflow hurts most and listen.

## Slide 3 — What agentplain is

- A done-for-you service: we install it, configure it for your firm, run a monthly review.
- A fleet of AI partners that drafts work into an approvals queue.
- Built for CPA firms specifically — close updates, document chases, onboarding letters.

*Speaker notes:* Distinguish from "another software subscription." We do the setup and the
running. They get drafts to review, not a dashboard to learn. The audience word is "local
businesses" — never SMB.

## Slide 4 — The honesty spine

- The fleet **drafts and proposes**. It does not send, file, pay, or state a tax position.
- Every item sits **pending** until a credentialed person at your firm approves it.
- What you approve goes out from your own systems, with your name on it.

*Speaker notes:* This is the differentiator — spend real time here. Most AI pitches imply
autonomy. Ours is the opposite by design: the approval gate is the product. Say plainly
that the fleet never states a tax position; the credentialed preparer's judgment is
irreplaceable and stays in the loop.

## Slide 8 — The downside we design around

- Circular 230 and IRC §6694 govern preparer conduct.
- A position that understates a client's liability: **$1,000–$5,000 per return.**
- That exposure belongs to a credentialed professional — never to software. So a person
  approves before anything files.

*Speaker notes:* This is why the approval gate exists. The penalty regime is the reason the
fleet drafts and never decides. Frame agentplain as reducing the volume of manual drafting
without ever touching the professional judgment or the sign-off that carries the liability.
