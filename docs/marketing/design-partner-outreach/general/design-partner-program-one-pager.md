## The problem

The admin lives in your head. The inbox you clear at 7pm. The follow-up you meant to send Tuesday. The scheduling shuffle, the same process explained for the fifth time. ## What agentplain does

We install a fleet of AI partners, connect your tools, and run a monthly review. It's a service, not a tool you have to drive.

- **Live workflows on day one:** a Chief of Staff that keeps the week organized, Inbox Triage that sorts and drafts replies, a Follow-Up Chaser that catches threads gone quiet, and a Process-Doc Drafter that writes down what only you know.
- **Tools we connect:** Gmail, Microsoft 365 / Outlook, Google Calendar, QuickBooks (read-only — AR aging, open invoices, month-end), DocuSign, and a generic CRM webhook receiver.
- **The honesty spine — this is the point:** the fleet **drafts and proposes**. Every item lands in your approvals queue as pending. **You approve and send**, from your own inbox. The fleet never sends, files, pays, or commits anything on its own.

**Honest limit:** the general on-ramp has **no vertical-specific compliance corpus**. If your business needs drafts checked against a particular regulatory rulebook, that's a Custom engagement, and the upside ceiling here is lower than a named-vertical product. That's the honest trade for a simple, fast on-ramp. The baseline safeguard on every general workspace is the human approval gate, plus standard guardrails against TCPA, CAN-SPAM, and FTC Act §5 exposure — nothing auto-sends, so nothing goes out you didn't read.
