# Discovery Call Agenda — general (SMB on-ramp)

30 minutes. Goal: understand what eats their week, show drafts-into-approvals, make the
design-partner ask. Honest throughout — if they need a vertical compliance corpus, name it
as a Custom engagement on the call. The fleet drafts; the owner approves and sends.

Sign-off / host: Conner Chambers, founder.

---

## Open (2 min)

**Talking points:**
- Thanks for the time; this is 15–30 minutes, not a pitch marathon.
- One line on what agentplain is: "We install a fleet of AI partners inside your business, connect your tools, and draft the routine admin into a queue you approve. You stay the only one who hits send."
- Set the frame: "I want to understand your week first, then show you the thing, then tell you about the design-partner program. Stop me with questions anytime."

---

## Their stack + pain (10 min)

**Goal:** find the admin that lives in the owner's head and confirm the tools we can connect.

**Qualifying questions:**
- What part of running {{business_name}} eats the most of your time that isn't serving customers?
- Walk me through a typical week of admin — inbox, scheduling, follow-ups. What slips when you get busy?
- How many hours a week would you guess go to back-office work only you can do?
- What's your email — Gmail or Outlook/Microsoft 365? Do you run Google Calendar?
- Do you use QuickBooks? (We connect it read-only — AR aging, open invoices, month-end.)
- Any CRM or other tools you'd want in the loop? (Note: we have a generic CRM webhook receiver; named connectors like Salesforce or HubSpot are roadmap, not live — be honest.)
- **Compliance check:** Is your business in a space with its own regulatory rulebook you'd need the drafts checked against? If yes — flag it: that's a Custom engagement, not the standard on-ramp. The general track has no vertical-specific compliance corpus; the baseline safeguard is the human approval gate plus the standard TCPA/CAN-SPAM/FTC guardrails.

**Talking points:**
- Listen for: inbox triage, follow-up chasing, scheduling, repetitive process docs. Those are the four live general workflows — Chief of Staff, Inbox Triage, Follow-Up Chaser, Process-Doc Drafter.
- Reflect their number back: "So roughly six hours a week — that's the operator-modeled floor we work from."

---

## Demo — drafts into approvals (10 min)

**Goal:** show, don't tell. Two workflows.

**Talking points:**
- **Inbox triage:** "Here's a real inbox. The fleet reads what's there, sorts it, and drafts first-touch replies. Every one lands in the approvals queue as PENDING. Nothing is sent."
- **Follow-up chaser:** "Here's a thread that went quiet. The fleet drafts the nudge and queues it. You read it, approve it, and it goes from your inbox — with your name on it."
- Name the control out loud: "The fleet drafts and proposes. You approve and send. It never sends, files, or commits anything on its own. That's the whole design."
- "It learns your voice from your edits — the drafts get more like you the more you correct them."

---

## Design-partner ask (5 min)

**Talking points:**
- "We're taking on a small cohort — a handful of design partners — so each one gets real founder attention."
- **What you get:** free for the first three months · weekly founder time during the pilot · early access to new features · a joint case study you approve every word of.
- **What we ask back:** a weekly 30-minute call during the pilot · an on-record testimonial once you've seen value · willingness to be a sales reference later.
- "After the pilot you convert to standard month-to-month pricing for your tier — Regular, $99–$199 a seat — or you don't. No obligation."

---

## Next steps (3 min)

**Talking points:**
- Gauge interest plainly: "Does this sound like something worth trying for three months?"
- If yes: "I'll send a short follow-up today with the program terms and a link to get your workspace started — card at signup, 7-day trial, then the three free months as a design partner."
- If maybe: "What would you need to see to decide?"
- Confirm the connect: "First setup step is connecting your email and calendar — ten minutes, we can do it together on our first weekly call."
- Always end with a concrete next action and a date.
