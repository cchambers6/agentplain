# Why agentplain — design-partner deck (general / SMB on-ramp)

10 slides. Plaino voice OK. Presenter: Conner Chambers, founder.
The spine of every slide: the fleet drafts; you approve and send; nothing auto-sends.

---

## Slide 1 — The admin lives in your head

- You started the business to do the work. You ended up running the back-office too.
- agentplain drafts the routine admin into a queue you approve.
- *Intelligence rooted in reality.*

*Speaker notes:* Open on them, not us. One sentence on what we do, then move. The promise is calm and concrete — drafts in a queue, not "AI magic." Don't oversell on slide one; the room hasn't bought in yet.

---

## Slide 2 — The 7pm inbox

- The messages you couldn't get to during the day.
- The follow-ups you meant to send Tuesday.
- The scheduling, the repeated explanations, the dozen small things only you can do.
- That hour, most nights, is the one the business takes back from you.

*Speaker notes:* This is the emotional anchor. Tell the Tuesday-night scene as a story, not a bullet list — the bullets are just the prompt. Let it land before moving on. Every owner in the room lives this.

---

## Slide 3 — What agentplain is

- A done-for-you service, not a tool you have to drive.
- We install a fleet of capable AI partners, connect your tools, and run a monthly review.
- We set it up. We keep it running. You stay in charge of what goes out.

*Speaker notes:* Make the service framing explicit — they are not buying software to learn. Never name the model vendor. If asked "what's it built on," it's fine to say "the easy way to actually use the best AI" — the model is invisible by design.

---

## Slide 4 — The honesty spine

- The fleet **drafts and proposes**. Every item lands in your approvals queue as pending.
- **You approve and send** — from your own inbox, your own name on it.
- The fleet never sends, files, pays, or commits anything on its own.

*Speaker notes:* This is the differentiator — slow down here. Most "AI automation" pitches promise the machine acts for you; we promise the opposite, on purpose. That control is the trust. Say it plainly: nothing leaves without you.

## Slide 5 — What's live for you on day one

- **Chief of Staff** — keeps the week organized.
- **Inbox Triage** — sorts what comes in, drafts the replies.
- **Follow-Up Chaser** — catches threads gone quiet and drafts the nudge.
- **Process-Doc Drafter** — writes down what only you know.

*Speaker notes:* These four are live today (they require Gmail or Microsoft 365 to be connected). Don't promise anything beyond them. If they ask about a workflow not on this list, say "not yet — here's what is."

## Slide 6 — The tools we connect

- Gmail and Microsoft 365 / Outlook (email)
- Google Calendar
- QuickBooks (read-only — AR aging, open invoices, month-end)
- DocuSign
- A generic CRM webhook receiver

*Speaker notes:* This is the live integration list — full stop. If they run Salesforce, HubSpot, or an industry tool, be honest: those are roadmap, not wired today. The generic webhook receiver covers many CRMs; don't claim a named one we haven't built.

## Slide 7 — What the time is worth

- ~6 owner-hours a week on admin only you can do.
- × $100/hr (conservative) × 4.3 weeks ≈ **~$2,580/month**.
- ≈ **13–15× return** — an operator-modeled floor, not a customer result.

*Speaker notes:* Frame this as a floor and a model, every time — we have zero customers today, so this is our math, not attested results. The honesty is the credibility. Offer to run it against their real number on the next call.

---

## Slide 8 — Where we're honest about limits

- **No vertical-specific compliance corpus** on the general track. Need drafts checked against a regulatory rulebook? That's a Custom engagement — and the upside ceiling here is lower than a named-vertical product.
- The baseline safeguard on every workspace is the **human approval gate** — nothing auto-sends.
- Standard guardrails against TCPA ($500–$1,500/message), CAN-SPAM, and FTC Act §5 exposure.

*Speaker notes:* Putting the limit on its own slide builds more trust than hiding it. The approval gate IS the compliance story for the general track — because nothing goes out without a human reading it first. If their business is heavily regulated, route them to Custom; don't fudge it.
