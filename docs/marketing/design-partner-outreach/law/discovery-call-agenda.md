# Law — Discovery Call Agenda (30 minutes)

For the facilitator (Conner). Sober, precise. The goal is to learn the firm's intake
and conflict workflow, show the draft-into-approvals gate honestly, and make the
design-partner ask. Never imply legal advice, autonomous sending, or existing clients.
Do NOT promise Clio or MyCase integration — they are not connected.

---

## 0:00–2:00 — Open (2 min)

**Talking points:**
- Thanks for the 15 — I'll respect it; this should run about half an hour if you have it,
  and I'll stop at 15 if you don't.
- One line on us: agentplain is a done-for-you service. A fleet of AI partners drafts the
  routine work; an attorney approves and sends every client-facing piece. Nothing sends
  itself.
- I have zero clients today — I'm building a small design-partner cohort, and this call is
  to see if your firm is a fit.

---

## 2:00–12:00 — Their stack and pain (10 min)

Listen more than talk. Capture specifics.

**Qualifying questions:**
- What are your main practice areas, and how many attorneys?
- Roughly how many new intakes come in a week — and how many arrive after hours?
- Walk me through your conflict check today. Who runs it, against what list, and how long
  does it take per matter?
- How do client status updates get written now — who drafts them, and how often do they
  slip?
- How heavy is your document-review load, and where does it bottleneck?
- What case-management or intake software are you on?
  *(Note internally: Clio / MyCase are NOT agentplain integrations. Do not promise them.
  If they name one, say: "We're not connected to that yet — today the live integrations
  are email and calendar. I'll be straight with you about what's wired and what isn't.")*

---

## 12:00–22:00 — Demo: drafts-into-approvals + the gate + conflict screen (10 min)

**Talking points:**
- Three things to show: the approvals queue, the attorney-approval gate, and the
  deterministic conflict check.
- **The queue.** Every piece the fleet drafts lands here as pending — a status update, an
  intake acknowledgement, a conflict notice. Nothing moves until a person decides.
- **The gate.** This is the differentiator. An attorney approves every client-facing draft
  before it goes anywhere. The system does not send, file, or state a legal conclusion.
- **The conflict screen.** It's a deterministic adverse-party check — it compares the new
  party against your matter list and drafts an internal notice. The legal conclusion is a
  merge field a human fills. It surfaces the match; it does not make the call.
- Be explicit on limits: live integrations today are email and calendar. Case-management
  systems are roadmap, not connected.

---

## 22:00–27:00 — The design-partner ask (5 min)

**Talking points:**
- What you'd get: free for the first three months, weekly time directly with me during
  the pilot, early access to new features, and a joint case study you approve word for
  word before anything is public.
- What I ask back: a weekly 30-minute call during the pilot, an on-record testimonial once
  you've seen value, and willingness to be a sales reference later.
- The cohort is small — a handful of firms across all verticals — so each gets real
  attention.

---

## 27:00–30:00 — Next steps (3 min)

**Talking points:**
- If there's interest: I'll send a short one-pager and a follow-up to set the first
  working session.
- If it's a maybe: what would you need to see to decide?
- If it's a no: appreciated — anything I should know about why, so I build the right thing?
- Confirm the single next action and who owns it before hanging up.
