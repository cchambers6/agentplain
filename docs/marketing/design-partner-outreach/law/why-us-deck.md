# Why agentplain — Law Firm Design-Partner Deck

10 slides. Sober, precise. No persona. Sign-off: Conner Chambers, founder, agentplain.
Honesty spine throughout: the fleet drafts; an attorney approves; nothing sends itself.
ROI is operator-modeled. Zero clients today. Never imply legal advice.

---

## Slide 1 — Intelligence rooted in reality

- agentplain: a done-for-you AI service for law firms.
- The fleet drafts the routine work. An attorney approves and sends.
- Nothing leaves the firm without a licensed person's sign-off.

*Speaker notes:* Open on the firm, not on us. One line on what we are, then straight to
the differentiator: the human stays in control of everything client-facing. Set the
sober register here — this is legal copy, not a pitch.

---

## Slide 2 — The problem

- Conflict screening: an adverse-party check on every new matter — unbilled, every time.
- Status updates: constant, low-value, easy to let slip between deadlines.
- After-hours intake: inquiries sit until morning — slow first response on convertible matters.

*Speaker notes:* These are the three time sinks at a small firm. Name them concretely. Ask
which one hurts most — let the prospect tell you where to focus the rest of the call.

---

## Slide 3 — What agentplain is

- We install a fleet of AI partners inside your firm.
- We connect your tools and run a monthly review.
- It's a service, not a tool you have to drive.

*Speaker notes:* The frame is done-for-you. We set it up, we tune it, we stay with you.
This matters to a managing partner who has no time to administer software.

---

## Slide 4 — The honesty spine

- The fleet **drafts** and proposes — into an approvals queue.
- An **attorney approves** every client-facing piece.
- **Nothing sends itself.** No filing, no advice, no legal conclusion stated by the system.

*Speaker notes:* This is the load-bearing slide. The differentiator isn't speed — it's
that a licensed person signs off on everything, every time. Say plainly what the system
will never do. In this market, the boundary is the selling point.

---

## Slide 5 — Live law workflows

- **Conflict screen:** deterministic adverse-party check; drafts an internal notice; the
  legal conclusion is a merge field an attorney fills.
- **Document-review acceleration:** first-pass drafts for an attorney's review.
- **Status-update drafting:** client-update drafts that land in your queue.

*Speaker notes:* Be precise about the conflict screen — it's deterministic, it surfaces a
match, it does not make the legal call. The merge-field design is how we keep the system
out of practicing law. Walk this one slowly.

---

## Slide 6 — Integration scope

- **Connected today:** email and calendar.
- **Not connected:** case-management systems — Clio, MyCase, and the like are roadmap.
- We will always tell you what's wired and what isn't.

*Speaker notes:* Do not overclaim. If they're on Clio or MyCase, say it isn't connected
yet. Being honest about the gap is what makes the rest of the deck credible — and it's the
brand. Never imply a case-management integration we don't have.

---

## Slide 7 — The ROI math

- Operator-modeled: ~$150,000/yr reclaimed at a 3-attorney firm.
- That's roughly 40% capture of a ~$375k annual opportunity in recovered time.
- Larger firms scale further — a 25-attorney firm models past $2.3M/yr.

*Speaker notes:* Frame this explicitly as a model, not a result — we have zero clients
today, so there are no attested numbers. Source: law content model. Pair it with the next
slide: the upside is the time reclaimed; the downside is the exposure if client work goes
out wrong.

---

## Slide 8 — The downside it guards against

- ABA Model Rule 1.6 — client confidentiality.
- ABA Model Rule 7.1 — no misleading client-facing claims.
- Exposure runs to malpractice and disbarment.

*Speaker notes:* This is why the approval gate exists. The safeguard isn't trusting the AI
to behave — it's that an attorney approves every client-facing draft. State the rules by
number; this audience respects precision. Nothing about the system constitutes legal advice.

## Slide 9 — The design-partner offer

- Free for the first three months of the pilot.
- Dedicated founder time — weekly, direct with me.
- Early feature access; a joint case study you approve word for word.
- We ask back: a weekly 30-min call, an on-record testimonial, a sales reference.

*Speaker notes:* This is the consideration exchange. Be clear the cohort is small — a
handful of firms — so each gets real attention. Don't invent a countdown. The founder time
is the rare thing here; it's offered to design partners and otherwise only at our top tiers.
