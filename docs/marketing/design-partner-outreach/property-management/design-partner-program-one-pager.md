## The problem

Three jobs run at once and none of them ever finish. The **tenant inbox** never empties —
maintenance pings, balance questions, lease asks, after hours. The **delinquency chase** runs
on top of it, and every day a balance sits un-chased is a day of float you don't get back.
Then **owner reports** eat what's left of the month. For a 50–500 door operator, that's not a
software gap — it's a time gap, and the people who should be managing properties are managing
an inbox instead.

---

## What agentplain does

A fleet of AI partners reads your tenant inbox and your QuickBooks balances and **drafts** the
work into an approvals queue. Every item is PENDING until you decide. You approve and send from
your own systems.

**Working today:**
- **Tenant Inbound** — reads incoming tenant messages, drafts a reply to each.
- **Collections** — reads overdue balances, drafts a chase per tenant, tone escalating as the
  balance ages.
- **Chief of Staff** — a daily summary of what needs your attention.

**Setting up (early access for design partners as they ship):** Work-Order Router, Renewal
Coordinator, Owner Reporter, Application Screening, Books Reconciler. We'll always be straight
about which is which.

**Connected today:** QuickBooks Online, Outlook + Microsoft 365, Gmail/Google, and Twilio Voice
(inbound call triage only). Buildium, AppFolio, Propertyware, and Yardi are on the roadmap, not
live yet — we won't claim a connection we don't have.

**The honesty spine:** the fleet drafts and proposes; it never sends, files, pays, or commits
anything on its own. Fair-housing-risky wording is caught at the draft stage — by you, before
anything goes out. The human approval gate is the safeguard.

---

## The fair-housing downside this guards against

A single discriminatory phrase in a tenant message is a **$26,262** first-offense HUD penalty
(24 CFR §180.671), on top of state landlord-tenant notice and timing rules. A dedicated
fair-housing review pass activates after our counsel review; until then, the safeguard is the
human approval gate — every message passes your eyes before it sends.
