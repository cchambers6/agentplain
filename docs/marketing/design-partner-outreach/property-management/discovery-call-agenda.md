# Property-Management — Discovery Call Agenda (30 min)

Internal runsheet for the founder on the intro/discovery call. Goal: understand their stack
and pain, show drafts-into-approvals, make the design-partner ask, agree on next steps.

Honesty discipline for this call:
- Tenant Inbound, Collections, and Chief of Staff are **working** today.
- Work-Order Router, Renewal Coordinator, Owner Reporter, Application Screening, and Books
  Reconciler are **setting up** — say so plainly when they come up.
- Live integrations: QuickBooks Online, Outlook + M365, Gmail/Google, Twilio Voice (inbound
  triage receiver only). **Buildium, AppFolio, Propertyware, Yardi are roadmap — if they're on
  one, say the connector isn't live yet and walk through the email + QuickBooks path instead.**
- ROI numbers are operator-modeled and pending primary research. Frame as a model, never as
  customer results. There are zero customers today — that's why the design-partner program exists.

---

## Open — 2 min

- Thanks for the time. Keep it to 30.
- One line on agentplain: "We're a done-for-you AI service for property managers. A fleet of
  AI partners drafts your tenant replies and collection chases into a queue you approve.
  Nothing sends without you."
- Frame the call: "I want to understand how you run things first, then show you what it drafts."

---

## Their stack + pain — 10 min

Listen more than you talk. Qualifying questions:

**Scale & structure**
- How many doors do you manage today? (Looking for the 50–500 range.)
- Single-family, multifamily, mixed? How many PMs on the team?

**Software in use**
- What property-management software do you run? (Buildium / AppFolio / Propertyware / Yardi /
  spreadsheets?)
  - *If Buildium or AppFolio:* "Be straight with you — a direct connector to that is on our
    roadmap, not live yet. Today the fleet works off your email and QuickBooks Online, which
    covers tenant comms and the chase. I'd rather tell you that now than oversell it."
- Do you use QuickBooks Online for the books? (Live integration — strong fit.)
- Email on Outlook/M365 or Google? (Both live.)

**Tenant comms volume**
- Roughly how many tenant messages a day across the portfolio?
- Who handles the inbox now, and when does it pile up? (Listen for the after-hours scene.)

**Delinquency process**
- How do you run collections today — who chases, how often, what triggers escalation?
- How many days, on average, until a late balance gets a first chase?
- How do owner reports get built each month, and how long do they take?

---

## Demo — drafts-into-approvals — 10 min

Show, don't tell. Two live workflows:

**Tenant Inbound (working today)**
- A tenant message comes in. The fleet reads it and drafts a reply.
- It lands in the approvals queue as PENDING. Show the edit + approve step.
- Hammer the control: "Nothing here has been sent. You're the one who hits send."
- Fair-housing point: "If a draft has risky wording, you catch it right here, before it goes
  out. The human approval gate is the safeguard."

**Collections (working today)**
- The fleet reads overdue balances from QuickBooks and drafts a chase per tenant, tone
  escalating as the balance ages.
- Again: drafts into the queue, you approve every send.

**Be honest about what's next**
- "These two are working. A few more — work-order routing, renewals, owner reporting,
  application screening, books reconciliation — are still setting up. Design partners get them
  early as they ship."

---

## Design-partner ask — 5 min

- "We're taking a small cohort of design partners — a handful across all our verticals — so
  each one gets real attention from me."
- **What they get:** first 3 months free; weekly time directly with me during the pilot; early
  access to the workflows still setting up; a joint case study they approve every word of.
- **What we ask back:** a weekly 30-minute call during the pilot; an on-record testimonial once
  they've seen value; willingness to be a sales reference later.
- After the pilot they convert to standard month-to-month for their tier (Regular) — no
  obligation to convert.

---

## Next steps — 3 min

- Gauge interest. If warm: "Want me to send the one-pager and hold a spot in the cohort?"
- Concrete next action: send program one-pager + a follow-up to book the kickoff.
- If they need to think: agree on a date to circle back (put it on the calendar live).
- Thank them. Keep it plain — no pressure close.
