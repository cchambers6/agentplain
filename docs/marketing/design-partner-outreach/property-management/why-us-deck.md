## Slide 1 — The fleet drafts. You approve and send.

- agentplain is a done-for-you AI service for property managers.
- A fleet of AI partners drafts your tenant replies and collection chases.
- Everything lands in a queue you control. Nothing sends without you.

*Speaker notes:* Open on the control, not on us. The whole pitch is in the title — the fleet
does the drafting, the human keeps the send button. Set the tone: calm, concrete, honest.

## Slide 2 — The problem: three jobs, no finish line

- The tenant inbox never empties — maintenance, balances, leases, after hours.
- The delinquency chase runs on top of it; every un-chased day is lost float.
- Owner reports eat what's left of the month.

*Speaker notes:* Paint the Tuesday-night scene. It's 7pm, three tenants have emailed since
five — a leaking disposal, a balance question, a parking complaint — and tomorrow's chases
still haven't gone out. For a 50–500 door shop this is the real job, and it's the wrong job.

## Slide 3 — What agentplain is

- We install a fleet of AI partners inside your business.
- We connect your tools and run a monthly review.
- It's a service, not a tool you have to drive.

*Speaker notes:* Distinguish us from "buy software and figure it out." We set it up, we tune
it, we stay with you. The fleet reads your real inbox and your real books and gets to work.

## Slide 4 — The honesty spine (this is the differentiator)

- The fleet **drafts** — every item is PENDING until you decide.
- You approve and send from your own systems.
- Fair-housing-risky wording is caught at the draft stage, before anything leaves.

*Speaker notes:* This is what makes us safe to put near a tenant inbox. The fleet never sends,
files, pays, or commits anything on its own. The human approval gate isn't a limitation we're
apologizing for — it's the safeguard. Lean in here.

## Slide 8 — The fair-housing downside this guards against

- A single discriminatory phrase: **$26,262** first-offense HUD penalty (24 CFR §180.671).
- Plus state landlord-tenant notice and timing rules.
- Until our fair-housing review pass activates (after counsel review), the human approval gate
  is the safeguard.

*Speaker notes:* Keep this sober — no jokes, no Plaino persona here. Most fair-housing slips are
accidental, written in a rushed reply at the end of a long day. Because the fleet only drafts and
you approve every message, the risky wording gets a second look before it ever sends.
