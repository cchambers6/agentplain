# Real-Estate Design-Partner — Discovery Call Agenda (30 min)

A structured 30-minute first call with a broker-owner. Goal: understand their
coordination load, show the drafts-into-approvals loop, and make the design-partner
ask. Honest throughout — the fleet drafts; they approve and send. Never imply
existing customers. ROI is operator-modeled.

---

## Open — 2 minutes

**Talking points:**
- Thanks for the time. This is 15–30 minutes, not a demo marathon.
- Quick frame: agentplain installs a fleet of AI partners inside your business. It
  drafts the routine work into a queue; you approve and send from your own systems.
  Nothing leaves without your name on it.
- I want most of this call to be you talking. Tell me where your week goes.

---

## Their stack + pain — 10 minutes

Listen for the coordination load (the 8–12 hrs/wk we model the ROI on). Ask, don't
pitch.

**Qualifying questions:**
- Where do your inbound buyer and seller leads land — inbox, a CRM, a portal? Which one?
- What CRM do you run? (Follow Up Boss, Sierra, kvCORE, a spreadsheet?) *Note: most
  CRMs are roadmap, not wired today — be honest. The live story is email + calendar
  + QuickBooks + DocuSign + Drive.*
- When a lead comes in at 9pm during a showing, what actually happens to it?
- Who handles transaction coordination — you, a TC, an assistant? How many hours?
- How do you bill and chase commission — through QuickBooks, or something else?
- What's the one recurring task you'd hand off tomorrow if you could?

**Talking point:** Reflect their number back. "So that's roughly ten hours a week
on coordination that isn't selling." That's the wedge.

---

## Demo — the drafts-into-approvals loop — 10 minutes

**Talking points:**
- Here's the loop, end to end. A lead comes in. The fleet scores it and drafts a
  first-touch reply — in your voice — into the approvals queue.
- Every item sits as PENDING until you decide. You read it, edit it, approve it.
  Then *you* send it, from your own inbox. The fleet never sends.
- Same loop for commission invoices: it reads your overdue invoices off QuickBooks
  and drafts a chase for each, escalating in tone as they age. You approve each send.
- The honesty spine is the point: this isn't an auto-send bot. An auto-send tool is
  one bad draft away from a Fair-Housing complaint. We put a human gate on every send.
- A compliance sentinel scans each draft against the HUD enumerated-phrase list and
  a PII check. It flags; it never blocks or edits. One bad phrase is a $26,262
  first-offense penalty — this catches it before you approve.

---

## The design-partner ask — 5 minutes

**Talking points:**
- I'm building this with a small cohort of broker-owners — three to five total — so
  each gets real attention. There are zero customers I can point you to yet; that's
  exactly why this program exists, and why partners get in on the ground floor.
- The deal: free for the first three months, my direct time weekly during the
  pilot, early access to what we ship, and a joint case study you approve every word of.
- What I ask back: a weekly 30-minute call, an on-record testimonial once you've
  seen value, and willingness to be a sales reference later.
- After the pilot you convert to standard pricing for your tier — Regular, $99–$199
  a seat — month-to-month, cancel anytime. No obligation to convert.

---

## Next steps — 3 minutes

**Talking points:**
- Gauge fit out loud: "Does this sound like a problem worth three months of pilot?"
- If yes: I'll send a one-pager and we book the kickoff to connect your inbox and
  calendar together.
- If maybe: what would you need to see to decide? Name it; I'll follow up with that.
- If no: totally fair — thanks for the honesty and the time.
- Confirm the single next action before you hang up.
