# Real-Estate Design-Partner Follow-Up Sequence

Four emails after the first-contact send. Each varies the angle — no email repeats
the prior one's hook. Sign as Conner Chambers. Subject lines lowercase-leaning,
≤ 8 words, no exclamation point.

---

## Email 1 — T+3 days · angle: concrete-scene value

**Subject:** what the first week actually looks like

Hi {{first_name}},

I'll skip the pitch and show you the shape of it. Week one, you connect your inbox
and calendar — one button each. The fleet starts reading what's already there. A
new buyer lead comes in; instead of sitting overnight, a first-touch reply is
waiting in your queue the next morning, scored and drafted in your voice. You read
it, fix what's off, send it from your own inbox.

That's the whole loop: it drafts, you approve, you send. By Friday it's drafting a
few things a day.

Still happy to walk you through it live. Want to find 15 minutes?

— Conner Chambers, founder, agentplain

## Email 2 — T+7 days · angle: the Fair-Housing downside

**Subject:** the $26,262 line in a listing draft

Hi {{first_name}},

Different angle today. One discriminatory phrase in a listing description or a
buyer reply is a $26,262 first-offense HUD penalty — and the agent who typed it
usually had no idea it crossed the line.

agentplain runs a compliance sentinel over every draft. It scans against the HUD
enumerated-phrase list and flags anything risky before a human approves it. It
advises; it never sends, never edits, never decides. You stay in control — you
just stop typing the sentence that costs you twenty-six grand.

That safeguard is part of what design partners get to shape early.

Fifteen minutes to see how it works?
