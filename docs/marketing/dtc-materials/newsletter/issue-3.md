Normally a Week-4 email is where a company drops a glowing customer quote.

We're not going to invent one. We're early, and we don't have named, public reference customers yet — so instead of a testimonial we can't honestly attribute, here's an honest composite: the kind of week the fleet gives back across the businesses it's built for. Representative, not a quote from a real named firm.

**The real-estate firm.**
Inbound leads used to go cold overnight. Now the first-touch reply is drafted by 6:30am, the agent changes one detail and sends, and the commission invoices that used to slip get a chase drafted the day they age past due. The morning that used to start with inbox triage starts with a queue that's already mostly written.

**The accounting firm.**
It's close season. Twenty-some clients are missing documents. The month-end-close fleet drafts a chase for each one — formal, specific, listing exactly what's outstanding — and never states a tax position, because that's the preparer's call. The partner approves a batch in a few minutes instead of writing each one by hand at 9pm.

**The trades shop.**
Estimates that used to sit and die get a follow-up drafted at each stage; the cold ones get handed back to the rep for a phone call instead of a guilt-trip text. Nothing auto-sends, so a non-consented blast never goes out by machine — which, given the per-text penalties for that, is the whole point.

**The law firm.**
Every intake gets an acknowledgement drafted, with the no-attorney-client-relationship language built in, and nothing client-facing goes out until an attorney approves it. The conflict screen runs deterministically and drafts the internal notice; the legal conclusion stays a field for a lawyer to fill.

**The advisory practice.**
The quarterly client update gets drafted with every dollar figure and every recommendation left as an advisor merge field — never rendered by the fleet — with the regulatory pointers riding along. The advisor fills the numbers, approves, sends.

Different work, same shape: the fleet drafts, a licensed human approves and sends, and the routine stops eating the parts of the week that should go to clients.
