# Reference-customer onboarding pack

**What this is:** the kit we send a design partner who has said "yes, I'll be a reference."
It is not a claim that we have reference customers — it's the gracious, low-burden process
for turning a willing early partner into honest, citable, owner-approved proof.

**Tone:** owner-to-owner. We are asking a favor and making it easy. No leading questions, no
invented numbers, nothing published without their approval.

---

### What you get

- A named service partner — a real person — who sets agentplain up for your firm and stays
  with you as it runs.
- Hands-on install: we connect your tools, configure the fleet for your line of work, and
  tune it from your edits.
- A direct line to shape what the fleet drafts next. Your feedback moves the roadmap.
- The standard guarantees: first month free, month-to-month, cancel anytime, 14-day
  money-back.

### What we need from you

- About **40 minutes of connection time** in week one (your email, calendar, and primary
  tool — we drive; you click "approve").
- Your **default hours and tone**, so the drafts sound like you.
- A few minutes a day in your **approvals queue** for the first two weeks — reading drafts,
  editing, sending. That's how the fleet learns your voice.
- Honest feedback when something's off. The off drafts are more useful to us than the good
  ones.

### The first two weeks

| When | What we do | What you do |
|---|---|---|
| **Day 1 — kickoff** | 20-min call; connect email + calendar + your primary tool together. | Approve the connections; tell us your hours and tone. |
| **Days 2–4** | The fleet reads what's already there and starts drafting into your queue. | Open the queue; read drafts; edit and send what's right. |

We don't ask for a quote or a logo in these two weeks. We ask you to *use* it. Everything in
Parts 2–4 comes later, only if you're glad to.

## Part 2 — Case-study questionnaire

**How we use this:** we'd love to turn your answers into a short, honest write-up. Two ground
rules. First, **don't invent numbers.** If you don't know a figure, "I don't track that" is a
perfect answer — we'd rather have an honest gap than a made-up stat. Second, **nothing gets
published without your approval** (see Part 3). Answer in your own words; a sentence each is
plenty.

1. What's your firm — line of work, roughly how many people, where you're based?
2. Before agentplain, what was the routine work that ate the most of your week? Be specific —
   the actual task, the time of day, the part you dreaded.
3. What had you tried before us (AI tools, a VA, staying late, nothing) and why didn't it
   stick?
4. What does the fleet draft for you now? Which of those drafts do you rely on most?
5. Walk us through one concrete moment it helped — a specific lead, a specific close, a
   specific week. What happened, start to finish?
6. Roughly how much time does it give back, and where does that time go now? Only if you have
   a real sense of it — a range or "a couple of mornings a week" is fine; please don't guess
   at a precise number.
7. Is there a dollar figure you can point to — chased invoices collected, work you could take
   on instead, hours you'd have paid someone for? Only if it's real and yours to share.
8. What did you expect to be hard about handing routine work to AI, and how has the
   approve-before-send control actually felt in practice?
9. Was there a draft that was wrong, and what happened next? (We're glad to include this —
   honest is more credible than perfect.)
10. Who would you tell to try this, and who would you tell it's *not* for?
11. Anything you'd want us to be careful **not** to say about your firm?
12. In one or two sentences, how would you describe agentplain to a peer across the counter?

> When we draft the write-up from your answers, we'll send it to you first. Nothing is final
> until you sign off — see Part 3.

## Part 4 — Reference-call coordination

**What this is:** sometimes a prospect in your vertical wants to hear it from a real owner,
not from us. If you're open to it, we'll occasionally ask you to take a short, low-stakes
call. You're never on the hook — any single ask is a yes/no with no pressure.

### How we ask you (the template we send)

> **Subject:** a 15-minute reference call — only if it's easy
>
> Hi {{first_name}},
>
> A {{vertical}} firm we're talking to would value hearing from someone actually using
> agentplain, and you came to mind. Would you be open to a 15-minute call with them — just
> your honest take, the good and the rough?
>
> Totally fine to pass; it won't change anything between us. If you're open to it, here are a
> couple of windows: {{two or three time options}}. I'll send the invite and a two-line prep
> note so it stays light.
>
> Thanks either way.
>
> — {{partner_name}}

### Scheduling logistics

- We send a calendar invite with a video link; you don't set anything up.
- We cap it at 15 minutes and we mean it.
- We never give out your contact details — the prospect reaches you only through the invite.
- We'll ask no more than once a quarter unless you tell us you're happy to do more.

### Prep note for you (so the call stays honest and light)

> A quick heads-up before the call:
>
> - Just be honest. The good *and* the rough. A balanced take is more useful to them and more
>   credible — you don't need to sell anything.
> - You don't have to know our pricing or roadmap. "I'd ask agentplain that" is a perfect
>   answer.
> - Talk about your own experience — what you handed over, what you still control, what
>   surprised you. You're the expert on your firm, not on our product.
> - There are no wrong answers and nothing's scripted. If something didn't work for you, say
>   so — we'd rather you be straight than kind.
> - 15 minutes, then you're done. Thank you for doing this.
