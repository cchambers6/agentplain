## The quarterly letters take a week of your evenings.

Every household expects an update. Each one has to sound like you, carry the right numbers, and stay on the right side of the SEC Marketing Rule. So you write them at night — after the meetings, after everything else. A week of evenings, every quarter.

And it isn't just the letters. The meeting recap that should go out the same day lands three days later, if at all. The inbox triage that never quite gets done. The admin that takes your time away from the clients you're meant to serve.

---

## What Plaino does for your practice

agentplain installs a fleet of capable AI partners inside your practice, connects your tools, and runs a monthly review. **The fleet drafts and proposes; you approve and send.** Nothing leaves without your name on it.

- **Quarterly client-update letters** — drafted overnight, one per household. Every dollar figure and every forward-looking line is left as a blank merge field for *you* to fill. Your Form ADV and qualified-custodian pointers ride along on the draft.
- **Meeting recaps** — drafted within hours of the meeting, not three days later. You edit and send from your own inbox.
- **Comms triage** — the fleet reads the inbox, drafts the routine replies, and lands them in your approvals queue.
- **Bookkeeping-side finance pulse** — connected to QuickBooks for AR aging, open invoices, and month-end on the practice's own books.

**Connected today:** email (Gmail / Outlook), Google Calendar, and QuickBooks. *(Custodial and portfolio-system integrations are on the roadmap, not claimed as live.)*

## What's different

- **Done-for-you, not another tool.** We install it, configure it, and run a monthly review. It's a service, built on Claude, configured by us — the easy way to actually use Claude in your practice.
- **Draft, then you approve.** Every item is PENDING in your queue until you decide. The fleet never sends, files, or commits anything on its own.
- **The SEC Marketing Rule moat.** A Marketing Rule (206(4)-1) corpus reviews each draft and *flags* — it advises, it never sends. The 2024 sweeps settled **$60k–$325k per adviser** for unsubstantiated claims. We built the workflow so the draft can't make the claim; you do, with the number in front of you, before anything is filed as an advertisement. An auto-send tool structurally cannot offer this.
- **Every number is a merge field.** No dollar figure, no performance number, no recommendation is ever rendered by the fleet. It's always a blank `{{advisor: …}}` for you to fill — the honesty spine, and the compliance one.
