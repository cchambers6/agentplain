## The load that isn't the law

The hours a firm loses usually aren't the legal work — they're the work around it:

- **New-matter intake & conflict screening.** Every matter waits on the adverse-party
  check. Done carefully, it can cost an afternoon before anyone bills a minute.
- **Document review at volume.** A review with 312 borderline calls can swallow 60
  billable hours — when maybe 14 of them actually needed a lawyer's judgment.
- **Status chasing.** Clients asking where things stand; documents that hold a closing;
  the routine acknowledgements that eat a morning.

This is the band of work agentplain drafts for you — and the band where an auto-send tool
is a liability, not a help.

---

## What Plaino does for your firm

agentplain installs a fleet of capable AI partners inside your firm, connects your tools,
and runs a monthly review. Plaino is your one named partner across the workspace. The fleet:

- **Runs the new-matter conflict screen in seconds** — a deterministic adverse-party
  screen, the same way every matter — and drafts the conflict notice for review.
- **Drafts intake acknowledgements and status updates** into your approvals queue, with the
  no-attorney-client-relationship language built in.
- **Accelerates document review** — drafts the routine calls, leaves you the judgment ones.
- **Drafts month-adjacent chases** for documents and signatures that hold a matter.

Every legal conclusion is an `{{attorney: …}}` merge field — the fleet never asserts one.
An attorney approves every client-facing draft before it leaves the firm.

## What's different

1. **Done-for-you, not a tool you drive.** We install, configure, connect your tools, and
   run a monthly review. Built on Claude, configured by us — the easy way to actually use
   it.
2. **Draft-then-approve, always.** The fleet drafts and proposes; an attorney approves and
   sends from your own system. Nothing leaves with anyone's name on it but yours.
3. **The Model Rule 1.6 moat.** Because a person approves every client-facing draft, a
   privilege breach (Rule 1.6) or a misleading claim (Rule 7.1) never leaves the firm by
   machine. Exposure under these rules runs to malpractice and discipline — which is
   exactly why the approval gate is the product, not a footnote. An auto-send tool cannot
   structurally make this promise.
