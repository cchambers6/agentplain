## Agenda (30 minutes, timed)

- **0:00–3:00 — The frame.** Why the lost hours at a firm aren't the law; they're the work
  around it. The one rule for the next 27 minutes: an attorney approves every client-facing
  draft.
- **3:00–8:00 — The conflict screen, live.** A new matter comes in. We run the deterministic
  adverse-party screen in seconds and show the drafted conflict notice — legal conclusion as
  a merge field, not asserted.
- **8:00–15:00 — The doc-review case.** 312 borderline calls, 60 billable hours, and the 14
  that actually need judgment. How the fleet drafts the routine and leaves you the rest.
- **15:00–21:00 — The approval gate & Model Rule 1.6.** Where the draft-then-approve model
  protects you on confidentiality (1.6) and misleading claims (7.1) — the promise an
  auto-send tool can't make.
- **21:00–25:00 — What we connect today, honestly.** Email and calendar are live;
  practice-management tools are roadmap. What "done-for-you" actually includes: install,
  configure, monthly review.
- **25:00–30:00 — Q&A + next step.** Pricing (Max, quote-based; Partner floor), the 14-day
  trial, and how to scope it for your firm. *Talk to a service partner.*

> CLE-adjacent framing ("AI without the malpractice risk") is the hook; we make no claim of
> CLE accreditation unless and until a provider grants it.
