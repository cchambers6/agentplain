## 2. Discovery Questions

Ask in sequence; adjust based on what the prospect offers.

1. "How many staff are you working with right now — just partners, or do you have preparers and an admin layer too?"
2. "What does your practice-management setup look like — TaxDome, Karbon, Canopy, or something else?"
3. "During tax season, what is the biggest time drain on your staff that you wish you could get back?"
4. "Walk me through how document chase works today — who owns it, how do you track what's missing, how do you know a client has responded?"
5. "Do you have a month-end bookkeeping client base in addition to tax? How does that reconciliation cycle run?"
6. "When a client calls or emails with a question mid-season, who fields that and how long does it typically take?"
7. "Do you have a formal compliance review step before a return hits your desk, or is your partner review the first pass?"
8. "How do you handle engagement-letter turnaround on new clients? Is there a lag between intake and getting the letter signed?"
9. "What does your AR situation look like? Any clients who consistently pay late?"
10. "What integrations are you running today — Outlook or Google, QuickBooks, DocuSign?"

## 3. Demo Flow

Pick two or three beats based on what the prospect named in discovery. Do not run the full list.

### Beat 1: Tax-season document chase (maps to "the chase cycle is killing us")
Show the month-end-close skill firing on a CPA workspace. The fleet read 23 client files in QuickBooks, ran the missing-document checklist against each engagement, identified the four clients on filing extensions and skipped them, then drafted 19 individualized chase emails — each one citing the specific missing items (K-1, auto-mileage log, fixed-asset additions) for that client. The partner reviews 19 drafts in a tray, approves them, and they go from the partner's own email.

*Tell the value-loop story from content.ts:* March 17, 5:42pm. Eight days to the corporate-return deadline. Twenty-three clients still missing documents. Before: six hours per evening drafting individual chase emails. After: partner reviews 19 drafts in 35 minutes; by noon the next day, 17 clients have responded.

*Tie back:* "You mentioned your staff was still chasing at 9pm during deadline week. This is what that looks like when the fleet runs the cadence overnight."

*Be honest:* The month-end-close skill is live and connected to QuickBooks today. TaxDome and Karbon read the client-document state (received vs. pending) once connected — that deepens the chase logic. The TaxDome and Karbon connections are live at the read layer today (they read workflow and job state); received-doc detection from those systems is Setting Up.

### Beat 2: Client inbound triage (maps to "clients ask questions all day")
Show the Client Inbound agent handling a mid-season client question. The fleet classified the question by engagement type, attached the relevant workpaper or return context, and drafted a response for the partner's review. The client gets a professional, grounded reply — not a hold ticket.

*Tie back:* "You mentioned fielding ten to fifteen client questions a day during season. Every one of those gets a draft for your review — the fleet does the classification and the first pass."

### Beat 3: Month-end books reconciliation (maps to "bookkeeping clients are a grind")
Show the Books Reconciler agent in the tray. The fleet drafted the reconciliation against QuickBooks bank feeds — classified transactions, flagged exceptions. Staff reviews exceptions only; routine entries are already drafted.

*Be honest:* Books Reconciler is Setting Up — it comes online once QuickBooks Online plus the bank feed is connected. Show the agent card with the "Setting Up" badge and explain what unlocks it.

### Beat 4: The compliance angle (maps to "I cannot afford a Circular 230 slip")
"The Compliance Sentinel is loaded with the federal and state checklist. Before a return hits your desk, the sentinel has already run the file against the checklist and flagged the items that need your attention. You review the flagged items; the routine checks are already cleared. It does not state a tax position — ever. It flags; you decide. The attorney-review gate on the per-state corpus is also in place so nothing fires outside its authorized scope."

*Be honest:* The Compliance Sentinel corpus is loaded. Draft scoring activates after counsel review — explain this plainly. "The sentinel is live on the federal side. State-pack activation is gated on a counsel sign-off step we have not completed for every state yet. I will give you a straight answer on which states are active before you commit."

### Beat 5: Setting Up agents (if asked)
Show agent cards honestly. Engagement Onboarding, Document Chase (via TaxDome/Karbon portal), Collections, Milestone Billing are all Setting Up. Explain unlock paths clearly.

"Here is the math. A single staff member spending 25% of their time on document chase across eight weeks of tax season — at $65 an hour loaded — is $10,400 per season, or roughly $42,000 a year when you add the onboarding-letter time and the bookkeeping-reconciliation cycle. ## 5. Top 10 Objections + Responses

### 1. "Isn't this just ChatGPT?"
"ChatGPT is a blank canvas — you describe what you want, type a prompt, copy the output, and stitch it into TaxDome or your email yourself. That takes time and requires you to know what to ask. agentplain is the service that runs it for your practice: agents configured for tax and bookkeeping workflows, connected to your QuickBooks and your inbox, running on a schedule, and dropping review-ready drafts into your tray. We are built on Claude, same family of models, but we do the configuration, the wiring, and the maintenance. You do not write a single prompt."

### 2. "We do not trust AI with our clients."
"That is the right place to start. Nothing in agentplain goes to a client without your approval. The fleet drafts — you review. If you reject a draft, nothing happens. If you approve it, it goes from your own email. The fleet also never states a tax position; every client-facing draft with a quantitative or advisory claim carries a merge field that you, the credentialed preparer, fill in before the message goes. The trust question is really about draft quality — and the answer to that is 14 days of real drafts on your real client files."

### 3. "What about Circular 230 liability? I cannot have AI filing positions."
"The fleet does not file positions. It drafts the client letter, the engagement note, the missing-doc chase — and it defers every tax-position sentence to an operator merge field that you complete before anything goes to the client. The Compliance Sentinel flags returns against the federal and state checklist before they hit your desk — it flags, you decide. The IRS makes the credentialed preparer responsible; the fleet is your first-pass assistant, not your co-signer."

### 4. "We are too busy to set it up, especially during season."
"Setup is 10 to 15 minutes of your time — connecting Outlook and QuickBooks via OAuth. We handle everything else. We deliberately designed setup for firms in the middle of tax season because that is when the value is highest. The first drafts show up in your tray that night. If you are three weeks from the April 15 deadline, those three weeks are exactly when you want this running."

### 5. "What if it sends something wrong?"
"It cannot send anything without your approval. That is not a setting — it is the architecture. Every draft lands in your review tray. You read it, edit if you want, and then you send it from your own system. agentplain never touches your send button and never touches your tax software. If a draft is wrong, you delete it. Nothing moves."

### 6. "We already have TaxDome / Karbon."
"Good — both are part of the integration layer. TaxDome and Karbon connect today and agentplain reads your workflow and job state — so draft quality is grounded in what is actually in your pipeline. The received-document detection from the TaxDome client portal is Setting Up and will deepen the chase logic when it comes online. You keep your practice-management system; agentplain runs on top of it."

### 7. "This is too expensive."
"Let us put a number on the other side. If one staff member spends 25% of their time on document chase over eight weeks — that is $10,400 gone from a $65/hour loaded cost. The Partner-tier seat is $299 a month. If the fleet saves that one staff member four hours of chase work a week during season, you are at a return in the first month. The question is not whether $299 is a lot — it is whether four hours of deadline-week capacity per staff member is worth it."

### 8. "I need to think about it."
"Of course. What is the part you are not settled on — is it the integration question, what happens with Circular 230, or the pricing relative to your season timing? I would rather answer the real question than leave you with an open item."

### 9. "Does it replace my staff?"
"No. The fleet handles the mechanical drafting — document chase, engagement-letter merge, books-reconciliation exceptions. Your preparers still review, sign, and handle every client relationship. The goal is to get your staff off of 6pm deadline-week email drafting and back onto returns that need their judgment. A preparer who spends four fewer hours a week chasing documents is a better-utilized preparer — not a replaced one."

### 10. "What is actually live today?"
"The straight answer: Client Inbound is live — it triages every inbound client question and drafts a grounded response for your review. The month-end-close skill is live — it reads your QuickBooks clients, runs the missing-doc checklist, and drafts individualized chase emails. The Chief of Staff is live once your calendar is connected. TaxDome and Karbon connect at the read layer today. Engagement Onboarding, Document Chase via the client portal, Collections, Milestone Billing, and Books Reconciler are Setting Up; each one has a clear unlock path I can walk you through. I will not sell you something that is not running."

## 8. Win-Back on No Strategy

### When they say no

"Understood. One question before I let you go — is this a not-right-now, or did something not add up about the fit? I want to make sure I understand the gap, not just mark it as a no."

If not-right-now: "What is the milestone that would change the timing — after April 15, after a new hire, after you see how this season goes?"

If not-a-fit: "Fair. What was the deciding factor? I will not try to talk you back, I just want to understand what the gap is so I can be straight with future conversations."

### Tag and drip cadence

Tag: `no-date`, `vertical: cpa`, `reason: [stated]`, `reopen-trigger: [their milestone]`.

Post-no drip:
- Day 30: One brief, factual note about a new integration or a real-firm result. No direct ask.
- Day 60: A short proof-point story tied to the reason they passed (e.g., if they said "too busy during season," write about the deadline-week chase result). Soft ask: "Anything change on your end?"
- Day 90: Check-in on the reopen trigger. Full ask if trigger has fired.

### The re-open trigger

The most reliable re-open triggers for CPA practices:
- After April 15 — firms surface to evaluate what they want to do differently next season.
- New hire — another staff member means another seat of document-chase exposure.
- A missed deadline or a collections problem — both are direct product use cases.

Message for post-season:
"Hi [Name] — season is behind you. This is usually when firms figure out what they want to change for next year. If the document-chase cycle was one of them, I would be glad to show you what this looks like with your actual QuickBooks client list. No pressure — just want to make sure you have the right information before you decide."
