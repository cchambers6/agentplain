## 2. Discovery Questions

Ask in order. Listen for pain; don't pitch until you hear it.

1. "Walk me through a typical storm week — who's handling the phones, the supplements,
   and the homeowner follow-up, and what does that actually look like on the ground?"

2. "When a homeowner doesn't sign within a week of the estimate, what happens? Who
   follows up, how many times, and how often does the deal go cold?"

3. "How are you managing the supplement process today — is there a dedicated person,
   or does it fall to whoever has time?"

4. "What tools are you running for your field service or CRM right now? ServiceTitan,
   Jobber, Housecall Pro, AccuLynx, something else?"

5. "How many leads are you pulling from right now — Google Local Service Ads, Angi,
   HomeAdvisor, door-to-door, referral? And is there a single queue, or are they
   scattered across a few inboxes?"

6. "When a tech calls in an ETA change mid-day, how does that get to the homeowner?"

7. "After a job closes, what does your review and referral process look like — is
   there a system, or does it happen when someone remembers?"

8. "If I were to guess, what's the one back-office bottleneck your office manager
   would name if you asked right now?"

9. "How many seats would this need to cover — just the owner, or are you thinking
   about reps and dispatchers too?"

10. "What does a bad outcome look like for you if you try something like this and
    it doesn't work out? I want to understand what we'd have to earn."

## 3. Demo Flow

Run in this order. Each beat ties to a named pain. Be honest about what is Working
vs Setting Up — the product labels them clearly, and so should you.

### Beat 1: The lead queue (3 minutes)
**Pain addressed:** Lead juggling across 5+ inbound sources

Show the approval queue with inbound leads scored and routed into one view. The
Lead Router is Working today — it scores inbound from any source, routes to the
right rep, and drafts the first-touch reply for owner sign-off.

Say: "This is what the owner or office manager sees. Every lead comes in, gets a
priority score, and the drafted first-touch is sitting there waiting for your
approval. You send it from your own system. We don't send anything automatically."

### Beat 2: Estimate follow-up (4 minutes)
**Pain addressed:** Estimates going cold; manual follow-up that gets skipped

Show the Estimate Followup agent in action — open estimates classified by stage
(fresh / soft-nudge / check-in / last-call / cold), each with a drafted homeowner
message appropriate to that stage. Cold estimates roll into a single rep handoff
with a phone-call ask, not another email.

Be honest: "The follow-up drafts are Working now — the fleet reads your open
estimate records and drafts the right message for each stage. The deeper
integrations with AccuLynx, JobNimbus, ServiceTitan, and Jobber are Setting Up
for later this year. Today it works from the estimate data you give it."

Say: "Every draft has a merge field where price and timeline go — the fleet never
quotes a number. That's always your call."

### Beat 3: The supplement draft (4 minutes)
**Pain addressed:** 30–60% of back-office time at storm-heavy shops; $50K+ reclaim

Show the Supplement agent queue — adjuster scope read, line-item rebuttal drafted
with evidence, queued for owner sign-off before it ever leaves the office.

Be honest: "The Supplement agent is Setting Up — it comes fully online once you
connect your Xactimate or Symbility adjuster scope feed. The draft logic is built
and tested; we're in the integration build window now."

Say: "The reason this matters is the rebuttal gets done every time, on every file,
not just the ones someone had time for. The owner signs it before it goes anywhere."

### Beat 4: TCPA posture (2 minutes — trust close)
**Pain addressed:** Compliance fear; auto-dialers as competitors

Show the approval queue — every single draft sitting pending until the owner acts.

Say: "The TCPA exposure on a non-consented text blast runs $500 per message,
up to $1,500 for willful violations — there's no statutory cap. A couple hundred
numbers is six-figure exposure. agentplain never sends anything automatically.
Every message is a draft waiting for your approval against your own consent records.
That's not a feature we'll change. It's the architecture."

### Beat 5: What's live on day one (2 minutes)
**Pain addressed:** "What do I actually get on day one?"

Show what connects on day one: Gmail or Outlook for email + calendar, QuickBooks
Online for accounting. The cross-vertical fleet (inbox triage, follow-up chaser,
invoice chase autopilot, weekly finance pulse, compliance watch) is Working from
the first day.

Say: "ServiceTitan, Jobber, Housecall Pro, AccuLynx — those are in the integration
roadmap for Q4 2026. We'll be honest with you about where the depth comes from today
versus where it's headed."

## 5. Top 10 Objections + Responses

### 1. "Isn't this just ChatGPT?"

"ChatGPT is a general-purpose tool — it doesn't know your estimate records, your
homeowner cadences, or your TCPA exposure. agentplain is a done-for-you service
built on Claude and configured specifically for trades contractors. We run it for
you. ChatGPT requires someone on your team to know how to use it well and to
actually open it. We already did the configuration work; you just approve the
drafts. That's the difference between a tool in a box and a service that's running."

### 2. "We don't trust AI talking to our customers."

"It doesn't. Nothing leaves your shop without your approval. Every homeowner message,
every estimate nudge, every ETA update — it's a draft sitting in a queue. The AI
does the reading and the writing; your team does the approving and the sending. If
your dispatcher would send it, it goes. If they wouldn't, it doesn't. The fleet has
no outbound capability on its own."

### 3. "What about TCPA? I can't have a system sending texts to homeowners without consent."

"You can't — and that's exactly why we built it the way we did. agentplain doesn't
send anything. Not a text, not an email, not a push. Every message is a draft waiting
in your queue. You approve it and send it from your own system, against your own
consent records. The TCPA risk comes from systems that auto-send. We structurally
can't do that. That's the one promise an auto-dialer or texting platform can't make."

### 4. "We're too busy to set this up."

"Setup is an afternoon, not a project. You connect your Gmail or Outlook, we configure
your vertical, and the fleet starts running. The supplement and FSM integrations
(AccuLynx, ServiceTitan, Jobber) are Setting Up and land later this year — those
don't block you from getting value from the inbox triage and estimate follow-up on
day one. The busiest shops usually benefit the most because the follow-up work gets
done even when nobody has time to do it."

### 5. "What if it sends something wrong?"

"It doesn't send. Every draft is pending in your queue. The fleet proposes; you
decide. If a draft is off — wrong tone, wrong estimate number, wrong homeowner — you
edit it or delete it and move on. The draft never becomes an outbound communication
without a person reading it first."

### 6. "We already have ServiceTitan / Jobber / Housecall Pro."

"Good — those are the tools the fleet is building to connect to. That integration
is in the roadmap for Q4 2026. In the meantime, the fleet works from your email,
your calendar, and your QuickBooks account today. And honestly, the biggest gap most
ServiceTitan shops have isn't in ServiceTitan — it's in the supplement drafting and
the estimate follow-up that falls through when the office gets busy. That part works
now."

### 7. "It's too expensive."

"$199 a month at Partner tier. A single missed supplement on a $15,000 claim can
be a $3,000 to $8,000 miss — one every other month covers the year. If the ROI math
doesn't hold up in your first 30 days, the 14-day money-back guarantee is there, and
we'll show you exactly where the value is or isn't landing before day 30. We'd rather
earn the renewal than argue about the signup."

### 8. "I need to think about it."

"That's fair. What's the part you'd want to think through? If it's the supplement
workflow, I can walk you through exactly how that works in practice. If it's the
pricing, we can size it to one seat first. If it's trust — whether this is real or
vaporware — I'd rather show you than tell you. The 7-day trial puts the fleet in your
shop so you can see it, not take my word for it."

### 9. "Does it replace my office manager?"

"No. It takes the read-and-draft work off their plate — the supplement line-by-line
reading, the estimate follow-up cadence, the homeowner ETA updates. Your office
manager still signs off on every draft, manages the exceptions, and handles the calls
that need a real person. We're not replacing the person; we're getting rid of the part
of the job that burns through their time and doesn't require their judgment."

### 10. "What's actually working today versus what's coming?"

Good question, and an honest answer:

**Working today:**
- Lead Router — scores and routes inbound leads, drafts first-touch replies
- Estimate Followup — walks open estimates by stage, drafts the right homeowner nudge
- Chief of Staff — calendar, inbox, to-do proposals (requires Gmail or Outlook connected)
- Inbox triage, follow-up chaser, invoice chase, weekly finance pulse — all cross-vertical fleet, Working from day one

**Setting Up (integration-dependent):**
- Estimate drafting from EagleView / measurement data (comes online when connected)
- Supplement drafting from Xactimate / Symbility scopes (comes online when connected)
- Dispatch routing from ServiceTitan / Housecall Pro / Jobber (Q4 2026)
- ETA updates from FSM job-state webhooks (Q4 2026)
- Project coordinator — material, crew, inspection sequencing (Q4 2026)
- Review + referral cadence (Q4 2026)

"We show you exactly which agents are Working and which are Setting Up inside the
product. We won't charge you for something that isn't running."

## 8. Win-Back-on-No Strategy

### When the prospect says no (or goes quiet after the sequence)

Do not argue the no. Respect it and set a long-game tag.

**Immediate action:** Tag the contact as "no — [date] — [reason if given]" in your
CRM. Log the most important pain they named in the call notes.

**What not to do:** Do not put them in a generic monthly newsletter drip. Do not
send the same deck again in 60 days. Do not follow up more than once per quarter
without a concrete reason.

---

### Re-open triggers — act on these immediately

These events warrant a personal, timely outreach:

- A major hailstorm or wind event in their service area
- News of the shop adding crew or expanding to a new market
- A job posting for an office manager or production coordinator
- A referral from a current agentplain customer in their trade
- Their current FSM platform (ServiceTitan, Jobber, etc.) announces a price increase

When a trigger fires, reach out within 48 hours. Reference the trigger directly.
Do not pretend the prior conversation didn't happen — acknowledge it:

"I know the timing wasn't right when we talked in [month]. I saw [trigger] and
wanted to reach back out in case the picture has changed."

---

### The long-game rule

A no today is usually a not yet. The supplement pain doesn't go away between
storm seasons — it just isn't urgent. Stay in the category; stay out of the noise.
One honest, value-first touch per quarter is more effective than a monthly drip
they filter out by week three.
