## 2. Discovery Questions

Ask these in sequence, but follow the conversation. Eight to ten is the target — not an interrogation.

1. "Walk me through what a typical Monday morning looks like for you — what is the first thing that pulls you into the inbox?"
2. "How many agents are in your brokerage right now? And are you on Follow Up Boss or Sierra, or something else for your CRM?"
3. "When a buyer inquiry comes in on a weekend — how does that get handled today? Who sees it first?"
4. "What does your showing scheduling process look like? Is anyone coordinating that, or is it ad hoc?"
5. "Do you have a compliance review step before listing descriptions go to MLS? Or does the broker-of-record catch those after?"
6. "How do you handle listing-intake follow-up when a new seller signs? Who owns that first week?"
7. "What does your CRM hygiene look like — is anyone cleaning up stale records, or does that happen in bursts?"
8. "Have you ever had a compliance flag on a listing description or a buyer reply — something that had to get pulled or corrected?"
9. "If you got eight hours back each week, where would that time actually go?"
10. "What tools are you running today — Outlook or Google, DocuSign, QuickBooks?"

## 3. Demo Flow

Match each beat to a pain the prospect named in discovery. Do not show all of this — read the room and pick the three beats that map to what they said.

### Beat 1: The morning review (maps to "inbox chaos / coordination overhead")
Show the approval queue. Overnight, the Buyer Inquiry Router classified every inbound lead — scored by motivation, timeline, and preapproval — and drafted a first-touch reply for each one. The broker sees a clean stack: five drafts, each already in the brokerage's voice, each with a one-line triage note. One tap to approve; it copies to clipboard and deeplinks into their own email client. Nothing sends automatically.

*Tie back:* "You mentioned you spend the first hour every morning sorting through lead emails. This is what that looks like when the fleet has already done the sort."

### Beat 2: Compliance pre-check (maps to "compliance risk / MLS submission")
Show the Compliance Sentinel flag on a listing draft. The sentinel ran the draft against the HUD Fair Housing Act enumerated-phrase set before the listing hit MLS. Flag surfaced with severity level and a suggested rewrite. The broker sees it in the approval queue before submission — not after a call from the broker-of-record.

*Tie back:* "You mentioned the compliance catch usually happens after the listing is already up. This moves that catch to before it leaves the brokerage."

*Be honest here:* The Compliance Sentinel is live and runs on every customer-facing draft. This is real.

### Beat 3: Showing Scheduler in action (maps to "showing coordination time sink")
Show a pending showing proposal. The Showing Scheduler read the buyer's availability against the seller's calendar, proposed three showing windows, and drafted the confirmation to both sides. The agent reviews the time slots, adjusts if needed, and approves. The confirmation goes from the agent's own email.

*Tie back:* "You mentioned your agents are coordinating showings over text and phone calls. This drafts the coordination; the agent still touches it before it goes."

### Beat 4: CRM Hygiene and Production Reporter (if asked — honest about state)
"These two are part of the fleet but they are still Setting Up — they come online once we connect your CRM and your MLS feed. I will show you what the cards look like so you can see the roadmap, but I do not want to demo something that is not live yet."

Show the agent cards with the "Setting Up" badge. Explain the unlock path (CRM connection via API key for Follow Up Boss or Sierra; MLS feed planned for Q3 2026).

### Beat 5: The value-loop story
Tell the Sarah story from content.ts verbatim if it fits: counter-offer lands at 9:14pm, she wakes at 6:30am, fleet has already drafted the response, summarized the buyer's agent thread, and surfaced three comparable closings from Drive. She reviews, edits one number, sends from dotloop. Four minutes instead of forty-five.

*Note for the rep:* dotloop is on the Q3 2026 integration roadmap. Be honest — "the Drive and email pieces are live today; the dotloop connector is in the roadmap."

## 4. Pricing Conversation

### How to present tiers

"agentplain runs on a per-seat model. The Regular tier is $199 per seat to start, and it slides down to $99 per seat as you add more agents — so a 25-agent brokerage is closer to the $99 range. First month is free; month-to-month after that; cancel any time.

If you want named-service-partner time — someone you can reach directly for setup, review, and monthly check-ins — that is the Partner tier, $299 per seat sliding to $199. Honestly, for a brokerage your size I would probably start you on Regular and let you tell me if you want the overlay."

### ROI framing

"Here is the math we have run. At the broker-owner level alone — 8 to 12 hours a week on coordination work, at $120 an hour in opportunity cost because you are a producer — the midpoint is about $5,160 a month. The Regular-tier solo seat is $199. You recover the cost of the product in the first working week. The broader number is around $61,000 a year returned to producing.

That is before the compliance angle. A first-offense Fair Housing Act civil penalty is $26,262. The fleet pre-checks every customer-facing draft before MLS. That downside removal is not in the ROI math — it is separate."

### Handling "is it per seat?"

"Yes, per seat means per workspace user. So if you have eight agents plus yourself, that is nine seats. At the Regular tier that is $199 per seat — first month free across all of them. The per-seat price scales down as you add seats, so a larger brokerage pays less per person. Happy to work through the math for your headcount."

### Trial

"The 7-day trial starts when you connect your first integration — usually Gmail or Outlook, which takes about five minutes. The fleet starts working that day. You see real drafts in your approval queue by morning."

## 5. Top 10 Objections + Responses

### 1. "Isn't this just ChatGPT?"
"That is the right question. ChatGPT is a general tool — you write the prompts, you copy-paste the output, you stitch it into your inbox and your CRM. agentplain is the service that runs that for you: pre-built agents for real estate specifically, connected to your inbox and your CRM, running on a schedule, dropping drafts into your approval queue. We are built on Claude, same underlying model family, but configured, wired, and maintained by us. You do not write a single prompt."

### 2. "We do not trust AI with our clients."
"That is a sound instinct, and honestly it is the right frame to start with. Nothing in agentplain goes to a client without you approving it first. Every draft lands in a queue. You read it, edit it if you want, then it copies to your own email client and you send it. The fleet drafts; you decide. If you never approve a single draft, nothing leaves the brokerage. The trust question is really about whether you trust the draft quality — and the answer to that is the free trial."

### 3. "What about fair housing exposure? I cannot have AI writing listing descriptions."
"That is exactly why the Compliance Sentinel exists. The fleet does not publish listing descriptions — it drafts them, and then the sentinel runs a pre-check against the HUD Fair Housing Act enumerated-phrase set before the draft ever reaches MLS. If there is a flag, it surfaces with severity and a suggested rewrite. The broker-of-record still reviews; the AI does the first pass to catch what a tired agent misses at 11pm. The flag catches the problem before it becomes a $26,000 civil penalty."

### 4. "We are too busy to set it up."
"Setup is mostly us, not you. The two integrations that matter most — your email and your CRM — take about 15 minutes to connect via OAuth or an API key. After that, the fleet starts running. You do not configure agents or write prompts. The one thing that requires your time is reviewing drafts, and that is the work you would have been doing anyway, just less of it."

### 5. "What if it sends something wrong?"
"It cannot send anything without your approval. That is a hard constraint, not a setting. Every draft lands as a pending item in your approval queue. You approve it, it copies to clipboard, and you send it from your own system. agentplain never touches your send button. If a draft is wrong, you edit it or delete it — nothing happens automatically."

### 6. "We already have Follow Up Boss / Sierra."
"Good — those are the two CRM integrations that are live today. agentplain connects to Follow Up Boss and Sierra via an API key, reads your leads, triages each one, drafts first-touch replies, and writes the triage decision back as a note and tag. You keep your CRM; agentplain runs on top of it."

### 7. "This is too expensive."
"Let me put a number on the other side. If you are spending 8 hours a week on coordination work — just you, not your agents — that is $120 an hour in opportunity cost because every hour you spend on coordination is an hour you are not in front of a client. That is $4,000 a month. The Regular-tier seat is $199. The question is not whether $199 is a lot; it is whether getting four to six hours of producing time back per week is worth it."

### 8. "I need to think about it."
"That is fair. What would make this a clearer call for you — is it the integration question, the pricing, or something you are not sure about yet on what it actually does? I would rather get you to a clean yes or no than leave you with open questions."

### 9. "Does it replace my staff?"
"No, and that is deliberate. The fleet drafts coordination work — lead replies, showing proposals, compliance pre-checks. Your agents still approve, send, and handle every client conversation. The goal is to get them out of the inbox and back into the field. An agent who spends four fewer hours a week on email is a better-producing agent — not a replaced one."

### 10. "What is actually live today?"
"Good question, and I want to be straight with you. Three agents are live and running on real data today: the Buyer Inquiry Router, the Showing Scheduler, and the Compliance Sentinel. The Chief of Staff is live once your calendar is connected. The CRM Hygiene agent, the Production Reporter, and the Recruiter Assistant are Setting Up — they come online once the CRM and MLS feed connections are built, which is on our Q3 2026 roadmap. The three live agents are the core of the daily value loop. The Setting Up agents are real, not vaporware; they just need the integration unlocked."

## 8. Win-Back on No Strategy

### When they say no

Do not argue. Do not discount. Say:

"Completely understand. Can I ask — is this a not-right-now or a this-is-not-for-us? Just so I know how to treat the follow-up."

If not-right-now: "I will check back in 90 days. Is there a specific milestone — a new hire, a MLS integration, tax season ending — where this becomes more relevant?"

If this-is-not-for-us: "Fair. What was the deciding factor? I am not going to push you, I just want to make sure I understand what the gap was."

### Tag and drip cadence

Tag the contact: `no-date`, `vertical: real-estate`, `reason: [what they said]`, `reopen-trigger: [their milestone if named]`.

Drip cadence after no:
- Day 30: One factual update on a new integration or a real-case proof point. No ask.
- Day 60: One case-study-style story (one paragraph, real situation, real result). Soft ask: "Anything change on the timing?"
- Day 90: Check-in on the reopen trigger if one was named. Full ask if trigger has fired.

### The re-open trigger

The single most reliable re-open trigger for real estate: a compliance incident, a fair housing complaint, or a new hire they need to get productive fast. Set a Google Alert for the brokerage name + "complaint" if the firm is large enough to generate press, and watch LinkedIn for new-agent announcements. When a trigger fires, lead with the relevant capability — not "just checking in."

"Hi [Name] — saw you added two new agents. The onboarding load usually doubles with each new hire. Happy to show you how the fleet handles the first-90-days inbox volume if the timing is better now."
