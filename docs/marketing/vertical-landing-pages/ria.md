## What the quarter actually feels like

- **Prep is 30 to 60 minutes per meeting.** You pull the same client across the
  CRM, the planning software, and the custodian, every single time, the day
  before every single meeting.
- **The recap waits, then it slips.** Post-meeting notes and the task list are
  memory-dependent and often delayed. The longer it waits, the more it costs you.
- **You read every outbound, and it is still slow and inconsistent.** Approving a
  client communication means reading it cold, against the Marketing Rule, with no
  first-pass layer in front of you.
- **The inbound never stops.** Client questions arrive all day. You answer when
  they surface, from memory or by digging through the CRM, with no triage layer
  catching them first.

## What Plaino does for advisors

Plaino is your named service partner inside the workspace. It works the way the
fleet works everywhere at agentplain: it reads, it drafts, it proposes, and it
puts the result in your approval queue. It never sends.

**It replaces the prep-and-document cycle.** The prep work drafts the agenda, the
delta since the last meeting, and the open task list per client. The note work
drafts the recap and task list from your meeting capture. You review and route.

**It augments your review on every client-facing communication** with a
fiduciary-aware compliance pass — an ADV, suitability, and plain-English check
before anything goes out.

**It triages the inbound** — classifying each client question, attaching the
account context, and drafting the response for your sign-off.

### The value loop, concretely

Take a Q1 quarterly review cycle: 87 client portfolios to discuss, 87 prep
packets to assemble.

- **Before:** pull each client's positions, run the planning model, draft a
  one-page review summary with the highlights and risks, build the agenda,
  schedule the call. Around 90 minutes per packet, across 87 clients.
- **With the fleet:** Plaino drafts the 87 one-page review packets with portfolio
  drift highlighted, surfaces the 11 clients with planning-assumption changes
  worth raising, and queues the scheduling. Every claim cites its source, and the
  output is built to be archiveable.
- **The outcome:** you review 87 drafts across two mornings instead of three
  weeks. Calls happen on schedule. You walk in with prep, not catch-up.

## What Plaino does not do

Honesty is the whole point of a fiduciary-aware tool. So here is the line, drawn
plainly.

- **It never states an investment recommendation.** Every quantitative or
  forward-looking claim in a draft is an `{{advisor}}` merge field. You fill it,
  or you do not.
- **It never renders dollar amounts.** Numbers are yours to confirm, never the
  fleet's to invent.
- **It never sends, and it never moves money.** Every output is a pending
  approval. Your own system performs any action you approve.
- **Portfolio and CRM sync are still setting up.** Today the quarterly-update work
  accepts a portfolio snapshot plus your advisor notes. Orion, Black Diamond, and
  Tamarac (portfolio) and Redtail and Wealthbox (CRM) connect as those systems
  come online — connect your portfolio and CRM systems as they do.

sourced drafts that fit into it.

**I am a solo RIA. Is this for ensemble firms only?**
It is for both. A solo advisor gets the cross-vertical fleet on their inbox from
