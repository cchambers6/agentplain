# 00 — Cohort shortlist (template — Conner fills in the names)

**Date:** 2026-07-03 · **Lane:** real estate, Georgia (the only active lane per `docs/sales/deep-dive-2026-07-02/01-named-icp-per-vertical.md`)
**Rule:** no name goes on this sheet unless it clears the ICP filters (independent brokerage, 5–40 agents, broker-owner active in operations, Georgia-first) and none of the three disqualifiers (franchise-locked stack, tech-forward automation shop, SOC 2 procurement gate).

This file is a worksheet, not a database. The CRM of record (document 03 of the sales deep-dive) holds ongoing state; this sheet exists so the first five names get chosen deliberately, on paper, before the first Monday block.

**Selection order (from the design-partner program doc):** named warm paths from the FlatSBO-adjacent network first, community-adjacent warm second, cold roster names last. A warm intro to one Georgia broker-owner is worth more than five cold sends.

---

## How to fill each field

| Field | How to find it |
|---|---|
| **Brokerage** | Georgia Real Estate Commission license rolls (free, complete census of GA brokerages with broker-of-record names), GAMLS/FMLS office rosters, local association directories (Cobb, Atlanta Realtors, etc.). Do not start in Apollo — the rosters are better coverage for this exact population. |
| **Name / role** | Broker of record from the GREC roll; confirm the person is operationally active (recent listings under their own name, association committee roles, posts about running the office) — not an absentee investor. |
| **LinkedIn** | Search "{{name}} {{brokerage}}" directly; Apollo enrichment fills gaps (email + LinkedIn) once the name is chosen. Note profile activity level — it decides whether the LinkedIn-first variant (email 1b) applies. |
| **Referral path or cold** | Work the warm map by hand first: FlatSBO-adjacent contacts, closing attorneys, lenders, past colleagues. Write the referrer's actual name or the word "cold" — this column decides which email variant to use and is the highest-value field on the sheet. |
| **Why them (one line)** | Evidence they carry the coordination load personally: they answer their own listing inquiries, they post at 9pm, the office has one drowning admin, they mention QuickBooks or doc-chasing pain anywhere public. Write the evidence, not the hope. |
| **Custom hook (one line)** | One true, specific, recent thing: a talk they gave, a newsletter they write, a market post, an association role, a mutual contact's comment. This becomes `{{custom-hook}}` in the email. If nothing true exists, the hook is the referrer or the roster fact — never invent one. |
| **Expected objection** | Pick the most likely from the discovery playbook library (doc 04 §6): usually #1 "you have no customers," #16 "my CRM isn't integrated," or #17 "no time to review drafts." Pre-reading one likely objection beats memorizing twenty. |
