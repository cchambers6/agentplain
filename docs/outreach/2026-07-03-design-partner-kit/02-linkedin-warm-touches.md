# 02 — LinkedIn warm touches

**When LinkedIn goes first:** the prospect is active on LinkedIn (posts or comments in the last month) and there's no warm email path. The LinkedIn note warms the ground; email variant (b) follows 3–7 days later, accepted or not.
**When to skip LinkedIn:** dormant profile, or a warm referral exists — go straight to email variant (a). A referral beats any platform touch.

Rules: 2–3 sentences. No pitch deck energy, no link, no ask for time in the connection request itself. Connection-request notes cap at 300 characters — the drafts below fit. Same truth rules as email: nothing invented, no customer implications, vendor invisible.

---

## Connection requests (pick by angle)

**General / roster-sourced (pairs with email variant b or c):**

> Hi {{first-name}} — I'm building agentplain, a service that drafts the chasing-and-follow-up work for Georgia broker-owners. No pitch in this note; I'd like to connect with the people running independent shops here.

**Referral-adjacent (a warm name exists but hasn't made the intro yet):**

> Hi {{first-name}} — we know {{referrer-name}} in common, and your name comes up when I ask who actually runs a tight independent shop around {{city}}. I'd like to connect.

**Event / speaker angle (pairs with email variant e):**

> Hi {{first-name}} — saw you're speaking at {{event-name}} on {{talk-topic}}. I work on taking the drafting-and-chasing load off Georgia broker-owners, so your topic is close to home. I'd like to connect.

**Newsletter / content angle (pairs with email variant d):**

> Hi {{first-name}} — your piece on {{newsletter-topic}} said something I hear from broker-owners all over Georgia: {{one-specific-point}}. I'd like to connect.

---

## After they accept (send within 2 days, then email follows)

> Thanks for connecting, {{first-name}}. Short version of what I do: agentplain reads the tools a brokerage already runs on — email, calendar, QuickBooks — and drafts the follow-ups, doc chases, and invoices so the owner just reviews and sends. I'm signing a small founding cohort of Georgia broker-owners right now, and I'll send you the honest one-paragraph version by email rather than pitch you in a DM.

Then send email variant (b) referencing this exchange. If they reply in the DM instead, answer there and move to the warm-yes booking reply (document 03) as soon as they show interest.

## If they don't accept

No second connection request, ever. Send email variant (b) on schedule — "I sent you a short note on LinkedIn" is still true — and let the follow-up chain (document 03) do its work.

## Log it

Every LinkedIn action gets a CRM line the same day: CONNECT-SENT, CONNECTED, or DM-REPLY, with the date. The Monday block (document 06) reads these to time the email sends.
