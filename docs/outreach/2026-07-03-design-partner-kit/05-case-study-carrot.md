# 05 — The case-study carrot (founding-partner offer letter)

**What this is:** the written version of the design-partner offer, sent within 24 hours of a yes-leaning discovery call. It puts every term on paper before anyone commits — the asks included, so nothing is sprung later. The prospect should be able to forward this to a business partner or spouse and have it hold up.

**Terms authority:** `CLAIMS_GROUND_TRUTH.md` §5 and `docs/sales/deep-dive-2026-07-02/02-design-partner-program.md`. Where this letter and those disagree, they win.

**Legal status:** a **counsel-reviewed short-form agreement is to follow** — the real document waits on counsel. Until it exists, the pilot starts on the standard terms of service plus this letter. Nothing in this letter is a contract and it says so.

---

## The letter (email, plain text, from Conner)

**Subject:** `the founding-partner terms, in writing`

```
Hi {{first-name}},

Good call today. As promised, here's the whole deal in writing —
including what I'm asking of you, because I'd rather you see the
full shape of it now than discover it later.

WHAT YOU GET

- The service, free for your first 3 months. We install it, connect
  your tools (email, calendar, QuickBooks, DocuSign), and run it.
  Every draft waits in your queue for your approval — nothing sends
  without your name on it.
- A weekly 30-minute call with me for the length of the pilot. Your
  stack, what's working, what isn't. This is founder time no later
  customer will get.
- Early access to new features, and a direct line into what gets
  built next. The weekly call is where the roadmap gets argued.
- A joint case study, co-authored. You approve every word before
  anything becomes public, and you keep edit rights on your quotes.

WHAT I ASK BACK

- That weekly 30 minutes, honestly spent. I record the calls (with
  your okay) so I can listen instead of taking notes.
- An on-record testimonial once — and only if — you've seen real
  value. If the service doesn't earn it, you don't give it.
- Willingness to take an occasional reference call from a future
  prospect, and a short on-record quote each quarter, wording yours.

THE TIMELINE

- Months 1–3: free pilot. Week 1: your tools connected, first drafts
  in your queue, and we define the measurement together — hours
  reclaimed and drafts approved, counted by the system, not by
  memory.
- End of month 3: you convert to standard month-to-month pricing for
  your tier, published on our pricing page — or you walk, no
  obligation and no hard feelings. Either party can end the pilot
  early with 14 days' notice.

WHAT SUCCESS MEANS

We'll set the number together in week 1, from your own words about
where your hours go. The pilot succeeds when the case study writes
itself from measured results — and if by month 3 the numbers haven't
earned your testimonial, the honest outcome is you tell me why,
on or off the record, and that's still worth the three months to me.

THE PAPERWORK

This letter is the plain-English version, not a contract. A short
agreement covering the case-study rights and confidentiality is
being reviewed by counsel and will follow; until then the pilot
runs on our standard terms of service plus what's written here.
Your client data never appears in any case study in identifiable
form, full stop.

If you're in, reply "in" and I'll send the kickoff invite and the
week-1 setup checklist.

Conner Chambers
Founder, agentplain
```

---

## Rules for using this letter

- **Send it only after a discovery call.** This letter confirms a conversation; it doesn't replace one.
- **Do not edit the terms per prospect.** Free months, call cadence, and the asks are program constants. If a prospect negotiates scope, that's a Max/Custom conversation, not a design-partner edit.
- **The success criterion stays unnumbered in the letter.** The specific target is set in week 1 from their own hours, per the pilot operating shape — a number invented before measurement exists would violate the no-fabricated-stats rule this program lives on.
- **Do not promise a start date until the pilot-activation gate is confirmed** (production readiness is sequenced with engineering — see the executive plan §1). "Kickoff invite" timing follows that gate, not enthusiasm.
- **When counsel's short-form agreement lands,** it replaces the paperwork paragraph and gets sent with this letter thereafter. Track that swap in this file.
