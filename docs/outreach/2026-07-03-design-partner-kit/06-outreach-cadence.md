# 06 — The Monday block (Conner's outreach cadence)

**One block, 60–90 minutes, same time every Monday, on the calendar as a recurring event.** This is the rhythm the whole sales plan hangs on: 5 sends a week sustained beats 50 sends once. The block runs on what's in this folder plus the CRM — **zero fleet dependencies during the block.** If fleet-drafted personalizations are waiting in the approval queue, use them as raw material; if they aren't, the block runs anyway. Nothing here waits on anything.

**Weekly founder budget check:** this block plus the Friday 15-minute pipeline review is the ~2 hrs/week pre-pilot commitment from the executive plan. Active pilots add their 30-minute calls on top — separate calendar entries, not part of this block.

---

## Order of operations

### 1. Review the CRM (10 min)

Open the CRM of record (stood up by the send-path work). Three passes, in order:

- **Replies since last Monday** — anything warm gets the warm-yes booking reply (document 03) *first, before any new outreach*. A live reply outranks every planned send.
- **Follow-ups due** — rows at day 5 / 12 / 21 since last touch. Queue them for step 3.
- **Stage sanity** — any row without a next action and a date gets one now, or gets closed.

### 2. Pick this week's 5 (10 min)

From the shortlist (document 00) or its CRM successor. Priority order is fixed: **named warm path → community-adjacent warm → cold roster.** For each of the five, confirm the row is complete: variant chosen, custom hook true and current, expected objection noted. A name whose hook has gone stale (the event passed, the newsletter angle aged out) gets a fresh hook or gets swapped out.

### 3. Edit and send (30–50 min)

For each of the five, plus due follow-ups:

1. Copy the variant from document 01 (or 03 for follow-ups) into a draft in your own inbox.
2. Personalize every token. Then **search the draft for `{{`** — zero hits or no send.
3. Run the pre-send checks (document 01): truth scan, autonomy test, across-the-counter test.
4. Send. Log it in the CRM before opening the next draft — stage, date, variant. Same sitting, no batching the logging for later; unlogged sends are how pipelines rot.

If the block runs short on time, **follow-ups beat new first touches** — momentum with a name that knows you outranks a fresh cold send.

### 4. Book next Monday (5 min)

- Confirm next Monday's block is on the calendar (it should already recur — verify, don't assume).
- Note anything for the Friday review: replies expected, calls to prep (each booked call needs a briefing doc from document 04 before it happens).
- If the week produced a discovery call, block the 10-minute briefing read directly before the call slot, now, while you're in the calendar.

---

## Rules that keep the rhythm honest

- **Send day is Monday; reply-handling is same-day, all week.** A warm reply on Wednesday doesn't wait five days for the next block — 4 business hours is the target on anything warm.
- **Five sends means five.** Not fifteen on a motivated week — the follow-up load compounds, and the conversion path (booking, briefing, call, letter) has to keep up with the front end. Volume grows only when the weekly rhythm has held for a month and the reply handling hasn't slipped.
- **Every send is Conner-reviewed and Conner-sent.** The fleet drafts and researches; the founder edits and sends from his own inbox. This is the same draft-and-approve pattern we sell — the outreach motion is itself the first demo.
- **The block never blocks.** No dependency on fleet runs, product deploys, or anyone else's calendar. Everything needed lives in this folder and the CRM. If either is unavailable, that's the only acceptable reason the block slips — and it moves to Tuesday, not to "later."
- **When all 5 founding-partner slots are signed, this document retires** and the cadence hands off to the standard pipeline motion (document 06 of the sales deep-dive). Don't keep first-touch outreach running past a full cohort.
