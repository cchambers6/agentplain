# Days 4–5 — the first Friday check-in

**Day 4 (Thursday) is prep; Day 5 (Friday) is the first weekly call of the pilot.** This call sets the template for the next eleven: it runs on evidence, not vibes; the partner talks more than Conner; and it produces three artifacts every week — a decision (what changes next week), a feedback capture (logged verbatim), and case-study accrual (see `04-DAY-5-case-study-material-capture.md`, which runs *inside* this same call).

---

## Day 4 — Thursday prep (Fable drafts, Conner reviews; 30 min total)

1. **The one-page call brief**, from the week's actual data:
   - Approvals: N approved / N edited / N rejected, by day, with the morning-open pattern.
   - Every lead-triage run: lead → time caught → draft latency → what happened to it.
   - Saved-time ledger total for the week, in calibrated minutes, with the per-action breakdown. (If the ledger disagrees with visible activity, fix or flag *before* the call — the first number this partner ever sees must survive scrutiny; Truth Wave applies hardest to our own reporting.)
   - Edits digest: what they changed, in three bullets — this is the "what it's learning" story.
   - Anything that broke, and what we did about it (including anything we already told them about mid-week — no Friday surprises in either direction).
2. **Confirm the Friday weekly report email fires** to the partner Friday morning — the call's opening question refers to it, so its non-arrival would open the call with an own-goal. Verify Thursday, not Friday 8am.
3. **The week-2 proposal, drafted:** one concrete adjustment based on the week (a preference to encode, a draft template to fix, QBO connect if invoice pain surfaced, briefing time shift if mornings aren't landing). One, not five — the call should end with a single visible change, and next week we tell them it changed because of them.
4. **Case-study capture prep** per `04-…material-capture.md`: the "before" fields from Day 1 open beside the week's numbers, the consent line ready.

## Day 5 — the 30-minute call

Recording consent, as always. Target talk ratio: partner >60% in the first half.

| Time | Block |
|---|---|
| 0–2 | Open: "You got Plaino's report this morning — is it lying?" (Their reaction to the weekly email is itself the product feedback.) |
| 2–15 | Their week, their words (the five questions below — follow the pain, not the order) |
| 15–22 | The data walk (what we show them, below) |
| 22–27 | Case-study capture — quote, numbers, consent (doc 04) |
| 27–30 | The week-2 change, agreed; confirm next Friday; done |

### What Conner asks (and then actually stops talking)

1. **"Walk me through the moment it was most useful this week — and the moment it was most annoying."** Both halves mandatory. The annoying half is the roadmap; the useful half is the case study.
2. **"When the [lead name] draft landed — what did you do, and what did you almost do instead?"** Anchored to a real event from the brief, never hypothetical. Gets at whether the habit is forming and where the friction lives.
3. **"You edited [specific edit] — say more about that."** Their articulated reasoning teaches us more than the edit itself; it's also the voice-calibration input for week 2.
4. **"What did you NOT trust it with this week?"** The trust boundary is the product's actual frontier for this partner. Whatever they name is either a week-2 fix, a roadmap item, or a thing to explicitly leave human — and saying "that should stay yours" out loud builds more trust than promising to automate it.
5. **"If the pilot ended today, what would you tell another broker it actually did?"** The dress rehearsal for the week-4 habit question and the eventual on-record quote — their unpolished answer now is the baseline the case study grows from.

Plus the standing close: **"What should we build or fix for you next week?"** — the design-partner promise ("your voice shapes the product") made operational, every single week.

### What Conner shows them (the data walk, 7 minutes, screens shared)

1. **Their approvals ledger for the week** — every item, what happened to it. No cherry-picking; the rejected drafts are on screen too, framed as "this is it learning."
2. **The saved-time number, with its honesty label:** "Plaino counts [N] minutes this week — that's per-action time credits for work it actually completed, counted conservatively. Does that match your gut?" Their gut-check response goes in the notes verbatim; if they think the number is high, *that conversation is gold* — recalibrate or explain, never defend.
3. **One draft, before and after their edit**, side by side — the visible learning loop.
4. **The Connections tab** — everything green, or the honest state of anything that isn't.

What we do NOT show: internal dashboards, spend/cost data, anything with a vendor name in it, or projections of future value. The wall between "what happened" (shown, sourced) and "what might happen" (not shown, not fabricated) is the Truth Wave applied to a screen-share.

### First feedback capture (the discipline, not the vibe)

Same day, into the partner folder and the memory inbox:

- **Verbatims, labeled:** quote-worthy praise (→ case-study file), friction (→ ranked Product/Engineering list), feature asks (→ logged with their why — the ask behind the ask, per discovery discipline).
- **The Friday synthesis paragraph** (CS plan, kaizen 06 fix 3): what the partner said, what broke, what we're fixing — one paragraph to the memory inbox, one personal line back to the partner ("heard you on X — here's what changes Monday").
- **The scoreboard line:** approvals this week (the number that predicts everything at this stage), against the week-1 healthy bar of ≥5 including edits and rejections.
- **Feature asks we won't build** get the honest treatment on the call itself, not a fake "great idea, roadmap!": see `05-when-things-go-wrong.md` §5.

## Exit criteria for week 1 (write the checkmarks, not the feelings)

1. ☐ Friday call held; partner did most of the talking; next Friday confirmed.
2. ☐ ≥5 approvals on the week (edits and rejections count — silence is the only dead signal).
3. ☐ One agreed week-2 change, named, with Conner on the hook to report it done next Friday.
4. ☐ Feedback captured and routed same-day (verbatims, ranked frictions, synthesis paragraph).
5. ☐ Case-study artifacts from doc 04 captured — or their absence explained honestly in the file ("no quotable moment yet" is a legitimate week-1 state; a fabricated one is not).

If the week finished under the healthy bar (approvals <5, or the partner missed the call): the graduated response ladder and save-motion in `05-when-things-go-wrong.md` §3 take over — inside week 1, the save-motion is cheap, the relationship is warm, and eleven pilot weeks remain to fix what the data surfaced.
