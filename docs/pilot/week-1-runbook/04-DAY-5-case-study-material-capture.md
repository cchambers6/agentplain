# Day 5 — case-study material capture (inside the Friday call)

**Where this lives:** minutes 22–27 of the Day-5 call (`03-DAY-4-5-first-check-in.md`). Five minutes, on the record, with consent — not a separate ceremony. The case study is written forward from Day 1 (the "before" fields were filled on the activation call); Friday's job is to start filling "after" with artifacts that survive publication review.

**Rule zero, inherited whole from `docs/sales/deep-dive-2026-07-02/05-case-study-template.md`:** every case study is earned from a real partner, with written permission, with numbers from instrumentation. Nothing publishes with an unfilled field or an unchecked permission box. Week 1 produces *material*, not a case study — publishing anything this early would be a Truth Wave violation even if the material is glowing.

---

## Consent framing (say it, don't bury it)

The partner already granted case-study rights in the signed letter, so this is re-consent in the moment — which is exactly why it builds trust instead of spending it:

> "Part of our deal is the case study you approve word by word. I want to start collecting the raw material while it's fresh instead of reconstructing it in month three. So: this call is recorded, and if you say something worth quoting I'll transcribe it, send it back to you in writing, and nothing gets used anywhere until you've approved that exact wording. And if you'd rather a thing stay off the record, say 'off the record' and it's gone. Good?"

Standing rules under that framing: quotes are **said by the partner and transcribed** — never drafted *for* them to sign. Approval is of the exact text, in writing, dated. No end-client of the partner (buyer, seller, lead) is ever identifiable, directly or by inference — scrub names from every artifact at capture time, not at publish time. Off-the-record requests are honored instantly and permanently.

## The three artifacts to capture

### 1. The quote (aim it, don't wait for it)

The most valuable quote category, per the template: **why the approval gate satisfied them** — a liability-conscious broker saying on record why they trust the arrangement outranks any superlative. Second-best: a concrete moment ("a lead came in at 9pm Tuesday and the reply was waiting for me at breakfast").

How to get it without leading the witness: the Day-5 question set already produces it. Question 1 ("the moment it was most useful") and question 5 ("what would you tell another broker") are the quote mines. When something quotable lands, flag it live: *"That — can I have that for the case study, in your words? I'll send you the transcription to approve."* Log the timestamp in the call notes so the transcription is exact.

What is NOT a quote: paraphrase, composite, "cleaned up" sentiment, or anything the partner didn't actually say. Light grammar tidying is fine **in the version sent back for approval** — they approve the tidied text, which then becomes the verbatim of record.

**Week-1 honesty check:** if nothing quotable happened yet, the file records "no quotable moment yet — revisit week 2" and that is a fine outcome. A forced week-1 quote reads forced forever.

### 2. The quantified outcome (with the methodology footnote attached at capture time)

Capture the week's instrumented numbers into the case-study file — never recollections, never projections:

- **Drafts landed in their queue: N** (source: workspace instrumentation).
- **Approval rate: N%** — approved / (approved + rejected), same source.
- **Hours reclaimed: N over the week** (source: saved-time ledger, per-action calibration table). This number is only as honest as the writers behind it — the Day-2/3 check already verifies the ledger fires on the lead-triage path; if any calibrated action ran without writing, the number carries an asterisk in the file until Engineering closes it.
- **Vertical-specific outcome only if real dollars rode a real payload** — nothing synthesized, no "estimated commission impact." Speed-to-lead facts are fine as *facts* ("evening leads got a drafted reply before morning, N times this week"); conversion claims we can't source are not.

**The methodology footnote is written into the file now, next to the numbers** — not retrofitted at publish time (template requirement, adapted verbatim):

> *"Hours reclaimed are computed from per-action time credits calibrated conservatively by agentplain (calibration table: `lib/guarantee/` on the date of publication), counted only for actions the fleet actually completed in [partner]'s workspace between [date] and [date]. [Partner] reviewed and approved these figures. Individual results vary."*

The "[Partner] reviewed and approved these figures" clause is earned on this very call — the data walk in doc 03 shows them the saved-time number and asks if it matches their gut. Record their answer; that's the review.

### 3. The before/after workflow (the narrative spine)

- **Before** is already in the file from Day 1: the concrete Tuesday-night scene in their own words — what the owner personally did with an evening lead, how long it took, what slipped. Do not rewrite it; it's a primary source now.
- **After**, captured Friday in the same shape: what happens to an evening lead *this* week, step by step, in their words. Prompt: "Walk me through what happened with [the real lead from the brief] — from when it came in to when the reply went out." Same scene, same specificity, real names scrubbed.
- **The delta sentence, theirs not ours:** "So what's the difference between that and three weeks ago?" Their phrasing of the before/after is worth more than any polished version marketing could write — capture it raw.

## After the call (same day, 20 min)

1. Transcribe the flagged quote(s); send back for written approval: *"As promised — the exact wording I'd like to use. Edit anything; nothing moves without your yes."* Log the approval date in the permissions checklist when it comes.
2. File the week's numbers + methodology footnote into the case-study "after" section, dated, with query sources noted so week 12's numbers are produced the same way.
3. Update the permissions checklist honestly — most boxes stay unchecked in week 1 (signed letter ✓; this week's quote approval pending; logo, final-text, and named-person approvals all still open). Unchecked boxes are the system working, not a gap.
4. One line to the file's header: what the study still needs (the running shot-list — e.g., "need: the approval-gate quote; a dollar-real outcome; week-4 habit evidence"). This list is what Conner aims the next eleven Fridays at.

**Publish path reminder (for month 3, not now):** partner-approved text → `/customers/[slug]` (route built only when the first study is real) → vertical landing-page pull-quote → founder-voice LinkedIn post with partner pre-approval → their association newsletter if they want it. Nothing on that path starts in week 1.
