# Adjacent / Platform-Level Threats (Horizon 4)
**agentplain · 2026-06-06**

_The biggest long-term risk is not the vertical incumbents — it is the platforms a local-business owner already has, or could adopt in one click. Live web research, June 2026; URLs cited inline with access date._

---

## The encroachment thesis

Vertical incumbents (Horizon 1) are racing toward auto-execution, which keeps them in a different quadrant from agentplain (see `LANDSCAPE.md`). The platforms below are the opposite danger: they sit in agentplain's *own* quadrant — generic + approve-gated — and are drifting **rightward** toward vertical depth. They have more capital, more distribution, more connectors, and (in one case) they supply the model agentplain runs on.

---

## 1. Generic ChatGPT / Claude used directly

A non-technical owner can already do a large slice of agentplain's read → categorize → draft loop today, cheaply:

- **ChatGPT Business** — $20/user/mo annual ($25 monthly, 2-seat min), 60+ connectors incl. Google Drive, SharePoint, Slack, Notion, Dropbox, plus Projects/GPTs (https://chatgpt.com/pricing/, accessed 2026-06-06).
- **Claude Pro** — $17/mo annual / $20 monthly; **Claude Team** $20/seat annual (5-seat min); both now bundle **Claude Cowork** (https://claude.com/pricing, accessed 2026-06-06).

**Gap vs. agentplain:** it's a blank chatbox — no vertical compliance corpus, no named done-for-you partner, no unattended-but-approved back-office sweep across all tools. The owner must drive it themselves. **Close on drafting; far on service + liability posture.**

## 2. Anthropic's "Claude for Small Business" — the scariest finding, and it is real

Anthropic shipped **"Claude for Small Business" on 2026-05-13**: 15 ready-to-run agentic workflows + 15 skills across finance/ops/sales/marketing/HR/customer-service, with connectors to **QuickBooks, PayPal, HubSpot, Canva, DocuSign, Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, Slack** — running inside Claude Cowork, **included free for any paying Claude subscriber** (https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-for-small-business, accessed 2026-06-06).

Decisively, it adopts **agentplain's exact posture**: *"You approve the plan first or… let it run end-to-end"* — **human-approval-before-send is now a platform default, not a differentiator.** Paired with a free PayPal AI-fluency course and a 10-city SMB workshop tour (Chicago, Tulsa, Dallas, etc.) with a free Max month (https://www.selfemployed.com/news/anthropic-claude-for-small-business-launch-2026/, accessed 2026-06-06).

**Why this matters most:** agentplain is built *on* Anthropic. The supplier just moved downmarket into agentplain's customer, at $0 marginal cost to a Claude subscriber, with the same approve-before-send loop and overlapping connectors.

**What still protects agentplain (per third-party review, https://digitalstrategy-ai.com/claude-for-small-business-review, accessed 2026-06-06):**
- **Vertical compliance:** the review explicitly notes Claude SMB has "no discussion" of vertical compliance and excludes strict-no-AI healthcare/legal. agentplain's counsel-reviewed corpus is the gap.
- **Done-for-you service:** Claude SMB is explicitly DIY/self-serve — the owner authenticates connectors and picks workflows themselves; non-technical owners find the hand-holding lacking. Plaino-as-service is the gap.
- **Cross-tool unattended-but-approved back office** tuned per vertical.

**What it erodes:** the no-outbound/approval-gate model is now table stakes — Anthropic ships the same approve-before-send default.

## 3. Google Workspace + Gemini

Gemini is now bundled into all paid Workspace plans (standalone add-ons discontinued early 2026): Business Starter $7/user/mo (Gemini in Gmail), Business Standard $14/user/mo (Gemini across Docs/Sheets/Slides/Meet/Drive) (https://workspace.google.com/pricing, accessed 2026-06-06). "Workspace Studio" lets owners build multi-step agentic workflows in plain English; Gems handle repeatable tasks. **Auto-execute:** drafting/assist in-app, agentic flows emerging — not a vertical-compliant unattended back office.

## 4. Microsoft 365 Copilot for SMB

Copilot Chat is free with eligible M365. **M365 Copilot Business $18/user/mo** (promo through 2026-06-30, then $21), up to 300 users. **Copilot Studio agents from $200/mo per tenant** + Azure subscription (https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoft-365-copilot/pricing, accessed 2026-06-06). **Auto-execute** via Studio agents, but requires a technical build-out — not owner-ready done-for-you.

## The single scariest scenario

**"Anthropic Cowork SMB eats the middle."** The supplier is now the competitor at $0 marginal cost to a subscriber, with the same approve-before-send loop and the same connectors. **If Anthropic (or a third-party configurator ecosystem) adds even shallow vertical templates and a partner/agency channel, agentplain's DIY-vs-done-for-you wedge narrows to compliance depth alone.** That makes counsel-reviewed vertical compliance the single most important asset to widen — and makes a *services/partner motion* (the human layer Claude SMB lacks) the second.

## Positioning deltas

- **vs. generic LLM / Claude SMB:** "They hand owners a blank chatbox; agentplain runs the vertical-compliant back office for you, unattended-but-approved, with a named partner who does the work."
- **vs. Google / Microsoft Copilot:** "They assist inside one productivity suite; agentplain coordinates across QuickBooks, your CRM, email, and calendar with industry compliance and a partner — not an IT build-out."

## Threat level

**High / strategic** — platform-encroachment on the supplier axis. The moat is now **compliance depth + true done-for-you service**, not the approval model or the connector list.
