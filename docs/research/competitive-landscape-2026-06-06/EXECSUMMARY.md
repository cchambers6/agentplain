# Competitive Landscape — Executive Summary
**agentplain · 2026-06-06**

_Method: live web research (WebSearch + WebFetch, June 2026) across 10 verticals, 8 horizontal agent platforms, 6 CRM-native AI suites, and 4 platform-level adjacents. Every pricing/funding/feature claim is cited to a URL with access date in the underlying deep-dive files. This summary is candor-first, not a sales document._

---

## The one-sentence read

agentplain's positioning is **mostly right and increasingly lonely in a good way** — the entire funded field is sprinting toward autonomous auto-execution while we deliberately stop at the human-approval line — **but the no-outbound/approval-gate is no longer a differentiator on its own.** As of 2026-05-13 our own model supplier, Anthropic, ships "Claude for Small Business" with the *exact* approve-before-send posture, free to any subscriber. The durable moat is now **vertical compliance depth + genuine done-for-you service**, not the approval model.

---

## What we got right

1. **The autonomy posture is contrarian and defensible.** Across all 10 verticals, the well-funded incumbents race toward auto-execution — bots that text leads in <60s (real estate), wire funds (title), auto-bill and move money (CPA — Anchor), auto-call candidates (recruiting — Sense, Paradox), answer the phone and book the job (home services — Avoca, ServiceTitan), auto-respond to tenants (property mgmt — EliseAI). agentplain's "we draft, you send" is the opposite bet, and in regulated verticals it is a *feature*, not a limitation. The TCPA/RESPA/SEC-Marketing-Rule/fair-housing/EEOC liability-transfer argument is real and underused.

2. **Integrate-not-replace is the right wedge against incumbents.** Buyers are locked into BoldTrail, Clio, AppFolio, Karbon, Epic. The competitors that demand migration (replace-the-CRM) hit the "rip-and-replace" objection. Layering on top is the lower-friction path.

3. **The vertical breadth is a real asset the point-tools lack.** Most competitors are single-workflow (Rilla = sales coaching; FinMate = notetaking; Structurely = lead texting). agentplain's "whole back office across all your tools" is a genuinely wider surface — when we can prove it.

4. **One transparent price is a differentiator.** Nearly every serious competitor is "contact sales" / demo-gated / metered per-action (Agentforce $2/conversation, HubSpot $0.50/resolution). Flat $99–$199/seat with a free first month is legible in a market of opaque enterprise pricing.

## What we got wrong (or oversold)

1. **The 15–107× ROI claim's top end is an outlier the field does not support.** Competitor ROI claims cluster as *hours-saved* and *% cost reduction*, and where clean multiples appear they land at **8–50×** (Structurely 21×; RIA implied 25–50×; legal intake 8–17×). The **15× floor is well-supported; 107× is a best-case we cannot defend head-to-head** and reads as pixie dust against our own "intelligence rooted in reality" tagline. Recalibrate to a defensible mid-band (see `ROI_BENCHMARKS.md`).

2. **"No-outbound as the headline differentiator" is now table stakes.** Roots/Bevaya (insurance), Capacity (mortgage), and — decisively — Anthropic's Claude for Small Business all ship human-in-the-loop approval. We can no longer lead with "you stay in control" as if it's unique. We must lead with *why our approval gate is smarter* (vertical compliance rules baked into the draft).

3. **Home-services is a structural mismatch we should stop treating as equal.** The vertical buys AI to *answer the phone and book the job* — the one thing our architecture forbids. We will lose almost every head-to-head here. It belongs in a "back-office augmentation only" framing or deprioritized in GTM (see `THREATS_AND_OPPORTUNITIES.md`).

4. **We have no traction proof against incumbents who quote it.** BoldTrail (200K+ agents), Jump (~27K advisors / ~1 in 10 US advisors), EliseAI (~10% of US apartments), Black Ore (40% of Top-20 CPA firms). "Built by agents / runs on flatsbo" is a good story but not a logo wall. Every deep-dive flags the proof gap.

## Where the moat actually is

- **Vertical compliance depth that is counsel-reviewed.** This is the one thing no horizontal, no CRM-native AI, and (so far) not even Claude for Small Business has. Reviews of Claude SMB explicitly note "no discussion of vertical compliance." This is the asset to widen and to put on the homepage.
- **Done-for-you service partnership (Plaino) vs. DIY blank-canvas.** Every platform-level threat — ChatGPT, Claude SMB, Gemini, Copilot, Lindy, Zapier Agents — hands the owner a toolkit and says "go build." Our non-technical, compliance-exposed local owner does not want a canvas; they want the work done. This is the second pillar, and it is exactly the wedge that narrows if Anthropic adds a partner/agency channel.
- **The compliance + service combination, not either alone.** Individually each is attackable; together they describe a managed, industry-aware fleet that the platforms structurally aren't built to deliver.

## Where the moat *isn't*

- **The approval/no-send model itself** — now a platform default (Anthropic, Roots, Bevaya).
- **Connectors** — Claude SMB, Gemini, Copilot, Zapier all have more integrations than we do.
- **The drafting quality on generic tasks** — a $20/mo ChatGPT Business or Claude Team seat already drafts a good email. The owner can do the read→draft loop today, cheaply, themselves.
- **Capital and distribution** — we are out-raised and out-distributed in every vertical (Harvey $1B+ raised at $11B; EliseAI $2.2B val; Avoca $1B val; Jump $105M; Black Ore $60M).

## Top 5 findings (PR-ready)

1. **The whole field is betting on autonomy; we're the human-approval contrarian — and that's correct for regulated verticals.** In real estate, title, recruiting, home services, property mgmt and CPA the flagship products *send, dial, wire, or book on their own.* Our liability-transfer story (TCPA/RESPA/SEC/fair-housing/EEOC stays on the customer's system) is the sharpest, most underused weapon we have.
2. **Anthropic — our supplier — moved into our customer.** "Claude for Small Business" (2026-05-13) ships 15 agentic workflows + connectors to QuickBooks/HubSpot/Google/Microsoft, with the same approve-before-send loop, free to subscribers. Supplier-as-competitor is now our #1 platform risk. The defense is compliance depth + done-for-you service — the two things Claude SMB explicitly lacks.
3. **15–107× should become ~15–50×.** The field's clean ROI multiples top out around 21–50×; our 107× ceiling undercuts our credibility. Keep the floor, drop the ceiling, anchor on avoided-violation math (a single 1,000-message TCPA misfire = $500K–$1.5M).
4. **CRM-native AI ("good enough" gravity) is the most underrated threat.** Owners already pay for HubSpot/Pipedrive/Apollo; bundled AI carries zero new contract. We only win on vertical compliance + cross-tool reach + no-send — so those must be the pitch, not "we have AI."
5. **Home-services is our worst-fit vertical and should not lead GTM.** Real estate, RIA, insurance, mortgage, title and CPA are where the compliance-and-control wedge lands hardest; home-services and (to a degree) recruiting are where the no-send architecture is a felt liability.

## The 3 sharpest positioning deltas (feed straight into copy)

1. **vs. every vertical incumbent:** *"They race to send, dial, and wire on your behalf in the first 60 seconds — and keep the liability on their platform. agentplain reads, drafts, and hands you the approve button, so the send and the TCPA/RESPA/fair-housing liability stay on your own system."*

3. **vs. CRM-native AI (HubSpot Breeze, Agentforce, Apollo):** *"Their AI only sees the CRM, doesn't know your industry's rules, and auto-fires in your name. agentplain reads everything, knows the compliance rules, and never sends without you."*
