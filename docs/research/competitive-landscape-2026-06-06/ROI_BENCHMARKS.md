### Three patterns in how the field claims ROI

1. **The field rarely quotes a clean multiple.** Most claims are *hours-saved* or *% cost/time reduction.* Only a handful publish a multiple: Structurely 21×, Roots 2.46× (246%), legal intake 3–5×.
2. **Where clean multiples appear, they cluster at 3–25×** for a *single workflow.* The RIA hours-saved figures, converted (8 hrs/wk × ~$150/hr ≈ $5K/mo vs. $99–$199/seat), imply **~25–50×** — but across the *whole* practice, not one task.
3. **The biggest headline numbers are throughput claims tied to an outbound action agentplain won't take** (Avoca "$1B booked," Paradox "$2M saved", 90–98% touchless). We structurally cannot produce those numbers and should not try to match them.

## Honest calibration of 15–107×

**Verdict: keep the floor, cut the ceiling, change the basis of the claim.**

- **The 15× floor is well-supported.** A whole-back-office fleet at $99–$199/seat that recovers even 8–12 hours/week of a $75–$150/hr practitioner clears 15× comfortably — and the field's own per-workflow multiples (21× Structurely, 25–50× implied RIA) corroborate that a *broader* surface should sit at or above the low-double-digits.
- **The 107× ceiling is an outlier the field does not support and our own tagline can't carry.** No competitor — including the best-funded, deepest-automation players — publishes anything near 100×. A 107× claim against a market that tops out near 50× reads as the exact "pixie dust" our brand bans. It invites the buyer to discount the *entire* number.
- **The mid-band 15–50× is both defensible and still category-leading.** It beats Structurely's 21× and the legal 3–5×, sits at the top of the RIA-implied range, and survives scrutiny because it rests on *breadth* (whole back office) rather than a single inflated workflow.

### Recommended reframing

1. **Headline:** "**15–50× ROI** for a typical local practice" — defensible, still best-in-class, brand-safe.
2. **Show the math, not the multiple, as the hero.** The field's credible claims are bottoms-up (hours × rate, cost-per-loan). Our ROI calculator (already specced: vertical + seats + hourly rate → hours-saved/mo + $/mo vs. subscription) is the right instrument. Let the *customer's own inputs* produce the multiple; don't assert 107×.
3. **Add the avoided-violation anchor in regulated verticals.** This is the number the autonomy-racing field *cannot* claim because their architecture *creates* the exposure:
   - TCPA: $500–$1,500 per message/call. A single 1,000-message misfire = **$500K–$1.5M** (empowerlo.com, accessed 2026-06-06).
   - Recruiting: one avoided AI-hiring disparate-impact settlement cited at **$365K** (per the recruiting deep-dive).
   - Title: against a backdrop of ~$275M/yr in wire fraud and a +1,760% rise in AI-driven BEC.
   "agentplain's architecture makes the most expensive mistake in your industry structurally impossible" is a stronger, more honest ROI story than a 107× multiple.

### What to retire from copy

- The **107×** ceiling and any multiple above ~50× without a customer-specific calculator output behind it.
- The implied claim that agentplain produces the throughput numbers (booking rates, touchless %, leads connected) that the auto-execute field quotes — we measure a *different* outcome (time recovered + risk avoided + work coordinated under control).

## One-line summary for the team

> The field tops out around 21–50× for single workflows and quotes hours-saved more than multiples. Our 15× floor is safe and our breadth justifies a 15–50× band — but **107× is an outlier that undercuts our own "rooted in reality" credibility.** Lead with the customer's own calculator math plus the avoided-violation anchor, not a headline multiple the market can't corroborate.
