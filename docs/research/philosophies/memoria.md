# Memoria Press — Research Notes (Chiron)

Memoria Press is the Latin-first, mastery-oriented implementation of classical Christian
education: a family-run publisher whose curriculum is written, taught, and field-tested in
an actual school (Highlands Latin School, Louisville, KY). Of the four philosophies in this
set, it is the most prescriptive and the most "open-and-go" — its differentiators are
simplicity, systematic mastery, and a defined grade-by-grade core.

## Founding vision: Cheryl Lowe

- **Cheryl Lowe** (B.S. chemistry, Univ. of Louisville; M.S. biology, Western Kentucky)
  discovered Latin while homeschooling her sons and became its self-styled apostle,
  claiming "Latin has more educational value than any other subject you can teach your
  children" (memoriapress.com/about-our-founder — this document's single direct quote).
- She and her son Brian founded **Memoria Press in 1994**; her Latin teaching materials
  became *Latina Christiana* (100,000+ copies shipped). She also authored *Prima Latina*,
  *Lingua Angelica*, the Latin Forms Series, *Classical Phonics*, *First Start Reading*,
  and *English Grammar Recitation*.
- Lowe died in June 2017; Memoria Press states her mission continues to inform the
  company and school daily.

## The Highlands Latin School connection

- In **2000**, Lowe's one-room Latin program grew into **Highlands Latin School** (HLS,
  Louisville), where she served as Headmistress.
- The connection is structural, not incidental: Memoria Press products are **taught and
  field-tested at HLS**, and Memoria Press authors develop and teach their courses in HLS
  classrooms (hls.org/meetfounder). HLS also seeds "Highlands Latin Cottage Schools"
  (part-time hybrid schools using the curriculum).
- Proof-point the company leans on: HLS ranks in the top 1% nationally on ITBS (K–8) and
  among the top of its state on SAT/ACT (memoriapress.com/about-our-founder).
- Per Wikipedia's article on HLS, the school gives special emphasis to Latin, mathematics,
  and music — called the "three universal languages" — with Latin from 2nd grade and
  Greek from 7th.

## Educational approach

From memoriapress.com/classical-education/:

- **Definition**: classical Christian education is the cultivation of wisdom and virtue
  through meditation on the Good, the True, and the Beautiful — accomplished through
  (1) training in the **liberal arts** (trivium + quadrivium: the "how" of education) and
  (2) familiarity with the **great books and great thinkers of the Western tradition**
  (the "what").
- **Three cultures** frame the content: Athens (the Greeks — philosophical/literary man),
  Rome (practical/political man), Jerusalem (the Hebrews — spiritual man); their
  coalescence into Christendom is the heritage being transmitted.
- **Latin-first**: Latin is presented as the best single instrument for academic
  vocabulary, formal grammar mastery, and (with math) early critical-thinking training —
  the systematic, inflected grammar *is* the logic training in the early years
  (memoriapress.com/latin/, /curriculum/classical-core-curriculum/).
- **Systematic mastery**: programs are deliberately incremental — step-by-step, with
  recitation, review worksheets, drill toward mastery of forms before progression. Product
  values stated as simplicity, quality, affordability; "easy to use" and "high rate of
  program completion" are marketed features (memoriapress.com/classical-education/).

### The Classical Core Curriculum

A complete PreK–12 classical Christian curriculum (grade-level boxed sets) emphasizing:
early focus on reading, writing, arithmetic; special emphasis on Latin; study of Athens,
Rome, and Biblical/Church history as the basis for understanding European and American
history; literature via great works. Sold with curriculum manuals and week-by-week lesson
plans — the schedule is part of the product
(memoriapress.com/curriculum/classical-core-curriculum/).

## Contrast notes (for pack differentiation)

- vs **Charlotte Mason**: Memoria Press embraces structured drill, recitation, and
  workbooks that CM would restrict; assessment is mastery-checked, not narration-only.
- vs **WTM trivium**: shares the trivium frame but centers Latin grammar (rather than the
  history cycle) as the spine of the early years.
- vs **CIRCE**: shares the wisdom/virtue/true-good-beautiful telos almost verbatim, but
  operationalizes it as a fixed, sequenced curriculum rather than a contemplative pedagogy.

## Scheduling/product implications (Chiron)

- A Memoria-flavored pack is **sequence-driven**: grade-level cores with weekly lesson
  plans; the orchestrator's job is pacing against the published plan and protecting daily
  Latin recitation/review slots.
- **Mastery gates**: progression to the next unit is conditional on demonstrated mastery
  of forms/facts (recitation checks), unlike CM narration or CIRCE contemplation.
- Daily question style: **recitation** — grammar questions, forms, and memory work with
  right answers, plus review of previously mastered material.
- "Good day" definition: the planned lessons were completed and recitation showed
  retention — coverage and accuracy matter here in a way they deliberately don't in the
  CM/CIRCE packs.
