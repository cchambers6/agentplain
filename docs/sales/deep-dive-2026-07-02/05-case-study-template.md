# 05 — Case-study template (earned, never fabricated)

**Rule zero:** every case study is earned from a real design partner, with written permission, with numbers from instrumentation. There are **zero** case studies today and this file says so out loud. Publishing anything from this template before a pilot produces it is a Truth-Wave violation.

**Measurement prerequisite (blocking):** hours-reclaimed numbers come from the saved-time ledger, and audit 9 (P0-1) found 4 of 7 calibrated actions have **no writer** — sweep-driven work (including invoice-chase, the flagship) records zero minutes today. **Fix the recorders before pilot week 1**, or the case study's headline number will be honestly-derived and wrong. Measurement is defined *before* the first pilot, not retrofitted at week 12 (kaizen 05, fix #5).

## Template

> Copy this block per case study. Nothing publishes with an unfilled field or an unchecked permission box.

### [Customer name] — [vertical]

**Permissions checklist (all required before publish):**
- [ ] Signed design-partner agreement covering case-study rights (02 §4) — or a standalone signed release
- [ ] Partner approved this exact text, in writing (date: ___)
- [ ] Named-person quote approved verbatim by that person (date: ___)
- [ ] Logo use approved (if used)
- [ ] No end-client of the partner is identifiable, directly or by inference

**1. Who they are** (2–3 sentences, plain)
Business, location, size (agents/doors/seats), and the one-line version of their week. Written from the discovery-call recording — their words, not marketing's.

**2. Before — the workflow as it was**
The concrete Tuesday-night scene from discovery. What the owner personally did, how long it took, what slipped. No adjectives; specifics.

**3. What we installed**
Which workflows went live (customer vocab only — "Setting up" / "Working" / "Watching"; never runtime labels), which of their tools connected (only ones actually connected), and the approval cadence they settled into.

**4. After — the quantified outcome** *(methodology footnote mandatory)*
- Drafts per week landed in their queue: **N** (source: workspace instrumentation)
- Approval rate: **N%** (approved / (approved + rejected), same source)
- Hours reclaimed: **N hrs over [period]** (source: saved-time ledger; calibration table citation)
- Vertical-specific outcome if real dollars rode the payload (e.g., invoices chased → collected): only when the payload carried a real amount — the weekly-report discipline (audit 7) applies here too: **no dollar figure is ever synthesized.**

> **Methodology footnote (publish verbatim, adapted):** *"Hours reclaimed are computed from per-action time credits calibrated conservatively by agentplain (calibration table: `lib/guarantee/` on the date of publication), counted only for actions the fleet actually completed in [customer]'s workspace between [date] and [date]. [Customer] reviewed and approved these figures. Individual results vary."*

**5. The quote**
One to three sentences, named person, named business, approved verbatim. The most valuable quote is *why the approval gate satisfied them* — weight the ask toward that, not toward superlatives. No quote is ever drafted *for* the partner to sign; they say it (on the weekly call, recorded), we transcribe, they approve the transcription.

**6. Quarterly refresh**
Date of last on-record quarterly quote: ___ (program term, 02 §1). A case study whose numbers are >2 quarters stale gets re-verified or gets a dateline.

**Publish path (in order):**
1. Site: `/customers/[slug]` (build the route when the first study is real — not before; no empty "Customers" nav item in the interim)
2. The vertical's landing page: one pull-quote + link
3. LinkedIn: founder-voice post (Conner's account — no company-page ghost town), partner tagged with their pre-approval
4. Press/local trade only if the partner wants it (their association newsletter beats TechCrunch for our buyer)
5. Sales assets: one-pager gains a proof block; discovery deck gains one slide; objection #1/#3 answers in 04 get updated the same week — the whole point of the artifact
