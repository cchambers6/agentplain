# Discovery kit 2026-07-08 — how to use this folder

**What this is:** the field kit for every design-partner discovery call. Six files, one job: Conner gets on a 20-minute call with a Georgia broker-owner and comes off it with either a booked kickoff, a dated not-yet, or a clean no with a logged reason. Nothing here is analysis — it's the words.

**Lineage:** this compresses and operationalizes `docs/sales/deep-dive-2026-07-02/04-discovery-call-playbook.md` (the full operating layer — qualification frame, living sections) and `docs/departments/2026-07-03/sales/03-discovery-call-playbook-tightened.md` (the 20-minute cut). Where this folder and the parents disagree on call mechanics, **this folder wins** — it's newer and it's the one open during the call. The parent's §7 living sections (heard-objection log, "what would have made it a yes") remain the single log — keep feeding them.

## Read order, the night before the first call

1. `01-20min-script.md` — the agenda and the actual sentences. Read it out loud once.
2. `02-objection-library-20.md` — skim all 20; deep-read the 5 flagged for this prospect in the briefing sheet.
3. `03-model-vendor-invisibility-crib-sheet.md` — the one page that must never be improvised.
4. `04-disqualification-framework.md` — know the walk-away signals before you're tempted to ignore one.
5. `05-close-language.md` — the last two minutes are the whole call. Know both endings cold.

## Memorize vs. open-tab

**Memorize (say without looking):**
- The opening framing paragraph (script, min 0–2). It sets the frame for everything after; reading it flat kills it.
- The three discovery questions, verbatim. If the call ends without all three answered, it failed as a discovery call regardless of how it felt.
- The vendor-question first response (crib sheet, variant 3). It arrives without warning and hesitation reads as hiding something.
- The design-partner terms in one breath: three months free, weekly call with me, case study you approve word by word, on-record testimonial once you've seen value.
- The two closes: the yes-path booking line and the warm-maybe dating line.

**Open as tabs during the call (glance, don't recite):**
- `02-objection-library-20.md` — scrolled to the theme the briefing sheet predicts.
- The per-call briefing sheet (`docs/outreach/2026-07-03-design-partner-kit/04-first-call-briefing-template.md`, filled out).
- The demo workspace, already loaded, on the approval queue — not the home page.
- `04-disqualification-framework.md` and `05-close-language.md` in one tab each.

## Standing rules (inherited, load-bearing)

- Recorded with stated consent: "I record these so I can listen instead of taking notes — okay?"
- Listen-first: the prospect talks more than you through minute 12.
- **Vendor invisibility:** never confirm or deny which AI runs underneath, in any direction, even casually. The subprocessor list on /privacy and /security is the sole sanctioned disclosure. The crib sheet is the only script for this.
- **No invented proof.** Zero named customers, said plainly, every time it's relevant. The sanctioned substitute: the same fleet pattern runs a real brokerage's operations daily.
- CRM updated same day; one line into the parent playbook's living sections per call.
- Never end on "I'll follow up sometime." A yes is a calendar entry; a maybe is a date; a no gets the learning question.
