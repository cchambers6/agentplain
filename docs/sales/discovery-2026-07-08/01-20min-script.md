# 01 — The 20-minute discovery script

The first-touch email asked for "a 20-minute call this week," so the call costs 20 minutes — running long is the prospect's choice, never the agenda's. The sentences below are the words Conner says. Adapt the connective tissue to sound like a person; keep the bolded lines intact — they're load-bearing.

| Time | Block |
|---|---|
| 0–2 | Frame: service partner, not tool |
| 2–6 | Three discovery questions |
| 6–12 | The demo, one thing only |
| 12–16 | Pricing + the design-partner offer |
| 16–18 | Objections (live traffic — see library) |
| 18–20 | Close: book it or date it. Never "we'll follow up." |

---

## Minute 0–2 — Frame

**Say, after hello:**

> "Before we start — I record these calls so I can listen instead of taking notes. Okay?"

*(Wait for the yes. Then:)*

> "Quick framing so you know what this is and isn't. We're not selling you software to figure out. We install a service that reads the tools you already run — your CRM, your email, your calendar, your books — and drafts the work you'd otherwise type at 9pm: the lead replies, the chasing, the status updates. **Nothing sends without your name on it — every draft waits for your approval.** We set it up, we run it, and you stay the one in control.
>
> Here's how I want to use the twenty minutes: I want to spend most of it hearing how your week actually runs, then I'll show you one thing, and then we'll both decide if there's a next step. Fair?"

What this frame forecloses on purpose: "another AI tool" (it's a service with a person accountable), "automation acting on its own" (the approval gate is the product), and "something my team has to learn" (we install and run it).

## Minute 2–6 — The three questions

Ask all three. Everything else is optional; if the call ends without these answered, it failed as a discovery call regardless of how it felt. Follow the pain each answer opens — don't march the list.

**Question 1:**

> "Walk me through last Tuesday — where did the day actually go?"

*Their answer is the case-study "before" in their own words, the input for the ROI math at minute 13, and the fastest test of whether a recurring block of draft-shaped work exists. No pain named = no pilot.*

**Question 2:**

> "What tools run the business — CRM, email, calendar, books, e-sign? And who touches them: just you, or staff too?"

*This is the activation gate in disguise. Follow Up Boss or a Google/M365 inbox plus QuickBooks = pilotable this month. Everything locked inside a franchise stack = an honest not-yet on this call, before either side spends more. It also surfaces seat count and pre-fires the "you don't integrate with X" objection on our terms.*

**Question 3:**

> "Has anything ever gone out to a client that shouldn't have — and what happened?"

*This opens the approval-gate value without pitching it. A broker carries fair-housing exposure personally; their own story makes the case better than our copy ever will. Their answer scripts the demo emphasis two minutes later.*

If it fits naturally (Georgia broker-owner default): *"When a lead comes in at 9pm, what happens before 9am?"*

## Minute 6–12 — The demo

**The 30-second intro, before sharing the screen:**

> "Let me show you one thing — not a slide deck, the actual product. **This is a demonstration workspace running on synthetic data — you're seeing the real product, not your data and not anyone else's.** I'm going to show you the moment that matters most: a draft waiting for approval."

Then run the killer-workflow demo (the synthetic-data demo runtime shipped in PR #303; the same lead-triage story autoplays in demo mode on the `/real-estate` Today tab — see `docs/pilot/week-1-runbook/01-DAY-1-activation-call.md` §2 for the beats). One workflow only. Resist showing a second thing.

**Narrate three beats, in this order:**

1. The trigger: > "A buyer lead landed at 9:14 last night — after everyone knocked off. By the time anyone's at a desk, there's a drafted reply, two proposed showing windows, and the CRM update — sitting in a queue. **Nothing has been sent.**"
2. The edit: > "Say the tone's off. You change a word —" *(edit one word, live)* "— and it learns that's how you sound."
3. The approve: > "And this is the whole product in one click: you approve it, and it goes out through *your* systems, under *your* name. The AI never sends anything. That gate isn't a setting somebody can flip off — it's the architecture."

If their Question-3 story was a compliance scare, lean the narration there: > "This is where that email you told me about would have been caught — a compliance pass flags issues on the draft, and then it still waits for you."

**Do not** claim integrations that aren't live. The live story: Follow Up Boss (API key), email, calendar, QuickBooks, DocuSign, Drive. Everything else is roadmap, and say the word "roadmap" if asked.

## Minute 12–16 — Pricing + the design-partner offer

**Value first, price second, honestly labeled:**

> "On the money: the way we model it, the drafting-and-chasing work in a shop like yours is worth somewhere between three and ten thousand dollars a month in owner time. **That's a model, not a customer result — I'll be straight that we don't have customer-attested numbers yet.** The subscription that covers it is on our pricing page — Regular runs $199 a seat and slides to $99 — so the honest way to test the model is against your own week, which is what I'm about to offer."

**The design-partner offer, gives and asks in the same breath:**

> "Here's the offer, and I'll give you the catch in the same sentence as the deal. **You'd be one of our first five design partners. You get three months completely free, a weekly 30-minute call with me — the founder — and real say in what we build next. In exchange: those weekly calls actually happen, you go on record with a testimonial once you've genuinely seen value — not before — and we write a case study together that you approve word by word before anyone sees it.** You're paying in proof, not dollars. And if the service doesn't earn the testimonial, you don't give it, and you walk."

One clarity line, always said:

> "One thing so it doesn't surprise you later: the weekly call with me is a design-partner benefit, not something the regular subscription includes. When the three months are up you'd convert at the normal price with the normal support — and you'll know exactly what that looks like before you decide."

## Minute 16–18 — Objections

This block belongs to them. Answer from `02-objection-library-20.md`: **short first response, then stop talking.** The silence after the short answer is where they tell you the real objection.

The two that arrive most while we have zero customers:

- "Who else uses this?" → > "Nobody I can name — zero named customers, and that's exactly why the terms are three months free and weekly access to the founder. You'd be first, on purpose, priced for it."
- Any form of "what AI is this?" → the crib sheet (`03-model-vendor-invisibility-crib-sheet.md`), verbatim. Never confirm or deny a vendor, in any direction.

## Minute 18–20 — Close

Never "we'll follow up." Full language in `05-close-language.md`; the skeleton:

**If it's a yes:**

> "Great. Let's book the kickoff right now while we're both looking at calendars — it's 90 minutes, and before we hang up that call, you'll have approved a real item with your own hands, in your own workspace. What does [day] look like?"

*(Calendar entry before the goodbye, or it isn't a yes.)*

**If it's a warm maybe:**

> "Totally fair. Two questions so I don't pester you: what would need to be true for this to be worth revisiting — and when should I check back?"

*(Named blocker + named date into the CRM. One-pager sent same day.)*

**If it's a no:**

> "Appreciate the straight answer — one question for my own learning: what would have had to be true for this to be a yes?"

*(Log it verbatim. Close the CRM row LOST with the reason. Never argue with a no.)*

## After the call (same day, 15-minute ceiling)

CRM row advanced with next action + date → recording noted → one line each into the parent playbook's living sections: the objection heard, whether the library answer landed, any agenda drift. If yes-leaning, the case-study-carrot letter (`docs/outreach/2026-07-03-design-partner-kit/05-case-study-carrot.md`) goes out within 24 hours.
