# Executive Summary — agentplain's response to Claude for Small Business

**2026-06-06. One page. The story in five paragraphs — paste-ready for a sales deck, investor email, or board update.**

On 2026-05-13 Anthropic launched **Claude for Small Business**: a plugin inside Claude Cowork bundling ~15 workflows and ~15–25 skills with connectors to QuickBooks, HubSpot, Google Workspace, and Microsoft 365, at no incremental cost on top of a Claude subscription ([anthropic.com](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-for-small-business), accessed 2026-06-06). Our core engine vendor now ships a product adjacent to ours. We treat this as our **#1 platform risk** and we address it head-on rather than around.

The frame is simple and it does not change: **Claude is the engine; agentplain runs it for you.** Claude for Small Business is a genuinely capable tool — and it hands a business owner a powerful engine and expects them to figure out which skills to turn on, what to put in memory, how to keep it current, and how to stay compliant. Most local-business owners don't have the time or an engineer for that. agentplain is the **service layer** on top: pre-built per-vertical skills and agents, curated and maintained memory, counsel-reviewed compliance, and a real person running it — plug-and-play, not configure-it-yourself. We are complementary, never a competitor; if the engine gets better, our service gets better.

We're honest about where they win. Their connector breadth is wider than ours today and their sticker price is lower — pretending otherwise gets us caught. But the parity matrix is decisive on the categories that are *service, not software*: managed memory, vertical-shaped skills, **counsel-reviewed compliance (which they ship none of)**, the multi-tenant operator plane that makes done-for-you scale, and a true total-cost-of-ownership story for an owner whose scarce resource is time. The model is the commodity Anthropic sells to everyone. The configured, run-for-you, compliance-backed service is the part they've deliberately left to partners.

That last point is the heart of the investment case, and it's Anthropic's own behavior, not our spin: in March 2026 they launched a Claude Marketplace taking **no commission**, and on 2026-06-03 they announced a **three-tiered services partner track** — they are recruiting the services layer to partners, not building it ([techzine.eu](https://www.techzine.eu/news/applications/139359/anthropic-launches-claude-powered-app-marketplace-without-taking-a-cut/); [channeldive.com](https://www.channeldive.com/news/anthropic-claude-partner-program-services-track/821936/), accessed 2026-06-06). We are exactly the kind of partner that strategy requires, pointed at the segment Anthropic reaches least well: time-poor, compliance-bound local businesses who want the outcome, not the operating manual. Our architecture is engine-portable by design, so a future shift in supplier economics doesn't break the customer-facing service.

Over the next twelve months we **double down on the moat that holds and spend nothing defending the moat that doesn't.** Six moves, all on the defensible side: counsel-reviewed compliance corpora across all ten verticals; **compliance-as-a-service with insurance backing** (the one thing a platform structurally cannot match — we take named liability for the scan); per-vertical Plaino personas; done-for-you onboarding with no wizard; customer-handoff intelligence; and per-customer voice fine-tuning that compounds switching cost the longer a customer stays. The net: Anthropic didn't enter our market — they enlarged it, validated the category, and told the market the service layer belongs to partners. We're built to be that partner.
