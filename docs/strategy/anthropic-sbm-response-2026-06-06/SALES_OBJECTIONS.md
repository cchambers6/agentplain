# Sales Objection Handling — the Claude-for-Small-Business questions

**Compiled 2026-06-06.** Twenty objections sales will hear now that Anthropic ships Claude for Small Business. Each answer is 2–3 sentences, in the heritage voice — calm, plain, grounded, no hype, never adversarial toward Claude. The frame is always the same: **Claude is the engine; we run it for you.** Pulls from the four pillars (pre-built skills+agents · managed memory · low-cost plug-and-play · human-staffed service) and the parity matrix.

**Two hard rules for every rep:**
1. Never disparage Claude or Anthropic. We are complementary. The words *compete / replace / instead of / alternative to* are banned (`project_sbm_wrapper_positioning`).
2. Where they're genuinely better — connector breadth, sticker price — say so. A softer true claim beats an inflated one, every time.

---

### 1. "Why not just use Claude for Small Business?"
Claude for Small Business is a real, capable tool — and it hands you a powerful engine and expects you to figure out which skills to turn on, what to put in memory, and how to wire it to your business. We're the service that does all of that for you and keeps it running, so you never touch a setup screen. Same engine; we drive.

### 2. "Claude is basically free with my subscription. You're not."
True — the plugin rides your Claude plan at no extra charge, and on the sticker that's cheaper than us. The fair comparison is the sticker *plus the hours you'd spend configuring and maintaining it* against our all-in fee. For an owner whose scarce resource is time, run-for-you comes out lower, not higher.

### 3. "I can configure it myself."
Plenty of owners can — the question is whether it's the best use of the week you'd spend learning what to write in a `CLAUDE.md`, which compliance edges to watch, and how to keep it from going stale. We've already built the per-vertical version and we maintain it. You get the outcome without the project.

### 4. "Aren't you just a thin wrapper around Claude?"
We're a wrapper the way a managed kitchen is a wrapper around an oven — the oven is excellent and it isn't the meal. What we add is the per-vertical skills and agents, the memory we curate, the compliance scan reviewed by counsel, and a real person running it. That's the part Anthropic deliberately leaves to partners.

### 5. "What if Anthropic just adds your features?"
They'll keep making the engine better, and we want them to — we're built on it. The things we do — knowing what belongs in a real-estate broker's memory versus a CPA's, carrying counsel-reviewed compliance for ten regulated trades, running it as a service across a book of customers — are vertical and human, not a feature they'd ship horizontally. Anthropic has publicly built a *partner services track* precisely because that layer isn't theirs to run.

### 6. "Isn't this just going to get commoditized?"
The horizontal back-office work — chasing invoices, drafting follow-ups — is commoditizing, and we don't defend it; our version and theirs do the same job. What doesn't commoditize is fair-housing-aware real-estate work, RESPA-aware mortgage work, and a human who owns the result. We double down on the part that's hard to copy.

### 7. "Claude has way more integrations than you do."
That's accurate today — their connector list is broader, and we're honest about it. The difference is you have to pick and wire yours; ours are configured for you during onboarding and every connected action runs through a compliance scan and a human before anything leaves. Breadth you assemble yourself versus depth that's run for you.

### 8. "My nephew/IT guy can set Claude up for me."
He probably can get it connected — and then it's on him to keep memory current, watch the compliance edges of your specific trade, and tune it as your business changes. That's a standing job, not a weekend. We do that part every day, for your vertical, with counsel behind the compliance piece.

### 9. "How are you different from Claude Cowork?"
Cowork is the workshop where the tools live; we're the crew that shows up, brings the right tools for your trade already set up, and does the work. Cowork forgets between sessions unless you maintain the context files yourself — we run that memory for you. You direct a tool, or you hand work to a partner; we're the partner.

### 10. "Claude says nothing sends without my approval — same as you."
Right, and that's a good design — the difference is Claude lets you turn approval off and go fully autonomous. We structurally can't auto-send, and there's a counsel-reviewed compliance scan in front of every approval. So the violation that would've been fileable gets caught as a draft, not after it's out the door.

### 11. "You're a tiny startup; Anthropic is a giant. Why bet on you?"
You're not betting against Anthropic — you're running on Anthropic, through us. We're small enough that a real person knows your account and your trade, and we're built on the same engine the giant ships. If the engine gets better, you get better; that's the point of building on it.

### 12. "What happens to you if Anthropic changes their pricing or terms?"
Our architecture is built to move — every model call sits behind an abstraction so the engine underneath can change without breaking your service (`project_living_portable_architecture`). Today Claude is the best engine and we run on it; if that ever shifted, the work we do for you wouldn't. The service is the durable thing.

### 13. "Can't I get the same vertical skills from the Claude marketplace?"
The marketplace lists Claude-powered software you still operate yourself; it isn't a service that runs your vertical for you. And the compliance corpora we carry are counsel-reviewed and we stand behind them — that's liability, not a listing. You can buy more tools there; you can't buy a crew there.

### 14. "Your price is higher than just buying Claude Pro."
On the sticker, yes. What's inside our fee is the engine, the configuration, the memory upkeep, the compliance scan, and a human running it — the things you'd otherwise pay for in your own hours. We price the outcome, not the login.

### 15. "I already pay for QuickBooks/HubSpot/Google. Why another bill?"
Keep all of them — we work inside the tools you already run, we don't replace them. The bill you're adding is for the crew that makes those tools talk to each other and do the repetitive work, configured for your trade. One fee for the work, not another tool to learn.

### 16. "What do you actually do that the plugin doesn't?"
Four things the plugin leaves to you: we bring skills and agents already shaped for your trade, we curate the memory that keeps it useful, we carry counsel-reviewed compliance for your industry, and a real person runs it. The plugin is the parts; we're the assembled, running machine.

### 17. "I don't trust AI to touch my books/clients."
Good — neither do we, unsupervised. Nothing the fleet does goes out without passing a compliance scan and a human approving it; it drafts and proposes, you decide. The point is to take the typing off your plate, not the judgment.

### 18. "Anthropic is running free workshops and courses. Why pay you?"
Take the course — AI fluency is genuinely useful, and we're glad they're teaching it. The course teaches you to operate the engine; we're for the owner who'd rather hand the operating to someone else and get back to their customers. Different jobs.

### 19. "How do I know your compliance scan is actually safe?"
It's a per-vertical corpus reviewed by counsel before it ever goes live, and we gate it on that review — it doesn't fire for a vertical until the legal sign-off is in (`COMPLIANCE_CORPUS_COUNSEL_REVIEWED`). Claude's plugin has no industry compliance layer at all today. That's the gap we're built to own.

### 20. "Why now? Why not wait and see what Anthropic does?"
Waiting costs you the hours you're spending right now on work the fleet can take today. The engine is finally good enough and the integrations are finally stable enough — that's why Anthropic launched too. We're the way to get the value of that without becoming the person who has to run it.

- **"Aren't you a thin wrapper / won't they just add your features?"** → "We add the vertical skills, the curated memory, and counsel-reviewed compliance run as a service — the layer Anthropic has publicly chosen to leave to partners, because it's vertical and human, not horizontal infrastructure."
