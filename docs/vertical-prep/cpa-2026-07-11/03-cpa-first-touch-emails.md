# 03 — Five CPA first-touch emails (send-ready at activation)

**Sender:** Conner, from his own inbox. **Voice:** sober, plain, owner-to-owner — the ratified CPA persona rule applies: no Plaino voice in this vertical's outreach. **Ask:** always soft — "worth a 20-minute call?" No booking link in a first touch (kit rule). Follow-ups run the kit's 5/12/21-day chain from each actual send date.

**These are drafts written before the lane opens.** Two things are deliberately conditional and must be resolved on activation day, per email:

1. **The proof line.** Each email says our first design partners are Georgia real-estate brokerages. That sentence is only sendable because the activation trigger *is* two RE partners live — but verify it reads true on send day (partners still active, still design partners). Never name the RE partners in a cold email; their case-study consent (`docs/case-studies/framework-2026-07-08/01-capture-framework.md`) doesn't cover being referenced in another vertical's outbound.
2. **Every prospect fact** re-verified ≤3 days before send against `02-5-named-cpa-prospects.md`. A drifted fact pulls the send.

Truth rules inherited verbatim from the RE Monday-send README: no fabricated relationships (all five are cold), no invented counts, no "pilot pricing," vendor invisible, and the autonomy line stays exact — the fleet drafts; the firm approves and sends and files. Nothing in these emails may imply we send, file, or e-file anything ourselves.

## 1. Stanley Dean — SDA CPA Group (send first: cleanest verified hook)

**Subject:** `the 2016 decision, ten years on`

```
Hi Stanley,

In 2016 you dropped 1040-only clients to go all-in on small-business
owners — your site still tells that story, and it's the same call I'm
asking Georgia firm owners to look at one layer down.

I'm building agentplain, a service that reads the tools your firm
already runs — email, calendar, QuickBooks Online — and drafts the
work that eats partner evenings: the missing-document chases, the
client status replies, the month-end follow-ups. Your team reviews
and sends; nothing goes out, to a client or anyone else, without a
person at your firm approving it.

Straight up: no accounting firms on the roster yet. Our first design
partners are Georgia real-estate brokerages, and I'm now signing the
first CPA firms on the same terms — three months free, a weekly call
with me, first say in what we build for accountants.

Worth a 20-minute call?

Conner Chambers
Founder, agentplain
```

**Expected objections:** "we run Double/Keeper for client docs" → keep it; the portal holds the documents, someone still writes the chase and the status note (library #4 in the adapted playbook). "What AI is this?" → crib sheet, verbatim.
