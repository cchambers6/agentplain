Both prior reads were wrong. The brand is **agent + the plains** — heartland, prairie, soil, where things take root.

## The real semantics

| Reading | Status |
|---|---|
| agent + airplane (plane) | WRONG — first miss |
| agent + plainspoken/clear/unadorned (plain) | PARTIAL — captures heritage feel but misses the place-and-rooted meaning |
| **agent + the plains (where things take root)** | **CORRECT** — the brand anchor |

The brand is about AI that is GROUNDED — planted in the actual reality of local business life, in the heartland, in the small towns and cities that aren't Silicon Valley. The tagline "Intelligence rooted in reality" is the literal claim — this intelligence has roots, in soil, in the plains.

## Why this fits everything else that's locked

- **Mission:** "We lift up local businesses by doing the work that takes their time and money away from the people they serve." Local. Heartland. The plains.
- **The 10 verticals:** real estate / mortgage / insurance / property mgmt / title-escrow / recruiting / home services / cpa / law / ria — these ARE the small-town businesses. Main Street. Not coastal tech.
- **Service partnership lock:** we INSTALL, RUN, CUSTOMIZE — we show up to the customer's actual ground, like a partner who knows the territory, not a SaaS tool that abstracts away from it.
- **Anthropic's 10-city training tour:** Chicago, Tulsa, Dallas, NJ, Baton Rouge, Birmingham, SLC, Baltimore, San Jose, Indianapolis. Heartland-leaning city list. The competitive battlefield IS the plains.
- **Brand v0 typography lock:** heritage gothic / transitional serif (Source Serif 4 + Inter + JetBrains Mono). Heritage = grounded = rooted. NOT sleek tech sans-serif.
- **The 2026-05-15 pricing structure:** Regular / Partner / Max — partnership model that says "we're with you on your ground."

The plains anchor isn't a marketing decoration. It's the unifying frame.

## How this reshapes brand decisions

**Visual marks:**
- DEFAULT to plains/rooted imagery: horizon line, prairie grass, wheat, lone tree, sunrise over flat horizon, soil cross-section with roots, grain silo, plowed rows, single seed, big sky over flat ground
- "Paper plane" works ONLY if the framing makes it a paper-airplane-as-folded-prairie-message metaphor (still grounded), not as airplane symbolism
- Robot / agent figure can appear as a tiny figure ON the plains (a partner who shows up to the ground), never floating or aerial
- BANNED: sleek tech logos, abstract gradients, aerial-only imagery, anything that floats away from earth

**Copy voice:**
- Heritage, grounded, patient. Say "we put down roots in your business" not "we deploy at scale."
- Use plains/agriculture/heartland metaphors generously: rooted, planted, ground, soil, season, harvest, yield, tend, cultivate, weather, deep
- Avoid: cloud, scale, deploy, ship, accelerate, viral, hypergrowth — those evoke abstraction away from ground
- "Intelligence rooted in reality" as the tagline takes its full meaning under this rule

**Positioning copy:**
- "Built for the heartland" / "AI ops for businesses with their feet on the ground" / "We partner where you work, not where Silicon Valley lives"
- The /general vertical (Conner's proposed on-ramp) becomes "for any local business with its feet on the ground" — the plains framing scales the whole offering

**Vertical-specific copy:**
- Each vertical's content should reference the actual ground its customers stand on (real-estate = local market, CPA = local tax codes, law = local jurisdiction, etc.)
- "Rooted in reality" gets specialized per vertical: rooted in your closing process, rooted in your client roster, rooted in your jurisdiction

**Anthropic differentiation:**
- Anthropic's Claude for SMB is generic horizontal SMB tooling. Their visual = abstract sleek tech.
- agentplain's plains/rooted positioning is a structural counter — we're the AI partner that shows up to your specific local ground, not a horizontal cloud tool.
- This is sharper than just "service partnership vs tool" — it's "rooted partner vs floating tool."

## How to apply

When writing ANY brand-touching content (homepage, vertical page, /custom, FAQ, About, sign-up, billing, even product UI copy):
- Test against: does this feel rooted, grounded, heartland? OR does it feel sleek, aerial, cloud-y?
- If sleek/aerial/cloud, rewrite. If rooted/grounded, keep.

When writing visual briefs (logo, hero illustrations, vertical scenes, social cards):
- DEFAULT to plains/heartland/rooted imagery
- If reaching for a "tech" visual, ask: is there a grounded equivalent? (e.g., instead of a cloud icon → a planted seed; instead of a circuit → a root system)

When critiquing AGENT-generated content:
- Add to the lens: is this rooted? Does it sound like Silicon Valley or like the heartland?
- Banned phrase audit (above) is now a real check
